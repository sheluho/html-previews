Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about maintaining your home's HVAC system.

How often should I service my heating and cooling system?

It is generally recommended to service your HVAC system twice a year. You should have your air conditioner checked in the spring before the summer heat arrives, and your furnace inspected in the fall before winter begins. This preventative maintenance helps avoid unexpected breakdowns.

What are the signs I need a new air conditioner?

Key indicators that it might be time for a new AC installation include your unit being over 10-15 years old, requiring frequent or costly repairs, making unusual grinding or squealing noises, or struggling to cool your home evenly. A steady increase in your energy bills can also indicate failing efficiency.

Is a heat pump right for my home?

Heat pumps are highly efficient systems that handle both heating and cooling by moving heat rather than generating it. They are ideal for many climates and can significantly lower utility costs. A professional assessment can determine if a heat pump is the optimal choice for your specific property and local climate.

How long does AC installation usually take?

A standard AC installation typically takes one full day to complete. However, if your home requires new ductwork, electrical upgrades, or complex modifications to accommodate the new system, the process may take two to three days. Your technician will provide a clear timeline during the initial estimate.

Why is my furnace blowing cold air?

If your furnace is blowing cold air, check your thermostat settings first to ensure the fan is set to "Auto" rather than "On." Other common culprits include a dirty air filter blocking airflow, an extinguished pilot light, or a malfunctioning thermocouple. If basic troubleshooting fails, it's time to call an HVAC professional.
