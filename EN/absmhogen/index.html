Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in a standard house cleaning?

A standard professional house cleaning typically includes dusting all accessible surfaces, wiping down kitchen counters and appliances, sanitizing bathrooms (toilets, showers, sinks), emptying trash bins, vacuuming carpets, and mopping hard floors. It is designed to maintain a clean and hygienic baseline for your home.

How does deep cleaning differ from recurring cleaning?

While recurring cleaning focuses on regular upkeep, deep cleaning is a much more intensive process. It targets hard-to-reach areas, built-up grime, and tasks not performed weekly, such as hand-wiping baseboards, cleaning inside ovens or refrigerators, dusting ceiling fans, and detailed scrubbing of tile grout.

Do I need to provide cleaning supplies for the maid service?

Generally, no. Our partnered professional house cleaning teams arrive fully equipped with high-quality, effective cleaning supplies and equipment. However, if you have specific products you prefer them to use on certain surfaces (like a specific wood cleaner), you can request they use your provided supplies.

What happens during a move-out cleaning?

A move-out cleaning is designed to return an empty residence to a pristine state for the next occupant or landlord inspection. It includes everything in a deep clean, plus cleaning the insides of all empty cabinets, drawers, and closets, as well as thoroughly wiping down doors, frames, and interior windows where accessible.

Do I need to be home during the service?

It is entirely up to your preference. Many clients provide access instructions (like a keybox code or garage code) so the team can perform the house cleaning while they are at work or running errands. If you prefer to be home, the team will respectfully work around you to minimize disruption.
