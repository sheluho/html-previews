Frequently Asked Questions

Understanding your transition to residential solar power.

How long does a typical solar panel installation take?

The actual physical installation of residential solar panels typically takes one to three days, depending on the size of the system and your roof's complexity. However, the entire process—including site assessment, custom design, permitting, and final utility interconnections—can take several weeks to a few months.

Will solar panels work during cloudy days or winter?

Yes. While photovoltaic systems produce the most solar power under direct, bright sunlight, modern panels are highly efficient and will continue to generate electricity on cloudy or overcast days. In winter, as long as the panels are not completely covered by heavy snow, they will still capture ambient light and produce energy.

Do I need a battery backup system for my residential solar setup?

A battery backup is not strictly required to benefit from solar power, but it is highly recommended. Without a battery, standard grid-tied solar systems will shut off during a power outage for safety reasons. Adding a battery allows you to store excess energy and keep critical appliances running during grid failures.

What kind of maintenance do solar panels require?

Residential solar panels are remarkably low maintenance because they have no moving parts. Typically, they only require occasional cleaning to remove dust, leaves, or debris that might block sunlight. Rain often takes care of routine cleaning, but an annual professional inspection ensures the system operates at peak efficiency.

How does solar power impact my property value?

Numerous real estate studies indicate that homes equipped with owned (not leased) residential solar systems often sell for a premium compared to non-solar homes. Buyers increasingly value the prospect of lower utility bills and the modern, eco-friendly upgrade that a robust photovoltaic system represents.
