Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I service my heating and cooling system?

It is highly recommended to schedule preventative maintenance at least twice a year. Have your air conditioner inspected in the spring before the summer heat, and your furnace checked in the fall prior to the winter freeze.

What is the difference between a furnace and a heat pump?

A furnace generates heat by burning fuel (like natural gas) or using electric resistance, whereas a heat pump transfers heat from the outside air into your home. Heat pumps can also reverse the process to act as an air conditioner during warmer months.

What does a standard AC installation include?

A professional AC installation typically includes removing the old unit, installing the new condenser and evaporator coil, updating the refrigerant lines, ensuring proper electrical connections, and testing the entire HVAC system for optimal airflow and efficiency.

How can I improve my HVAC system's energy efficiency?

You can improve efficiency by regularly changing your air filters every 1-3 months, sealing leaky ductwork, upgrading to a smart thermostat, and ensuring your outdoor unit is free of debris. Regular professional maintenance is also crucial.

Are there signs that my air conditioner needs to be replaced?

Key indicators include an AC unit that is over 10-15 years old, frequent breakdown and repair bills, rising energy costs despite normal usage, uneven cooling throughout the house, and unusual grinding or squealing noises during operation.
