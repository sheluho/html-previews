Frequently Asked Questions

What defines a whole home remodeling project?
A whole home remodel typically involves updating 50% or more of your home's square footage. This often includes major structural changes, reconfiguring floor plans, fully updating kitchens and bathrooms, and unifying the design aesthetic across all living spaces rather than just renovating a single room.
Do I need to move out during a turnkey renovation?
For an extensive whole house project, it is highly recommended and often necessary to vacate the property. Major structural work, utility shut-offs, and daily construction activities make living in the home impractical. Vacating allows the general contractor to work more efficiently, often shortening the overall project timeline.
How long does a typical home addition take?
Timelines vary significantly based on the scope and complexity of the addition. The design, engineering, and permitting phases can take several months before construction even begins. Once ground is broken, active construction for a significant addition typically spans between four to eight months, depending on weather and material availability.
Are architectural permits handled for me?
Yes, when utilizing a professional design-build firm, the general contractor team manages the entire permitting process. They liaise with local municipal building departments to ensure all architectural plans, structural engineering, and zoning requirements are fully approved before construction commences.
What is the first step to starting my project?
The first step is an initial consultation to discuss your vision, budget, and the current state of your property. This allows the team to assess feasibility and outline the preliminary scope of work before moving into formal architectural design and material selection.
