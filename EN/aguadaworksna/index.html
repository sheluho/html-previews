Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about replacement windows and the installation process.

What makes a window truly energy efficient?

Energy efficient windows typically feature multiple panes of glass separated by insulating gas (like argon), specialized low-E (low emissivity) coatings that reflect heat, and high-quality frame materials that prevent thermal transfer. Together, these elements keep your home warmer in the winter and cooler in the summer.

Are vinyl windows better than traditional wood frames?

Vinyl windows are often preferred because they are highly durable, moisture-resistant, and virtually maintenance-free. Unlike wood, vinyl will not rot, peel, or require repainting. They also offer excellent insulation properties at a generally more accessible price point compared to premium wood frames.

What is the advantage of double hung windows?

Double hung windows feature two operable sashes that slide vertically. This allows you to open either the top or bottom half for optimal ventilation control. Additionally, most modern double hung models feature sashes that tilt inward, allowing you to easily clean the exterior glass from the safety and comfort of inside your home.

How long does professional window installation take?

The timeline depends on the number of windows and the complexity of the project. However, a professional crew can typically install a standard set of replacement windows in an average-sized home within one to two days, ensuring minimal disruption to your daily routine.

Do I have to replace all my windows at once?

No, you do not have to replace every window simultaneously. Many homeowners choose to replace windows in phases, often starting with the most problematic windows or focusing on a specific side of the house that faces extreme weather. This allows you to manage the investment over time.
