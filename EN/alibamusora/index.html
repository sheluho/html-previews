Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about professional wedding planning.

What is the difference between a full-service wedding planner and a day of coordinator?

A full-service wedding planner is involved from the very beginning, handling budget creation, vendor sourcing, event design, and all logistics. A day of coordinator (or event manager) typically steps in a few weeks before the wedding to finalize timelines and oversee the execution on the actual day.

How far in advance should we book wedding planning services?

For comprehensive full-service planning, it is recommended to book 12 to 18 months in advance, especially for popular seasons. If you are looking solely for a wedding coordinator for month-of management, 6 to 8 months prior is generally sufficient.

Do you handle destination wedding planning?

Yes, the professionals featured on this platform specialize in destination wedding logistics. This includes managing travel arrangements, negotiating with international vendors, and organizing multi-day itineraries for guests traveling from afar.

Will a planner save us money on our overall budget?

While a wedding planner is an investment, their industry expertise often prevents costly mistakes. They help allocate your budget efficiently, recommend vendors that fit your financial parameters, and ensure you receive the best value for your investment without sacrificing quality.

Do we still get to make the final decisions?

Absolutely. A wedding planner acts as your guide and advocate. They present curated options based on your vision and expertise, do all the heavy lifting regarding research and communication, but you always retain full control over all final decisions and selections.
