Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I save with residential solar panels?

Savings vary depending on your local electricity rates, the size of your system, and your energy consumption. However, most homeowners see a significant reduction in their monthly utility bills immediately after their solar installation is activated.

What happens during the solar installation process?

The process begins with a site assessment and custom system design. Once permits are secured, professional installers will mount the racking system, place the solar panels, wire the photovoltaic array to your electrical panel, and finally schedule an inspection to turn the system on.

Do photovoltaic panels work on cloudy days?

Yes, modern photovoltaic panels still generate electricity on cloudy or overcast days, though at a reduced capacity compared to direct sunlight. Ultraviolet light penetrates clouds, allowing your residential solar system to continue producing valuable solar power.

How long do solar panels typically last?

High-quality residential solar panels are incredibly durable and typically last 25 to 30 years. Even after this period, they will continue to produce solar power, though their energy output may gradually decrease over time.

Is my roof suitable for solar power?

Most roofs are suitable for solar panel installation. The ideal roof has good sun exposure, minimal shading from trees, and structural integrity. During the initial consultation, experts will evaluate your specific roof to design the most efficient photovoltaic layout.
