Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into the renovation process.

What exactly does whole home remodeling include?

Whole home remodeling typically involves a complete overhaul of your property's interior. This can include reconfiguring floor plans, moving walls, updating electrical and plumbing systems, and modernizing all major spaces like kitchens and bathrooms to create a unified aesthetic throughout the home.

How does a turnkey renovation work?

A turnkey renovation means the general contractor handles every single aspect of the project from start to finish. You approve the initial designs and materials, and the team manages the permits, demolition, construction, and final cleaning. You simply "turn the key" and walk into a completed, move-in-ready home.

Do you manage the permits for a home addition?

Yes. Expanding your home's footprint requires strict adherence to local zoning laws and building codes. The service provider will draft the necessary architectural plans, submit them to the city, and secure all required permits before any structural work begins.

What is the role of a general contractor in home renovation?

The general contractor acts as the project manager and primary point of contact. They are responsible for hiring and scheduling specialized trades (electricians, plumbers, framers), sourcing materials, ensuring quality control, and keeping the project on timeline and budget.

Can we remain in the house during a major home remodeling project?

For extensive whole home remodeling, it is often highly recommended to secure temporary housing. Major structural work, lack of functional plumbing or kitchen facilities, and construction dust make living on-site challenging. The contractor will advise you on the best course of action during the initial consultation based on the project's scope.
