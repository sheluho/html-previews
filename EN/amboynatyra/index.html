Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about preparing for your upcoming residential move.

How far in advance should I book a moving company?

It is highly recommended to book your residential moving company at least 4 to 6 weeks in advance, especially during peak moving season (May through September). Booking early ensures you secure your preferred dates and allows ample time to organize packing services if needed.

Do you recommend full packing services?

Yes, professional packing services are an excellent investment for reducing stress and ensuring the safety of your belongings. Movers use specialized materials and techniques to pack fragile items, electronics, and bulky furniture, significantly lowering the risk of damage during transit.

What is the difference between local movers and long distance movers?

Local movers typically handle relocations within a 50-100 mile radius or within the same state and usually charge by the hour. Long distance movers manage interstate or cross-country moves, and their pricing is generally based on the total weight of your belongings and the distance traveled.

How are moving costs calculated?

Costs depend on several factors: the distance of the move, the volume and weight of your items, the time of year, and any add-on services like packing or storage. The service providers we connect you with will provide a detailed, transparent estimate prior to your move.

Are there items that movers will not transport?

Yes. For safety and legal reasons, a moving company will generally not transport hazardous materials (like gasoline, fireworks, or harsh chemicals), perishable food, plants, or highly valuable personal items like jewelry, cash, or essential documents. You should plan to transport these items yourself.
