Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about securing your wedding film.

What makes a cinematic wedding video different from standard recording?
A cinematic wedding video goes beyond merely pressing record. It involves storytelling, high-end camera equipment, creative angles, expert audio design, and intensive color grading. The goal is to produce a wedding film that evokes deep emotion and feels visually comparable to a high-end movie.
Do we really need a wedding videographer if we already have a photographer?
Absolutely. While photography captures beautiful frozen moments, a wedding videographer captures motion and, crucially, audio. Hearing your vows, the quiver in a voice during toasts, and seeing the movement of your first dance are experiences that only event videography can preserve.
What exactly is included in event videography coverage?
Coverage typically depends on the package selected, but generally includes the morning preparations, the full ceremony, speeches, formal dances, and the ambient joy of the reception. The goal is to document the complete narrative arc of your celebration.
How long does it typically take to receive our final wedding film?
Editing a cinematic masterpiece requires significant time and attention to detail. Depending on the season and the complexity of your event, you can generally expect your final films to be delivered within 8 to 12 weeks following your wedding day.
Can we get the raw, unedited footage from the day?
Yes, raw footage or long-form documentary edits are often available as an add-on or included in higher-tier packages. This allows you to have a polished highlight reel to share, while keeping an unedited archive to preserve every single recorded second for yourselves.
