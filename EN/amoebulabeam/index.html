Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about exterior upgrades and insulation.

What are the primary benefits of spray foam insulation?

Spray foam provides a superior air barrier compared to traditional fiberglass. It expands into gaps and cracks, significantly reducing drafts, lowering energy consumption, and helping to maintain a consistent indoor temperature year-round.

How long does vinyl siding typically last?

High-quality vinyl siding is designed for longevity and can often last 20 to 40 years or more with minimal maintenance. It resists rot, pests, and severe weather, making it a highly durable choice for house siding.

How do I know if I need new attic insulation?

Signs you may need an upgrade include fluctuating indoor temperatures, unusually high heating or cooling bills, drafts in the upper levels of your home, or visible settling and degradation of your current insulation materials.

What is fiber cement siding and why choose it?

Fiber cement siding is a composite material made of cement, sand, and cellulose fibers. It offers the aesthetic appeal of wood but provides superior resistance to fire, rot, and insects, making it an excellent long-term investment for exterior protection.

Will upgrading my home insulation disrupt my daily routine?

Professional installation is generally swift and minimally invasive. While spray foam applications may require you to vacate the immediate area for a short curing period, most attic insulation and exterior siding projects are completed with minimal disruption to your household.
