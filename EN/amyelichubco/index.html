Frequently Asked Questions

Guidance on selecting the right planning support for your celebration.

What does a full-service wedding planner actually do?

A full-service wedding planner manages the entire scope of your event from inception to conclusion. This includes budget development, venue selection, aesthetic design, hiring and communicating with all vendors, and orchestrating the timeline. They act as your primary advocate and project manager throughout the entire engagement.

Do I really need a day of coordinator if my venue provides one?

Yes. A venue coordinator represents the venue's interests, ensuring the food is served and the facility rules are followed. An independent day of coordinator works exclusively for you. They manage your specific timeline, organize your personal decor, coordinate your external vendors (like photographers and florists), and handle any personal emergencies that arise.

How early should we start planning a destination wedding?

For a destination wedding, it is highly recommended to begin planning 12 to 18 months in advance. This extended timeline allows guests ample notice to arrange travel and accommodations. It also gives your planning team sufficient time to navigate international vendor contracts, secure prime locations, and organize multi-day itineraries.

When does a wedding coordinator typically step in?

While often referred to as "day of" coordination, a professional wedding coordinator usually begins their intensive work 4 to 6 weeks before the wedding date. During this handover period, they review all your contracts, build the master timeline, introduce themselves to your vendor team, and conduct the final venue walkthrough.
