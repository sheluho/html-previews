Frequently Asked Questions

What is a ceramic coating and how does it work?

A ceramic coating is a liquid polymer that is carefully applied by hand to the exterior of a vehicle. It chemically bonds with the factory paint, creating a permanent or semi-permanent layer of protection. This layer prevents dirt, grime, and stain marks from bonding to the paint, making the car much easier to clean while providing a deep, glossy finish.

How long does a full auto detailing session take?

The duration depends entirely on the condition of the vehicle and the specific package chosen. A standard interior detailing and exterior wash might take 3 to 4 hours. However, extensive paint correction followed by a ceramic coating application can take anywhere from a full day to multiple days to ensure the surface is perfectly prepped and cured.

Is paint protection film (PPF) better than ceramic coating?

They serve different primary purposes and are often used together. PPF is a thick physical barrier designed to absorb impacts from rocks and prevent physical scratches. Ceramic coating provides chemical resistance, UV protection, and intense gloss, but won't stop a rock chip. For maximum protection, we recommend applying PPF to high-impact areas and ceramic coating over the entire vehicle.

What does your interior detailing service include?

Our comprehensive interior detailing includes deep vacuuming, steam cleaning of air vents and hard surfaces, hot water extraction for carpets and cloth seats, and specialized cleaning and conditioning for leather. We also clean all interior glass and dress plastics to provide a fresh, non-greasy finish.

How should I maintain my car after a ceramic coating?

To maximize the lifespan of your ceramic coating, we recommend washing your vehicle every two weeks using a pH-neutral car shampoo and the two-bucket wash method. Avoid automatic drive-through car washes with harsh brushes, as they can degrade the coating and introduce swirl marks into the paint.
