Frequently Asked Questions

What are the primary benefits of fiber cement siding?

Fiber cement siding is incredibly durable, highly resistant to fire, and impervious to wood-boring insects. It elegantly mimics the look of natural wood but lasts significantly longer with a fraction of the maintenance required.

How do I know if my house needs new attic insulation?

Common signs include unusually high heating and cooling bills, drafts in the upper levels of your home, and uneven temperatures between rooms. If your current insulation is visibly settling or damaged by moisture, an upgrade is recommended.

Is spray foam better than traditional fiberglass?

Yes, in many applications. Spray foam expands upon application to fill cracks, crevices, and hard-to-reach areas, creating a superior air seal. This drastically limits air leakage compared to standard fiberglass batts.

How long does a typical vinyl siding installation take?

Most standard residential vinyl siding projects are completed within 1 to 2 weeks. The exact timeline depends on the size of the house, weather conditions, and the complexity of the architectural details being wrapped.

Do you recommend handling siding and home insulation at the same time?

Absolutely. Removing old house siding presents the perfect opportunity to assess and upgrade exterior wall insulation or install rigid foam board before the new siding goes up. Tackling both simultaneously maximizes your overall energy efficiency.
