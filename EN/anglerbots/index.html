Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

Generally, a tire rotation is recommended every 5,000 to 8,000 miles, or every time you have your oil changed. Regular rotation ensures that your tires wear evenly, which extends their useful life and maintains optimal handling and traction.

Why is wheel alignment necessary after a tire replacement?

When you invest in new tires, a wheel alignment ensures they sit perfectly parallel to each other and perpendicular to the road. Without proper alignment, your new tires can suffer from rapid, uneven wear, significantly reducing their lifespan and negatively impacting your vehicle's steering precision.

What exactly is included during tire installation?

Professional tire installation includes safely removing the old tires, mounting the new ones onto your rims, installing new valve stems (if applicable), and dynamically balancing the wheel assembly. This comprehensive process prevents vibrations at high speeds and ensures a safe, smooth ride.

How does the seasonal tire storage service work?

When you bring your vehicle in for a seasonal swap, your off-season tires are cleaned, documented, and transported to a secure, climate-controlled facility. They are stored away from direct sunlight, extreme temperatures, and harsh chemicals until you are ready to swap them back on next season.

How do I know when it is time for a complete tire replacement?

You should consider a tire replacement if your tread depth falls below 2/32 of an inch, if you notice uneven wear patterns, visible cracks, bulges on the sidewall, or if the tires are more than six years old, regardless of remaining tread.
