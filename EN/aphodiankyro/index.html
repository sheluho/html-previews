Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I save with residential solar panels?

Savings vary widely based on your geographic location, roof orientation, and current energy usage. However, most homeowners who switch to solar power see a significant reduction in their monthly electricity bills over time.

Will solar installation damage my roof?

No. Professional installers use specialized mounting equipment and flashing techniques designed specifically to protect your roof's integrity and prevent any leaks or structural damage during and after installation.

How long do photovoltaic panels last?

Modern photovoltaic panels are highly durable and engineered to withstand harsh weather conditions. They typically have an operational lifespan of 25 to 30 years with minimal maintenance required.

Does solar power work on cloudy days?

Yes, solar panels continue to generate electricity on cloudy or overcast days. While energy production is slightly reduced compared to direct sunlight, modern panels are efficient enough to harvest diffused ambient light.

What happens to my solar system during a power outage?

Standard grid-tied residential solar systems are designed to shut down during a utility outage for the safety of grid repair workers. To keep power running in your home during an outage, a dedicated battery backup system is required.
