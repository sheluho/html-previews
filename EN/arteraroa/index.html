Common Questions About Residential Solar

Is my flat roof suitable for solar panels?

Yes, flat roofs are excellent candidates for residential solar systems. Specialized mounting hardware is used to angle the panels perfectly toward the sun without requiring major structural changes to your roof membrane.

What is a solar canopy and why choose one?

A solar canopy elevates the photovoltaic array approximately nine feet above the roof surface. This is ideal for urban homeowners who want to generate solar power while maintaining full use of their roof deck for recreation or gardening.

How does a solar installation impact my energy bill?

By generating your own electricity, you significantly reduce the amount of power you need to draw from the public grid. Over time, these monthly savings can offset the initial investment of the solar panel installation.

Do I need battery backup with my system?

While not strictly mandatory, adding a battery backup allows you to store the solar power generated during the day. This provides crucial energy security during grid blackouts and allows you to use clean energy even after the sun goes down.

Who handles the permitting process?

The actual service provider, such as Brooklyn Solar Works, manages the entire bureaucratic process. This includes structural engineering reviews, securing city permits, and managing utility interconnection approvals prior to the solar installation.
