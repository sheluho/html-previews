Common Questions

Information to help you plan your exterior project.

Why should I consider spray foam over traditional fiberglass insulation?

Spray foam expands to fill every crack and crevice, creating an airtight seal that traditional fiberglass simply cannot match. This superior seal prevents air leakage, drastically improves energy efficiency, and helps maintain a consistent indoor temperature year-round. It is an ideal solution for attic insulation where heat loss is typically the highest.

How long does new house siding typically last?

The lifespan of your house siding depends heavily on the material you choose. High-quality vinyl siding can last upwards of 20 to 40 years with minimal maintenance. Fiber cement siding is even more durable, often lasting up to 50 years. Both options provide excellent protection against the elements when installed professionally.

Can you install siding and insulation at the same time?

Yes, and we highly recommend it. When removing old house siding, it presents the perfect opportunity to assess and upgrade your exterior wall insulation. Adding rigid foam board under new vinyl siding or utilizing spray foam in exposed cavities creates a comprehensive thermal break that significantly enhances your home's energy performance.

What are the aesthetic benefits of fiber cement siding?

Fiber cement siding offers a premium, architectural look that closely mimics the texture and grain of natural wood, without the susceptibility to rot or pests. It can be painted in virtually any custom color, allowing you to achieve a deeply personalized and upscale exterior appearance that significantly boosts curb appeal.

Will upgrading my attic insulation really lower my bills?

Absolutely. Heat naturally rises, meaning an under-insulated attic is the primary source of winter heat loss and summer heat gain. Upgrading to modern home insulation standards ensures your HVAC system doesn't have to work as hard, leading to noticeable reductions in your monthly heating and cooling costs.
