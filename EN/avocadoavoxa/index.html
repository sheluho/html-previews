Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about ceramic coating and auto detailing.

What is the difference between wax and a ceramic coating?

Traditional wax sits on top of the paint and typically wears off within a few months. A ceramic coating utilizes nanotechnology to form a semi-permanent chemical bond with the clear coat. It provides superior paint protection against UV rays, chemical etching, and dirt, often lasting several years while maintaining a high-gloss finish.

Why is paint correction necessary before ceramic coating?

Because a ceramic coating acts as a durable, transparent layer, it will lock in whatever state your paint is currently in. Paint correction safely removes swirl marks, holograms, and light scratches so that the surface is flawless before the protective coating is applied.

What does interior detailing include?

Comprehensive interior detailing involves deep cleaning every surface inside the cabin. This typically includes vacuuming, steam cleaning carpets and upholstery, conditioning leather seats to prevent cracking, and meticulously cleaning the dashboard, vents, and glass for a fresh, restored environment.

Can paint protection film (PPF) be applied over a ceramic coating?

The standard professional practice is the opposite: Paint Protection Film is applied directly to the corrected paint to ensure proper adhesion, and then a ceramic coating can be applied on top of the PPF. This combination offers the ultimate physical defense against rock chips along with enhanced hydrophobic properties.

How often should I schedule professional car detailing?

For daily driven vehicles, a thorough auto detailing service is recommended 2 to 3 times a year to maintain the clear coat and interior materials. If you have a ceramic coating installed, regular maintenance washes are still required to keep the hydrophobic properties performing optimally.
