Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about heating, cooling, and maintaining your HVAC systems.

How often should I schedule preventative HVAC maintenance?
It is highly recommended to schedule HVAC maintenance at least twice a year. You should have your air conditioner serviced in the spring before the summer heat arrives, and your furnace or heating system inspected in the fall before winter.
What are the common signs I need a new air conditioner?
Signs that it might be time for a new AC installation include frequent and costly repairs, unexplained increases in your monthly energy bills, uneven cooling throughout your home, and if your current unit is over 10 to 15 years old.
Is a heat pump better than a traditional furnace?
A heat pump is highly energy-efficient because it moves heat rather than generating it. It is an excellent choice for moderate climates as it provides both heating and cooling. However, in regions with extreme freezing temperatures, a traditional furnace may be necessary for optimal warmth.
How long does a typical AC installation take?
Most standard air conditioner replacements can be completed in a single day (typically 4 to 8 hours). However, if significant ductwork modifications are required or if it is a complex commercial installation, the process may take longer.
Why are some rooms in my house hotter or colder than others?
Uneven temperatures can be caused by several factors, including leaky or poorly designed ductwork, inadequate insulation, blocked vents, or an improperly sized HVAC unit. A professional technician can diagnose the specific issue and recommend solutions like zoning systems or duct repair.
