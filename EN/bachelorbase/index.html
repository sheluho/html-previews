Knowledge Base

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule HVAC maintenance? +

It is highly recommended to have your heating and cooling systems inspected at least twice a year. Schedule an air conditioner tune-up in the spring before the summer heat arrives, and a furnace inspection in the fall to prepare for winter weather.

What are the signs that I need a new AC installation? +

If your air conditioner is over 10-15 years old, requires frequent repairs, struggles to cool your home evenly, or is causing an unexpected spike in your energy bills, it might be time to consider a modern, energy-efficient AC installation.

What is a heat pump and how does it differ from a furnace? +

A heat pump transfers heat rather than generating it. In the winter, it pulls warmth from the outside air into your home, and in the summer, it reverses the process to act as an air conditioner. A furnace, on the other hand, generates heat by burning fuel or using electrical resistance.

Why is my heating system making strange noises? +

Banging, rattling, or squealing noises from your furnace or HVAC unit can indicate loose components, a failing motor, or issues with the blower assembly. It's best to have a qualified technician diagnose the sound before it leads to a total system failure.

Can upgrading my thermostat really save me money? +

Yes. Upgrading to a programmable or smart thermostat allows you to set precise schedules for your heating and cooling. This prevents your HVAC system from running unnecessarily when you are not home, significantly reducing your monthly energy costs.
