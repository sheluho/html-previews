Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a windshield replacement typically take? ▼

A standard windshield replacement usually takes about 60 to 90 minutes to complete. However, technicians require the vehicle to sit for an additional safe drive-away time—often an hour or more—to allow the urethane adhesive to cure properly before the vehicle is safely operable.

Can you perform chip repair on any size crack? ▼

No, chip repair is generally viable for damage smaller than a quarter and cracks less than a few inches long. If the damage is in the driver's direct line of sight, reaches the edge of the glass, or is too large, a full auto glass replacement is required to ensure safety.

Do I need an appointment for car window replacement? ▼

While emergency situations happen, scheduling an appointment is highly recommended. This ensures that the specific glass pane required for your vehicle's make and model is in stock and that a technician is available to perform the car window replacement promptly.

What is ADAS calibration and why is it necessary after replacement? ▼

Modern vehicles feature Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) like lane departure warnings and automatic braking, which rely on cameras mounted to the windshield. After a windshield replacement, these cameras must be precisely recalibrated to ensure the safety systems function correctly.

Is it safe to drive in the rain immediately after auto glass service? ▼

If you received a windshield repair, rain is generally not an issue immediately after. However, for a windshield replacement, it is crucial to adhere to the technician's advised drive-away time. The adhesives need time to set, and while light rain might be okay after curing, high-pressure car washes should be avoided for at least 24 hours.
