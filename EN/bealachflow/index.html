Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical residential solar installation take?

The physical installation of solar panels on your roof typically takes between one to three days, depending on the system size. However, the entire process, including site assessment, custom design, and securing local permits, usually spans a few weeks before the actual installation day.

Are my home's roof requirements suitable for solar power?

Most modern roofs can support residential solar panels. During the initial consultation, structural integrity, angle, and shading are thoroughly evaluated. If a roof requires reinforcement or repairs prior to integrating the photovoltaic system, the installation team will advise you accordingly.

What maintenance do photovoltaic systems require?

Solar panels are highly durable and require minimal maintenance. Generally, keeping them clear of heavy debris and occasionally washing off dust or pollen is sufficient to maintain optimal energy production. The smart monitoring system will alert you if performance drops unexpectedly.

Will I still have power during a grid outage?

Standard grid-tied residential solar systems automatically shut off during an outage for safety reasons. However, if your solar installation includes a dedicated solar battery backup system, your home can continue running on stored solar power even when the main grid goes down.
