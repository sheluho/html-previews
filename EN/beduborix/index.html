Frequently Asked Questions

What are the main benefits of upgrading my house siding?

Upgrading your house siding dramatically improves your home's curb appeal while providing crucial protection against the elements. New siding materials like fiber cement siding or high-grade vinyl siding offer superior moisture resistance, prevent structural damage, and can significantly increase the overall market value of your property.

How does spray foam differ from traditional home insulation?

Unlike traditional fiberglass batts, spray foam expands upon application to fill every crack and crevice. This creates a superior airtight seal that prevents drafts and moisture infiltration. It typically offers a higher R-value per inch, making it a highly efficient home insulation choice for both walls and attics.

Is fiber cement siding a better choice than vinyl siding?

Both materials have distinct advantages. Fiber cement siding is exceptionally durable, fire-resistant, and mimics the look of real wood beautifully, though it requires a higher initial investment. Vinyl siding is highly cost-effective, incredibly low maintenance, and quick to install. The best choice depends on your specific budget and aesthetic goals.

When should I consider replacing my attic insulation?

You should consider upgrading your attic insulation if you experience drafts, notice fluctuating indoor temperatures, or suffer from unusually high energy bills. Additionally, if your current insulation is visibly settling, damp, or older than 15 years, a professional assessment is highly recommended to restore energy efficiency.

How long does a typical siding installation project take?

The timeline varies based on the size of your home and the specific materials chosen. Generally, a professional crew can complete a standard house siding replacement within 1 to 2 weeks, assuming favorable weather conditions. A more precise timeline will be provided during your initial consultation.
