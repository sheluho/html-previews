Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about our wedding planning services.

What exactly does a full-service wedding planner do?

A full-service wedding planner manages the entire scope of your event from the moment you say "yes" to the final send-off. This includes budget development, scouting venues, assembling a team of trusted vendors, conceptualizing the design, and managing all contracts and timelines. We act as your project managers and creative directors, ensuring every element is cohesive and expertly executed.

Do I need a day of coordinator if my venue provides a manager?

Yes, absolutely. A venue coordinator represents the venue's interests—ensuring the food is served on time and the facilities are functioning. A dedicated wedding coordinator works exclusively for you. We manage your personal timeline, assist the bridal party, coordinate with all external vendors (like photographers and florists), and handle personal details that fall outside the venue's purview.

How far in advance should we secure your wedding planning services?

For full-service wedding planning, we recommend reaching out 12 to 18 months prior to your desired date, as premium venues and vendors book quickly. If you are exclusively seeking a day of coordinator, we suggest booking 6 to 9 months in advance to ensure our availability for your specific weekend.

Can you assist with a destination wedding outside the local area?

Yes. Destination wedding logistics are one of our specialties. We handle the complexities of planning an event from afar, including sourcing reliable international vendors, managing time-zone differences, and coordinating multi-day itineraries to ensure your guests have a remarkable travel and celebration experience.

What is the difference between a planner and a wedding coordinator?

A planner is involved from the very beginning, helping conceptualize, design, and book the entire event over many months. A wedding coordinator typically steps in a few weeks before the wedding. You do the planning and booking, and the coordinator takes over the logistics, timeline management, and execution to ensure your hard work pays off flawlessly on the actual day.
