Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about residential solar panel systems.

How long do solar panels typically last?

Most high-quality residential solar panels are designed to be incredibly durable and typically last 25 to 30 years or more. Even after their initial warranty period, they will continue to produce solar power, though at a slightly reduced efficiency rate.

Will solar installation damage my roof?

No. Professional solar installation is designed to protect your roof. Certified installers use specialized flashing and mounting hardware that seals penetrations completely, preventing leaks and actually shielding the covered portion of your roof from weather wear.

Do photovoltaic systems work on cloudy days?

Yes, solar panels still generate electricity on cloudy or overcast days, though their output will be lower than on days with direct, unobstructed sunlight. Modern photovoltaic cells are highly efficient at capturing diffused light to keep your home powered.

How much can I save with residential solar?

Savings vary depending on your current energy usage, the size of your system, and local utility rates. However, most homeowners see a significant reduction in their monthly electricity bills immediately after installation, often saving thousands of dollars over the lifespan of the system.

What maintenance does a solar power system require?

Solar panels require very little maintenance because they have no moving parts. Occasional cleaning to remove dust, leaves, or debris is usually sufficient to maintain optimal performance. The included monitoring system will alert you if production drops unexpectedly.
