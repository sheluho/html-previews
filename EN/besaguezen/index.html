Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a windshield replacement typically take?

Most standard auto glass replacements take between 1 to 2 hours to complete. Additionally, there is usually a safe drive-away time required for the adhesive to cure properly before you can operate the vehicle.

Can my auto glass be repaired instead of replaced?

It depends on the size and location of the damage. Generally, if the crack is smaller than a dollar bill or the chip is smaller than a quarter, a windshield repair is possible. However, damage directly in the driver's line of sight usually requires a full replacement.

Do the providers handle car window replacement for all makes and models?

Yes, the professionals we connect you with are equipped to handle auto glass needs for virtually all domestic and foreign vehicles, including specialized car window replacement for side and rear glass.

Is chip repair really effective?

Absolutely. Professional chip repair restores the structural integrity of the windshield and prevents the crack from spreading further. While a faint blemish may remain visible, the glass becomes structurally sound again.

How soon can I drive after a windshield repair?

After a simple chip repair, you can usually drive immediately. For a full windshield replacement, you must wait for the urethane adhesive to dry, which is typically 1 to 3 hours depending on weather conditions and the materials used.
