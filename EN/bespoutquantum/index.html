Frequently Asked Questions

Why is professional tire storage important?

Professional tire storage protects your valuable tires from environmental damage. Garages and sheds often have fluctuating temperatures, moisture, and UV light exposure that can degrade the rubber compounds over time. Climate-controlled storage ensures they are kept in optimal condition until your next tire installation.

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

As a general rule, a tire rotation should be performed every 5,000 to 8,000 miles, or whenever you get an oil change. Rotating your tires promotes even tread wear across all four wheels, which extends the life of your tires and improves your vehicle's handling and gas mileage.

When do I know it is time for a tire replacement?

You should consider a tire replacement when your tread depth falls below 2/32 of an inch, if you notice uneven wear patterns, deep cracks in the sidewall, or if the tires are older than six years regardless of tread depth. Driving on worn tires significantly increases the risk of accidents, especially in wet or snowy conditions.

Is wheel alignment necessary after getting new tires?

Yes, getting a wheel alignment is highly recommended after any new tire installation. If your wheels are misaligned, they will drag rather than roll freely, causing your brand new tires to wear out unevenly and prematurely. An alignment ensures your vehicle drives straight and protects your new investment.

How does seasonal tire installation work?

Seasonal tire installation involves removing your current set (e.g., summer tires) and mounting your alternate set (e.g., winter tires) onto your vehicle. If you utilize a tire storage service, the shop will simply retrieve your stored set, install them, and then clean and securely store the tires you just took off until the next season.
