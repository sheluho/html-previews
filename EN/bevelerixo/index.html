Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about residential solar panel installation.

How long does a residential solar installation take?

The physical installation of solar panels typically takes 1 to 3 days, depending on the size of the system and roof complexity. However, the entire process—including site assessment, custom design, permitting, and final utility inspections—can take a few weeks to a couple of months from start to finish.

Will solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes, photovoltaic systems still generate electricity on cloudy or overcast days, though at a reduced capacity compared to direct sunlight. Modern residential solar panels are highly efficient and can capture different spectrums of light to keep your home powered even in less-than-ideal weather.

What maintenance does a photovoltaic system require?

Residential solar panels are remarkably low maintenance because they have no moving parts. Generally, occasional cleaning to remove dust, leaves, or snow is sufficient to maintain optimal performance. Most systems come with monitoring software so you can easily track efficiency and spot any potential issues early.

Can I store excess solar power for later use?

Absolutely. By integrating a home battery storage system during your solar installation, you can capture excess solar power generated during peak sunlight hours. This stored energy can then be used overnight or serve as a backup power source during utility grid outages.

How do I know if my roof is suitable for solar panels?

Ideal roofs for residential solar have a clear, unshaded area facing south, east, or west, and are in good structural condition. During our initial consultation, our experts evaluate your roof's age, material, angle, and shading to determine the viability and optimal placement for your solar array.
