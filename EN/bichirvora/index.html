Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about commissioning bespoke furniture.

What is the process for ordering custom furniture?

The process begins with an initial consultation to discuss your vision, space requirements, and material preferences. Following this, the artisan will create a design proposal or sketch. Once approved, material sourcing begins, followed by the meticulous crafting of your handmade furniture piece.

How long does a bespoke furniture piece take to build?

Lead times vary significantly based on the complexity of the design, the current workload of the custom furniture maker, and material availability. Generally, you can expect a timeline of 8 to 16 weeks from the finalization of the design to delivery.

Can I choose the specific wood species for my custom dining table?

Absolutely. Selecting the wood species is a core part of the bespoke experience. Artisans work with a variety of domestic and exotic hardwoods, including walnut, cherry, white oak, and maple, allowing you to choose the grain pattern and color tone that best suits your interior.

Do you offer custom sofa designs for unusual room layouts?

Yes, this is a primary benefit of bespoke seating. Whether you have an awkward alcove, a uniquely shaped living room, or specific ergonomic requirements, a custom sofa is designed precisely to maximize your space and deliver tailored comfort.

How do I care for handmade solid wood furniture?

Solid wood furniture requires minimal but specific care. Maintain a consistent indoor humidity level to prevent warping. Wipe spills immediately with a damp cloth, avoid harsh chemical cleaners, and use coasters. Depending on the finish, occasional waxing or oiling may be recommended by the artisan.
