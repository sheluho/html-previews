Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into home insulation and siding solutions.

What are the benefits of spray foam over traditional home insulation?

Spray foam expands to fill gaps and cracks, creating a continuous air barrier that traditional fiberglass batts often miss. This superior seal prevents drafts, reduces moisture intrusion, and significantly enhances overall energy efficiency.

How often should house siding be replaced?

The lifespan depends on the material. High-quality vinyl siding can last 20-40 years, while fiber cement siding can last 50 years or more with proper maintenance. Signs you need replacement include severe fading, cracking, rotting, or rising indoor drafts.

Does attic insulation really lower energy bills?

Absolutely. Since heat naturally rises, a poorly insulated attic allows costly heated air to escape during winter. Proper attic insulation traps that air inside, meaning your HVAC system works less, leading to noticeable reductions in monthly energy bills.

What is the difference between vinyl siding and fiber cement siding?

Vinyl siding is cost-effective, lightweight, and requires minimal maintenance. Fiber cement siding is heavier, more expensive, and requires occasional painting, but it offers unmatched durability, fire resistance, and a more authentic wood-like appearance.

How do I know if my home needs new insulation?

Common indicators include fluctuating room temperatures, unusually high heating or cooling bills, cold walls or floors, drafts around windows, and the presence of ice dams on your roof during winter months.
