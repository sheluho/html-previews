Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about auto detailing and paint protection.

What exactly is a ceramic coating?

Ceramic coating is a liquid polymer applied to the exterior of a vehicle. It chemically bonds with the vehicle's factory paint, creating a semi-permanent layer of protection. This paint protection shields against UV rays, chemical stains, and makes washing much easier due to its hydrophobic properties.

How long does an interior detailing appointment take?

A standard interior detailing service typically takes between 2 to 4 hours. The exact time depends on the size of the vehicle and the condition of the interior. Deep stain removal or pet hair extraction may add additional time to ensure the highest quality result.

Is paint protection really necessary for a new car?

Yes, applying paint protection to a new car is highly recommended. Factory clear coats are thin, and environmental contaminants begin attacking the paint immediately. A protective layer applied early on preserves the flawless factory finish and maintains the vehicle's resale value.

How often should I schedule a full car detailing?

For most daily drivers, a full car detailing every 4 to 6 months is ideal to maintain a clean appearance and protect the surfaces. If your vehicle is garage-kept or driven infrequently, once a year may be sufficient.

Can auto detailing remove deep scratches?

Professional auto detailing includes paint correction, which can remove or significantly reduce light to moderate surface scratches and swirl marks. However, if a scratch is deep enough to catch your fingernail, it has likely penetrated the clear coat and may require touch-up paint or bodywork rather than just detailing.
