Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about our home remodeling process.

What is a design-build approach to home remodeling?

A design-build approach means that the entire home renovation process—from initial architectural drawings and interior design to the final construction—is handled by one unified team. This eliminates the disconnect often found when hiring a separate architect and general contractor, ensuring a smoother turnkey renovation.

How long does a typical home addition take?

The timeline for a home addition varies significantly based on scale and complexity. Generally, the design and permitting phase can take several months, followed by 3 to 6 months of active construction. A professional general contractor will provide a detailed schedule before any work begins.

Do I need to move out during a whole home remodeling project?

It depends on the scope of the project. For extensive whole home remodeling where major systems (plumbing, electrical, HVAC) are disrupted or multiple rooms are under construction simultaneously, moving out is often recommended for your safety and to expedite the construction timeline.

What makes a renovation "turnkey"?

A turnkey renovation implies that the general contractor manages every single aspect of the project. From securing necessary permits and ordering materials to managing subcontractors and final clean-up, you simply "turn the key" to enter your fully finished, ready-to-live-in space without having to coordinate the moving parts yourself.

Are you licensed and insured general contractors?

The professionals promoted through Biotite Pyra are fully licensed, bonded, and insured general contractors. Proper credentialing is essential for protecting your property and ensuring that all home remodeling work meets or exceeds local building codes.
