Frequently Asked Questions

What are the main benefits of spray foam over traditional home insulation?

Spray foam expands to fill gaps and cracks, creating an airtight seal that traditional fiberglass batts simply cannot achieve. This superior sealing prevents drafts, blocks moisture, and significantly increases the energy efficiency of your home.

How do I know if my house siding needs to be replaced?

Signs that you need new house siding include visible cracks, severe fading, warping, or peeling paint inside your home (which indicates moisture penetration). High cooling or heating bills can also suggest that your current exterior is failing to insulate properly.

What is the difference between vinyl siding and fiber cement siding?

Vinyl siding is lightweight, highly cost-effective, and requires virtually no maintenance, as it never needs painting. Fiber cement siding is heavier, more expensive, and incredibly durable, offering superior resistance to fire, rot, and severe weather while mimicking the look of real wood.

Will upgrading my attic insulation really lower my energy bills?

Yes. Because heat rises, a poorly insulated attic is the primary source of heat loss during the winter. Proper attic insulation traps the conditioned air inside your living space, meaning your HVAC system works less, which directly translates to lower utility bills.

How long does a typical siding or insulation project take?

Timeline varies based on the size of the home. A typical spray foam or attic insulation job can often be completed in 1 to 2 days. A full house siding replacement usually takes between 1 to 2 weeks, depending on weather conditions and material availability.
