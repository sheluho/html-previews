Common Questions

Insights into the home renovation process.

What does a turnkey renovation include?

A turnkey renovation means the entire project is handled from inception to completion by a dedicated general contractor. This includes architectural planning, securing permits, procuring materials, construction, plumbing, electrical work, and final interior design touches. The goal is to present you with a completed home that is ready to live in immediately.

Do I need a general contractor for a home addition?

Yes, home additions involve complex structural changes, foundation work, and integration with existing roofing and utility systems. A general contractor coordinates all specialized trades, ensures local building codes are met, and manages the timeline to ensure the new addition safely and seamlessly merges with your current home.

How long does whole home remodeling typically take?

The timeline for whole home remodeling varies greatly depending on the scope of the project, the size of the property, and the materials selected. On average, comprehensive renovations can take anywhere from three to eight months. Proper upfront planning is critical to avoiding delays during the construction phase.

Are permits required for whole home remodeling?

Absolutely. Any significant alterations that involve structural modifications, electrical rewiring, plumbing changes, or expanding the home's footprint require municipal permits. Your general contractor will handle the application and inspection process to ensure all work complies with local building and safety regulations.

What is the first step in the home renovation process?

The first step is a detailed consultation to establish your vision, budget, and functional needs. During this phase, you will discuss design ideas and structural requirements. Following the consultation, architectural planning and feasibility studies begin to lay the groundwork for your project.
