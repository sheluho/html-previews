Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about switching to residential solar.

How much can I save by installing solar panels?

Savings depend heavily on your current utility electricity rates and your roof's sun exposure. However, most homeowners see a significant reduction in their monthly energy bills immediately after their residential solar system is activated. Over the lifespan of the system, these savings can be substantial.

Will solar panels work on my specific roof type?

Solar panels can be installed on a wide variety of roofing materials, including asphalt shingles, metal, and tile. Before any commitment, we conduct a thorough structural and shading analysis to ensure your roof is highly suitable for a photovoltaic system.

How long does the solar installation process take?

The physical solar installation on your roof typically takes only 1 to 3 days. However, the comprehensive process—including system design, acquiring local permits, and securing utility interconnection approvals—can take several weeks prior to the actual installation day.

Do residential solar systems require maintenance?

Residential solar systems require very little active maintenance because there are no moving parts. Occasional cleaning to remove dust or debris, along with routine professional inspections, will ensure your panels continue generating optimal solar power year after year.

What happens to my solar power during a grid outage?

For safety reasons, standard grid-tied solar systems automatically shut down during a utility power outage to protect repair crews working on the lines. If maintaining power during an outage is a priority, we can discuss adding dedicated battery storage to your solar setup.
