Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between custom blinds and shades?

Blinds are typically made of hard materials like wood, faux wood, or aluminum and feature individual slats that can be tilted to control light. Shades are constructed from continuous rolls or folds of soft fabric, offering different levels of light filtering or blackout capabilities depending on the material chosen.

Are plantation shutters suitable for all window types?

Plantation shutters are incredibly versatile and can be custom-fitted to a wide variety of window shapes, including arches, angles, and French doors. Because they are built into the window frame, they require precise professional measurement to ensure flawless operation and aesthetic integration.

How do I choose between light filtering and blackout roller shades?

The choice depends entirely on the room's function. Blackout shades are ideal for bedrooms and home theaters where maximum darkness is desired for sleep or screen viewing. Light filtering shades are perfect for living rooms, kitchens, and offices, as they diffuse harsh sunlight while maintaining daytime privacy and a bright atmosphere.

Can custom drapes improve my home's energy efficiency?

Yes, high-quality custom drapes, especially those with thermal linings, act as an excellent insulating barrier. They help keep rooms warmer in the winter by preventing drafty air from entering and cooler in the summer by blocking intense solar heat gain, potentially lowering your energy costs.

Why should I invest in professional window treatments?

Off-the-shelf options rarely fit perfectly, leaving gaps that compromise privacy and aesthetics. Professional custom window treatments ensure exact measurements, superior material quality, flawless installation, and access to advanced features like motorization, resulting in a significantly more polished look and longer-lasting durability.
