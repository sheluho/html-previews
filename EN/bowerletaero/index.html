Common Inquiries

Information on our ceramic coating and detailing processes.

What exactly is ceramic coating?

Ceramic coating is a liquid polymer applied to the exterior of a vehicle. It chemically bonds with the factory paint, creating a layer of rigid paint protection. It does not wash away or break down quickly like traditional wax, providing a long-lasting, hydrophobic surface that repels water, dirt, and UV damage.

How often do I need auto detailing?

The frequency of auto detailing depends on how often you drive, where you park, and the conditions your vehicle is exposed to. Generally, a comprehensive interior detailing and exterior wash is recommended every 4 to 6 months to maintain optimal condition and prevent long-term wear.

Does paint protection prevent scratches?

While ceramic coating and sealants provide significant paint protection against micro-marring, chemical stains, and UV fading, they are not entirely scratch-proof against deep physical impacts. They act as a sacrificial layer that takes the brunt of minor abrasions, preserving the clear coat underneath.

What is included in interior detailing?

Our interior detailing process is exhaustive. It includes deep vacuuming, steam cleaning or extraction of upholstery and carpets to remove embedded dirt and stains, conditioning of all leather surfaces, and specialized cleaning of the dashboard, console, and glass to restore a factory-fresh environment.

How long does a ceramic coating application take?

A professional ceramic coating application typically requires a full day, sometimes longer, depending on the vehicle's initial condition. The bulk of the time is spent on paint correction and preparation—ensuring the surface is absolutely flawless before the coating is applied and allowed to cure.
