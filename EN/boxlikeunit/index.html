Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about maintaining your home's climate.

How often should I service my HVAC system?

It is generally recommended to have your heating and cooling system serviced twice a year. Schedule a cooling check-up in the spring before the summer heat hits, and a heating inspection in the fall to ensure your furnace is ready for winter.

What are the signs I need a new air conditioner?

Key indicators include your unit being over 10-15 years old, requiring frequent and costly repairs, producing weak airflow, or causing a sudden spike in your energy bills. If you notice these issues, a professional AC installation might be more cost-effective than continued repairs.

Is a heat pump a good choice for my home?

A heat pump is an excellent, highly efficient option for many homes because it handles both heating and cooling in a single unit. They are particularly effective in moderate climates and can significantly reduce your seasonal energy consumption compared to traditional separate systems.

Why is my furnace making strange noises?

Unusual sounds like banging, squealing, or rattling can indicate various issues, from a loose belt to a failing blower motor or a cracked heat exchanger. It is crucial to have strange noises investigated promptly by a professional to prevent complete system failure or safety hazards.

How long does a typical AC installation take?

A standard AC installation usually takes about one full day (4 to 8 hours) to complete. However, if the job requires significant modifications to your existing ductwork or electrical systems, the process may take longer. Your technician will provide a specific timeline during your estimate.
