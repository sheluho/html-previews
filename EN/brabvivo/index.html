Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into the renovation process.

What exactly does a turnkey kitchen remodel include?
A turnkey kitchen remodeling project covers every aspect of the transformation from start to finish. This includes design consultation, demolition, installation of custom cabinets and countertops, plumbing, electrical work, flooring, and final finishing. You simply hand over the old space and step into a fully functional, completed room.
How long does installing custom cabinets usually take?
Because custom cabinets are built to the exact specifications of your home, the fabrication process happens off-site and can take several weeks. However, the actual physical installation in your home is highly efficient, typically taking only a few days to ensure precision alignment and secure mounting.
Which materials are best for heavy-use countertops?
For heavy culinary use, quartz and dense granite are highly recommended. Quartz is engineered to be non-porous and highly resistant to stains and scratches, making it ideal for busy families. Natural granite offers unique veining and exceptional heat resistance, providing a durable and timeless surface.
Can I change my kitchen's footprint during a remodel?
Absolutely. Modifying the footprint is a common request during a comprehensive custom kitchen project. This might involve removing non-load-bearing walls to create an open concept, relocating plumbing for a new island sink, or shifting appliance zones to improve the overall cooking workflow.
How do I prepare my home for a major kitchen remodeling project?
Preparation involves clearing out all personal items, dishes, and small appliances from the existing space. It is highly recommended to set up a temporary makeshift kitchen in another room with a microwave and fridge. The renovation team will handle structural preparations, including laying down floor protection in high-traffic pathways.
