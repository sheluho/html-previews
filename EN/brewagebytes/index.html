Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule HVAC maintenance?

It is highly recommended to have your heating and cooling system serviced at least twice a year. Schedule an inspection for your air conditioner in the spring and your furnace or heat pump in the fall to ensure optimal performance.

What is the difference between a furnace and a heat pump?

A furnace burns fuel (like gas or oil) or uses electrical resistance to generate heat. A heat pump, however, transfers heat from the outside air into your home during winter, and reverses the process to act as an air conditioner during the summer.

How long does a typical AC installation take?

A standard AC installation usually takes a single day, typically between 4 to 8 hours. However, if ductwork modifications are required or if it is a complex dual heating and cooling setup, the process may take longer.

Why is my HVAC system making unusual noises?

Unusual sounds like banging, squealing, or grinding indicate mechanical issues within your HVAC unit. This could be a loose belt in the blower, failing motor bearings, or debris in the outdoor unit. It is best to turn off the system and request professional service immediately.

Can upgrading my air conditioner lower my energy bills?

Absolutely. Modern air conditioning units and heat pumps are designed with significantly higher SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) ratings than older models. Upgrading reduces the energy required to cool your home, leading to noticeable savings on monthly utilities.
