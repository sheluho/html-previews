Frequently Asked Questions

Understanding the process behind professional textile care.

How often should I schedule professional carpet cleaning?

For most households, it is recommended to have carpets professionally cleaned every 12 to 18 months. However, homes with indoor pets, high foot traffic, or allergy sufferers may benefit from more frequent cleanings, typically every 6 to 9 months to maintain optimal air quality and carpet condition.

Is steam cleaning safe for all upholstery types?

While steam cleaning is highly effective for many fabrics, it is not universally safe. Professional technicians will always inspect the manufacturer's care tags and perform a colorfastness test before beginning. Delicate materials like silk or certain natural blends may require specialized dry cleaning methods instead.

Can you guarantee complete stain removal?

Professionals utilize advanced chemistry and techniques that are highly effective at removing a wide variety of spots and spills. However, some substances may permanently alter the dye structure of the fibers. While technicians make every effort to restore the area, complete removal of deeply set or chemically altering stains cannot always be guaranteed.

Do I need to move my furniture before rug cleaning?

Most service providers will handle the moving of light furniture such as chairs and small tables as part of the service. For larger, heavier items like entertainment centers or large beds, technicians typically clean around the base. It is recommended to clear the floor of small items, toys, and fragile objects prior to their arrival.

How long does it take for carpets to dry after steam cleaning?

Drying times typically range from 4 to 8 hours, depending on the humidity levels, ambient temperature, and airflow in the home. Professionals use powerful extraction equipment to remove the majority of moisture. You can accelerate the drying process by keeping windows open or utilizing ceiling fans and HVAC systems.
