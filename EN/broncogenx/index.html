Frequently Asked Questions

What is considered a whole home remodeling project?

A whole home remodeling project involves significant structural and aesthetic changes to multiple rooms or the entire footprint of your house. It often includes reconfiguring floor plans, modernizing electrical and plumbing systems, and completely updating kitchens, bathrooms, and living areas under one cohesive design strategy.

What does a turnkey renovation mean?

A turnkey renovation means the general contractor manages every single aspect of the project from start to finish. You only deal with one point of contact. This includes design, obtaining necessary permits, sourcing materials, managing subcontractors, construction, and final cleanup, delivering a completed home ready for you to move back into.

Do you handle home additions as part of a larger renovation?

Yes, home additions are a common component of our whole home remodeling services. Whether you need to add a second story, expand the kitchen outward, or build a new master suite, we integrate the new construction seamlessly with your existing structure.

How long does a typical whole house renovation take?

Timelines vary greatly depending on the scope of work, structural changes required, and material availability. A comprehensive whole home project can take anywhere from 4 to 8 months or more. We provide a detailed timeline during the planning phase and keep you updated throughout the process.

Why should I hire a general contractor instead of managing trades myself?

Managing a large-scale renovation yourself requires immense time, industry knowledge, and a network of reliable tradespeople. A professional general contractor assumes the risk, coordinates scheduling to prevent delays, ensures all work meets building codes, and guarantees the quality of the final product, saving you significant stress.
