Common Questions About Residential Solar

How long does a typical solar installation take?

While the actual physical installation of solar panels on your roof usually takes only 1 to 3 days, the entire process—including site assessment, custom design, permitting, and final utility interconnections—can take several weeks. The exact timeline depends on local regulations and the complexity of your home's energy needs.

Are my roof shingles suitable for solar panels?

Most common roofing materials, including asphalt shingles, metal, and concrete tiles, are highly suitable for residential solar systems. During the initial consultation, technicians will evaluate the age, condition, and structural integrity of your roof to ensure it can safely support a photovoltaic array for decades.

What happens to my solar power during a grid outage?

Standard grid-tied solar panels will automatically shut off during a power outage for safety reasons, preventing electricity from back-feeding into the grid while utility workers are making repairs. If you wish to maintain power during outages, you will need to incorporate a solar battery storage system into your design.

Do solar panels require a lot of maintenance?

Residential solar systems are generally very low maintenance because they have no moving parts. Occasional cleaning to remove dust, debris, or snow is usually sufficient to keep them operating at peak efficiency. Most modern systems also come with monitoring software so you can easily track performance and spot any issues early.

How do I know if my home receives enough sunlight?

While south-facing roofs without shade are ideal, solar technology has advanced significantly, allowing panels to generate substantial power even in less-than-perfect conditions. A professional site assessment will analyze your roof's orientation, pitch, and shading from nearby trees or buildings to provide an accurate estimate of your potential solar power generation.
