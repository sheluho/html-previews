Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about the kitchen renovation process and materials.

What does a turnkey kitchen solution include?

A turnkey kitchen project means the service provider manages everything from start to finish. This typically includes demolition, design, plumbing, electrical, installing cabinets and countertops, flooring, and final painting, allowing you to step into a completed space.

How long does a full kitchen remodeling project take?

The timeline varies based on the scope of work. A minor update might take a few weeks, while a comprehensive structural remodel involving a custom kitchen design, new plumbing lines, and extensive custom cabinetry can take anywhere from 6 to 12 weeks to complete.

Can I just replace my countertops without changing the cabinets?

Yes, if your existing base cabinets are structurally sound and level, new countertops can be installed over them. This is a popular way to refresh a space without committing to a full renovation, though it limits your ability to change the overall layout.

What are the benefits of a custom kitchen over pre-fabricated options?

Custom designs allow you to maximize every inch of your specific floor plan. You have total control over the height, depth, and internal storage features of your cabinets, ensuring the final space perfectly accommodates your specific appliances and cooking habits.

What materials are best for high-traffic kitchen countertops?

For busy kitchens, engineered quartz and natural granite are highly recommended. They offer excellent durability, resistance to scratches and heat, and require minimal maintenance compared to more porous stones like marble, which can stain more easily.
