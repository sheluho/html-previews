Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about home insulation and siding upgrades.

What are the main benefits of spray foam insulation?

Spray foam provides a superior air barrier compared to traditional fiberglass. It expands to fill gaps and cracks, significantly reducing drafts, improving indoor air quality by blocking outside pollutants, and drastically lowering heating and cooling costs over time.

How do I know if I need new attic insulation?

Signs include fluctuating indoor temperatures, unusually high energy bills, drafts in the upper rooms, and the formation of ice dams on your roof during winter. A professional inspection can determine if your current levels meet modern energy standards.

What is the difference between vinyl siding and fiber cement siding?

Vinyl siding is highly cost-effective, low-maintenance, and comes in a wide variety of colors. Fiber cement siding is thicker, offers exceptional durability, is highly fire-resistant, and can closely mimic the texture of natural wood or masonry.

Will new house siding improve my home's energy efficiency?

Yes, particularly if it is paired with proper house wrap or insulated siding options. While the siding itself primarily protects against weather, the installation process often includes sealing gaps and adding a layer of rigid foam board beneath, which acts as supplemental home insulation.

How long does a typical installation take?

Timelines vary based on the scope of work. A standard attic insulation job might be completed in a single day, whereas a full house siding replacement or whole-home spray foam application could take several days to over a week. The contractor will provide a detailed schedule during the estimate.
