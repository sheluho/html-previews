Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical solar panel installation take?

The physical installation of residential solar panels typically takes between one to three days, depending on the size of your system and the complexity of your roof. However, the entire process, including permitting and inspections, may take several weeks.

Will my solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. While photovoltaic panels are most efficient in direct sunlight, they continue to generate solar power even on overcast or cloudy days. The energy produced will be less than on a sunny day, but your home will still capture valuable ambient light.

What maintenance is required for residential solar systems?

Solar panels require very little maintenance. Because there are no moving parts, the primary upkeep involves keeping the panels clean from heavy dust, leaves, or snow. A routine annual inspection by your service provider ensures everything functions optimally.

How does photovoltaic technology integrate with my current electrical panel?

During the solar installation, technicians connect the solar inverter directly to your home's existing electrical panel. This allows the converted solar power to safely distribute throughout your home, prioritizing solar energy before pulling any additional power from the traditional grid.

Can I store excess solar power for later use?

Absolutely. By integrating battery storage solutions into your residential solar setup, you can capture and store excess energy produced during peak sunlight hours. This stored power can then be utilized at night or during unexpected grid outages.
