Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about booking your wedding videographer and what to expect on your big day.

What is included in a standard wedding videography package?

Most standard packages feature a highlight cinematic wedding video (typically 5-8 minutes long), full-day coverage by at least one professional wedding videographer, and high-quality audio recording for vows and speeches. Exact deliverables vary by the specific provider you book.

How long does it take to receive our final cinematic wedding video?

Post-production is an intensive, detailed process to ensure top quality. You can typically expect your final wedding film to be delivered within 8 to 12 weeks following your event date, depending on the season and editing complexity.

Do the featured wedding videographers travel for destination weddings?

Yes, many of the professionals we promote gladly offer their wedding videography services for destination events. Travel fees and accommodations are usually handled as a custom quote directly with the provider.

What is the difference between a highlight reel and a full documentary wedding film?

A highlight reel is heavily stylized and edited to music, acting as a short, emotional summary of the day. A documentary film is much longer, showing extended cuts of the ceremony, toasts, and major events in real-time sequence for a thorough archive of your day.

Can we choose the music for our event videography edits?

Due to strict copyright laws, videographers must use properly licensed music for any wedding film that is distributed or posted online. However, they will work closely with you to understand your musical tastes and select licensed tracks that perfectly match the mood of your event.
