Frequently Asked Questions

What makes spray foam better than traditional insulation?

Spray foam insulation expands upon application to fill every gap and cavity, creating an airtight seal that traditional fiberglass batts simply cannot achieve. This superior air barrier drastically reduces heat loss, prevents moisture buildup, and significantly lowers utility costs.

How often should attic insulation be replaced?

While high-quality materials can last decades, attic insulation should be inspected every 10 to 15 years. Factors such as roof leaks, pest infestations, or settling can degrade its effectiveness over time. Upgrading to modern materials can quickly pay for itself in energy savings.

What are the main benefits of vinyl siding?

Vinyl siding is highly cost-effective, incredibly low maintenance, and available in a vast array of colors and styles. It never requires painting, resists rotting, and provides a durable protective shell for your home against severe weather conditions.

Is fiber cement siding worth the extra investment?

Yes. Fiber cement siding offers unparalleled durability. It is non-combustible, impervious to termites, and withstands harsh impacts and extreme temperatures without warping. It closely mimics the elegant look of natural wood but with a much longer lifespan and less upkeep.

Can I update my siding and insulation at the same time?

Absolutely. In fact, doing both simultaneously is highly recommended. When old house siding is removed, contractors have unobstructed access to the exterior walls, making it the perfect opportunity to upgrade to advanced home insulation before the new siding is installed.
