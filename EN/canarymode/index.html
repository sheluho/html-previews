Frequently Asked Questions

What is involved in whole home remodeling? ▼

Whole home remodeling involves a comprehensive overhaul of your existing living space. A dedicated general contractor oversees everything from structural changes, flooring, and electrical updates to custom kitchen and bath installations. It's a turnkey renovation approach designed to modernize the entire property simultaneously.

Do I need a general contractor for a home addition? ▼

Yes, a home addition is a major structural project that requires architectural planning, permits, foundational work, and framing. A licensed general contractor is essential to coordinate all trades, ensure compliance with local building codes, and seamlessly integrate the new addition with your existing home.

What does "turnkey renovation" mean? ▼

A turnkey renovation means the general contractor manages the entire project from the initial design phase through to the final construction and cleanup. When the project is completed, you simply "turn the key" and step into a fully finished, ready-to-use living space without having to hire separate designers or tradesmen.

How long does a home renovation typically take? ▼

Timelines vary significantly based on the scope of work. A focused kitchen or bathroom remodel may take a few weeks, while a large home addition or whole home remodeling project can take several months. Your contractor will provide a detailed timeline during the initial consultation.

Can I live in my house during the remodeling process? ▼

It depends on the extent of the project. For targeted renovations, contractors can often seal off work areas so you can remain at home. However, for extensive whole home remodeling or major structural additions, it is usually safer and more efficient to relocate temporarily until the heavy construction phases are complete.
