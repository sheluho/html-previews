Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about our whole home remodeling approach.

What exactly is a turnkey renovation?

A turnkey renovation means that a general contractor handles every single aspect of your project from start to finish. You only deal with one main point of contact for architectural design, permitting, demolition, construction, and final finishes, allowing you to simply "turn the key" and enjoy your newly completed space.

How long does a whole home remodel typically take?

Timelines vary greatly based on the scope of work, material availability, and the complexity of the design. However, a comprehensive whole home remodeling project generally takes anywhere from 4 to 8 months once construction begins. Detailed timelines are provided during the planning phase.

Do I need to hire my own architect for a home addition?

No, you do not. A design-build approach integrates architectural design and construction under one umbrella. Our promoted services provide in-house design professionals who collaborate directly with the builders to ensure your home addition is structurally sound and aesthetically cohesive.

Do I need to move out during the home renovation?

For a full-scale whole home remodel, moving out is often recommended for your safety and comfort, and it allows the general contractor to work more efficiently. If the project is localized, such as a specific home addition, it may be possible to remain in the home, though there will be noise and dust.

How do you handle budget overruns?

Transparent communication and meticulous upfront planning are key. By utilizing a design-build framework, the general contractor provides highly accurate estimates early on. Any potential changes to the scope are discussed and approved by you in writing before additional costs are incurred.
