Common Inquiries

Answers to the most frequent questions regarding home climate management.

What is the average lifespan of a residential HVAC system?

Generally, a well-maintained HVAC system can last between 15 to 20 years. Air conditioners and heat pumps tend to have a lifespan of 10 to 15 years, while a gas furnace can often operate efficiently for up to 20 years with proper annual maintenance.

How often should I replace my air conditioner filters?

For optimal air quality and system efficiency, it is recommended to check your filters monthly and replace them every 30 to 90 days. Homes with pets, high dust levels, or residents with allergies should replace their heating and cooling filters more frequently.

What are the main benefits of upgrading to a heat pump?

A heat pump provides both heating and cooling from a single unit, making it highly versatile. They are incredibly energy-efficient because they transfer heat rather than generating it from a fuel source, which can significantly lower your utility bills compared to traditional furnace and AC setups.

How do I know if my furnace requires an upgrade?

Signs that you may need a new furnace include rising energy bills, uneven heating throughout your home, strange noises (like rattling or banging), frequent repair requirements, and if the unit is over 15 years old.
