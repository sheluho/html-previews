Common Questions

What is the difference between full-service wedding planning and a day of coordinator? ▼

Full-service wedding planning means we are with you from day one—managing budgets, designing the aesthetic, and booking all vendors. A day of coordinator (often starting 4-6 weeks out) steps in to take over the logistics and timeline execution for a wedding you have already planned yourself.

Do you handle destination wedding logistics? ▼

Yes. A destination wedding requires extensive logistical support, including managing travel blocks, communicating with international vendors, and organizing multi-day guest itineraries. Our team handles these complex moving parts seamlessly.

When should we hire our wedding planner? ▼

We recommend booking your wedding planner 12 to 18 months in advance for full-service coordination, especially if you are seeking high-demand venues. If you are looking for a day of coordinator, reaching out 6 to 8 months prior ensures our availability.

Will our wedding coordinator be the main point of contact for vendors? ▼

Absolutely. Once your package begins—whether that is 12 months out or 6 weeks out—your dedicated wedding coordinator becomes the primary liaison for all vendor communication, ensuring everyone is aligned on the final timeline.

Can you create custom packages? ▼

Yes. We understand that no two celebrations are alike. We can build a bespoke wedding planning package that addresses your specific needs, bridging the gap between partial planning and full event design.
