Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our tire services and storage solutions.

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?
It is generally recommended to schedule a tire rotation every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, or every six months. Regular rotation ensures even tread wear, which significantly extends the life of your tires and maintains optimal handling.
How does your seasonal tire storage work?
When you come in to swap your seasonal tires, we take your off-season set, clean them, and place them in our secure, climate-controlled tire storage facility. This prevents rubber degradation from extreme temperatures and frees up valuable space in your home or garage.
How do I know when it's time for a tire replacement?
You should consider a tire replacement if your tread depth falls below 2/32 of an inch, if you notice uneven wear patterns, or if the tire sidewalls show visible cracks or bulging. Our technicians can perform a quick inspection to determine if a replacement is necessary for your safety.
What is included in your tire installation service?
Our comprehensive tire installation includes removing the old tires, mounting the new tires onto your wheels, precision computerized balancing, installing new valve stems (where applicable), and properly torquing the lug nuts to manufacturer specifications.
Why is wheel alignment important after getting new tires?
A proper wheel alignment ensures that your new tires meet the road at the correct angle. Without it, your new tires could experience rapid, uneven wear, reducing their lifespan and potentially compromising your vehicle's steering and handling capabilities.
