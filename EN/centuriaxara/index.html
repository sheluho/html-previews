Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is a ceramic coating?
Ceramic coating is a liquid polymer applied by hand to the exterior of a vehicle. It chemically bonds with the factory paint, creating a rigid layer of protection. It acts as a premium alternative to traditional wax, offering superior durability, hydrophobic properties, and resistance to chemical etching and UV damage.
How long does an auto detailing session typically take?
The duration depends heavily on the services requested and the condition of the vehicle. A standard interior detailing and exterior wash might take 3 to 5 hours. However, multi-stage paint correction followed by a ceramic coating application can take anywhere from 1 to 3 days to ensure the coating cures properly in a controlled environment.
Is paint protection film (PPF) different from ceramic coating?
Yes. Paint Protection Film (PPF) is a physical, transparent urethane film applied to the vehicle's panels to prevent rock chips and deep scratches. Ceramic coating is a liquid chemical that hardens over the paint to protect against UV rays, chemicals, and minor abrasions while enhancing gloss. Many clients opt for PPF on high-impact areas and ceramic coating over the entire vehicle.
How often should I invest in comprehensive interior detailing?
For a vehicle driven daily, we recommend a comprehensive interior detailing at least twice a year. This prevents the buildup of ingrained dirt, conditions leather to prevent cracking, and maintains a hygienic environment. Lighter maintenance cleans can be performed monthly to keep the cabin looking fresh between deep details.
Will paint correction remove deep scratches?
Paint correction is highly effective at removing swirl marks, holograms, and light to moderate scratches that reside within the clear coat layer. However, if a scratch has penetrated through the clear coat and into the base color or primer (usually detectable if you can catch your fingernail in it), paint correction alone cannot remove it; touch-up paint or respraying would be required.
