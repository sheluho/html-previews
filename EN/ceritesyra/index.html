Common Inquiries

What is included in a turnkey renovation?

A turnkey renovation means the general contractor handles every single aspect of the project. This includes initial architectural design, securing necessary building permits, sourcing all materials, managing subcontractors, and overseeing the final construction to ensure the space is ready for you to live in immediately upon completion.

How long does a whole home remodeling project take?

Timelines vary significantly based on the scope and complexity of the design. Generally, the design and planning phase can take 2-4 months, while the actual construction phase for a whole home remodel may range from 6 to 12 months. An accurate timeline is established after the initial design consultation.

Do I need a general contractor for a home addition?

Yes, a home addition is a major structural change that involves foundation work, framing, roofing, and integrating new electrical and plumbing systems with the existing house. A licensed general contractor ensures that all work meets local building codes and structural engineering standards.

Will I need to move out during the renovation?

This depends entirely on the scale of the project. For a targeted kitchen remodel or a small home addition, homeowners can often remain in the house. However, for extensive whole home remodeling where essential utilities (water, electricity) are interrupted for extended periods, temporarily relocating is highly recommended for safety and comfort.

How are building permits handled?

As part of our comprehensive service approach, navigating local zoning laws and securing necessary building permits is managed entirely by the project team. We ensure all architectural plans are submitted to the appropriate municipal authorities and approved before any construction begins.
