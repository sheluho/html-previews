Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about residential solar panel installation.

How much space do I need for solar panels?

The space required depends on your current energy consumption and the efficiency of the photovoltaic panels chosen. A typical residential solar installation requires between 300 and 400 square feet of unobstructed, sun-facing roof space.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes, solar power systems continue to generate electricity on cloudy or overcast days, though at a reduced capacity compared to direct sunlight. High-efficiency photovoltaic cells are designed to capture diffused light effectively.

How long does a residential solar installation take?

While the physical installation of the solar panels on your roof typically takes just 1 to 3 days, the entire process—including custom design, permitting, and grid interconnection—can take a few weeks to complete.

What happens during a power outage?

Standard grid-tied solar systems automatically shut off during blackouts for safety reasons. However, if your residential solar setup includes a battery storage solution, your home can seamlessly draw stored solar power to keep essential appliances running.

Will installing solar panels damage my roof?

No. When installed by certified professionals, solar panels actually protect the portion of the roof they cover from weather and UV wear. Installers use specialized flashing and mounting hardware to ensure your roof remains entirely watertight.
