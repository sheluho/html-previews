Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a standard windshield replacement take?

A standard windshield replacement typically takes between one to two hours to complete. However, you must also factor in the safe drive-away time, which is the time required for the urethane adhesive to cure sufficiently to meet safety standards before the vehicle can be driven.

Can every type of windshield chip be repaired?

Not all damage can be repaired. Generally, chip repair is viable if the damage is smaller than a quarter and is not located directly in the driver's primary line of sight. If the damage exceeds these parameters, a full windshield replacement is usually recommended for safety reasons.

Is auto glass repair covered by my auto insurance?

In many cases, yes. Comprehensive insurance policies often cover auto glass repair and car window replacement. Depending on your specific policy and local regulations, the deductible may even be waived for a simple chip repair. It is best to consult with your insurance provider to confirm your coverage details.

What happens to the broken glass inside my car?

During a side or rear car window replacement, professional technicians will thoroughly vacuum and clean the interior of your vehicle, including inside the door panels if necessary, to ensure all shattered glass fragments are safely removed before installing the new window.

Do I need an appointment for auto glass services?

While walk-ins are sometimes accommodated depending on the shop's schedule, scheduling an appointment is highly recommended. This ensures that the correct piece of auto glass is in stock for your specific vehicle and that a technician is available to perform your windshield replacement without unnecessary delays.
