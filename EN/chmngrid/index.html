Frequently Asked Questions

What are the main benefits of spray foam insulation?

Spray foam provides a superior air barrier compared to traditional fiberglass batts. It expands to fill microscopic gaps and cracks in your walls and attic, significantly reducing drafts and preventing moisture accumulation. This leads to a more stable indoor climate and notable reductions in monthly heating and cooling expenses.

How do I know if I need new house siding?

Key indicators that it is time to replace your siding include visible warping, rotting wood, frequent need for repainting, or noticeably higher energy bills due to drafts. If you observe structural damage or moisture penetration inside your home, we highly recommend scheduling an exterior evaluation immediately.

Is fiber cement siding better than vinyl siding?

Both materials offer excellent durability, but they serve different needs. Fiber cement siding is exceptionally robust, fire-resistant, and can mimic the texture of natural wood beautifully, though it requires a higher initial investment. Vinyl siding is incredibly low-maintenance, cost-effective, and does not require painting. The right choice depends on your budget and architectural preferences.

How often should attic insulation be replaced?

Standard attic insulation can degrade over 15 to 20 years due to settling, moisture, or pest interference. However, modern materials like closed-cell spray foam can last the lifetime of the house without losing their thermal resistance. An inspection can determine if your current insulation is underperforming.
