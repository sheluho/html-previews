Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about switching to solar power.

Are solar panels suitable for my residential roof?

Most residential roofs can accommodate solar panels. The ideal setup depends on your roof's orientation, shading from nearby trees, and structural integrity. A professional assessment will determine the best photovoltaic layout for your home.

How long does a complete solar installation take?

The physical solar installation usually takes 1 to 3 days. However, the entire process—including site assessment, custom design, permitting, and final utility inspections—can take a few weeks to complete properly.

Will residential solar eliminate my electricity bill entirely?

While solar power significantly reduces your monthly utility costs, eliminating the bill entirely depends on your energy consumption, system size, and local net metering policies. Most homeowners experience a drastic reduction in energy expenses.

What maintenance do photovoltaic systems require?

Residential solar systems require minimal maintenance. Because there are no moving parts, simple periodic cleaning to remove dust or debris and an occasional professional inspection are usually enough to keep them running efficiently.

What happens to my solar power during an outage?

Standard grid-tied solar panels will automatically shut off during a blackout for safety reasons. To keep your home powered during outages, you can integrate a solar battery backup system with your installation.
