Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our ceramic coating and detailing services.

What exactly is a ceramic coating?

A ceramic coating is a liquid polymer applied to your vehicle's exterior. It chemically bonds with the factory paint, creating a layer of rigid paint protection. It doesn't wash away or break down quickly like traditional wax, offering lasting defense against dirt, UV rays, and minor scratches.

How long does interior detailing take?

Thorough interior detailing generally takes between 2 to 4 hours, depending on the size and condition of the vehicle. This includes vacuuming, deep-cleaning upholstery, conditioning leather, and safely sanitizing all dashboard and console surfaces.

Is professional paint protection worth the investment?

Absolutely. High-quality paint protection reduces the frequency of car detailing needed, preserves the vehicle's resale value, and prevents environmental contaminants like bird droppings or tree sap from permanently etching into your clear coat.

How often should I schedule car detailing?

For a daily driver without a ceramic coating, a full car detailing is recommended every 4 to 6 months. If your vehicle has a ceramic coating applied, simple maintenance washes are often enough, with a thorough detail needed only once a year to maintain optimal condition.

What is the difference between wax and ceramic coating?

Traditional wax sits on top of the paint and provides a warm glow, but typically wears off in 1 to 3 months. Ceramic coating forms a semi-permanent chemical bond with the clear coat, offering a significantly harder layer of paint protection that can last for years rather than months.
