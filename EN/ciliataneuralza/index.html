Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about embarking on a home renovation journey.

What exactly is a turnkey renovation?
A turnkey renovation implies that the general contractor handles the entire scope of the project from concept to completion. You simply "turn the key" to enter your newly finished space, without having to coordinate multiple subcontractors, designers, or permit offices yourself.
How long does whole home remodeling usually take?
The timeline for whole home remodeling varies greatly depending on the scope of work, material availability, and square footage. A comprehensive interior overhaul can take anywhere from 3 to 8 months. A detailed schedule is always provided by the design-build team before construction begins.
Do I need to hire a separate architect for a home addition?
No, you generally do not. By partnering with a design-build general contractor, architectural drafting, interior design, and construction management are all housed under one roof. This ensures your home addition is structurally sound and aesthetically unified.
Why should I hire a general contractor instead of managing it myself?
A professional general contractor brings an established network of reliable tradespeople, deep knowledge of local building codes, and the ability to foresee and mitigate complex structural issues. Managing a home renovation yourself often leads to costly delays and compliance problems.
Can I live in my house during the home renovation?
This depends heavily on the extent of the project. For a targeted home addition or kitchen remodel, careful staging often allows you to remain in the home. However, for a massive whole home remodeling project where utilities and major living areas are disrupted, temporarily relocating is usually recommended for your comfort and safety.
