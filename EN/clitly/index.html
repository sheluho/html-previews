Frequently Asked Questions

Answers about our siding and insulation solutions.

What are the benefits of spray foam over traditional insulation?

Spray foam expands to completely fill gaps and crevices, creating an airtight seal that traditional fiberglass batts cannot match. This superior home insulation prevents air leaks, significantly lowers energy bills, and helps maintain a consistent indoor temperature while also adding structural integrity to your walls.

How long does vinyl house siding typically last?

High-quality vinyl siding can last anywhere from 20 to 40 years depending on the specific product grade and weather conditions. It is engineered to resist fading, moisture damage, and insect activity, making it a very low-maintenance exterior solution for modern homes.

Is fiber cement siding worth the extra investment?

Yes, fiber cement siding is widely considered a premium upgrade. It offers the aesthetic appeal of natural wood but is non-combustible, rot-resistant, and impervious to termites. While the initial material and installation costs may be higher than vinyl, its exceptional longevity and durability provide excellent long-term value.

Why is attic insulation so important for energy efficiency?

Because heat naturally rises, a poorly insulated attic allows heated air to easily escape your home during winter, forcing your heating system to work overtime. Proper attic insulation acts as a thermal barrier, trapping heat inside during cold months and keeping hot air out during the summer, drastically improving overall efficiency.

Can you install new siding over my existing exterior?

In some cases, new house siding can be installed over existing siding if the underlying structure is sound and flat. However, it is generally recommended to remove the old siding. This allows contractors to inspect the wall cavity for moisture damage, address any structural issues, and properly install a weather-resistant barrier and new home insulation if needed.
