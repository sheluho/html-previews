Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about transitioning to residential solar.

How much can I save with residential solar?

Savings depend on your current energy usage, local utility rates, and the size of the solar panels installed. Most homeowners see a significant reduction in their monthly bills immediately after their residential solar system is activated, hedging against future utility rate hikes.

Is my roof suitable for solar panels?

Ideal roofs face south, west, or east and have minimal shading from trees or nearby buildings. However, modern photovoltaic technology is highly efficient. A professional assessment will determine the best configuration for your specific roof layout.

How long does the solar installation process take?

The physical solar installation typically takes only 1 to 3 days. However, the entire process—including custom design, permitting, and utility inspections—usually spans several weeks to ensure everything meets local regulatory standards perfectly.

What happens to my solar power during an outage?

Standard grid-tied solar panels will automatically shut off during a blackout for safety reasons, protecting utility workers. If maintaining solar power during an outage is a priority, you can opt to include a solar battery backup system with your installation.

Are photovoltaic systems difficult to maintain?

Not at all. Residential solar setups require very little maintenance. Because there are no moving parts, simple occasional cleaning to remove dust or debris is usually all that is needed to keep your photovoltaic panels operating at peak efficiency.
