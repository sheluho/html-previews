Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about our insulation and siding solutions.

How long does spray foam insulation take to install?

Most residential spray foam installations can be completed in one to two days, depending on the size of the area being treated. Proper ventilation and curing time are required before re-entering the space, which the installation team will manage.

What is the difference between vinyl siding and fiber cement siding?

Vinyl siding is highly cost-effective, low-maintenance, and resistant to moisture. Fiber cement siding is thicker, incredibly durable, fire-resistant, and can closely mimic the look of natural wood grain. Both are excellent choices depending on your budget and aesthetic goals.

Is a complete attic insulation upgrade really that important?

Yes. Because heat naturally rises, an under-insulated attic is the primary source of energy loss in most homes. Upgrading your attic insulation creates a thermal barrier that keeps conditioned air inside, lowering your heating and cooling bills significantly.

How do I know if my current home insulation needs replacing?

Signs that you need new home insulation include drafts, uneven temperatures between rooms, unusually high energy bills, indoor drafts, and ice dams on your roof during winter. A professional assessment can determine the exact state of your thermal envelope.

Do you handle the removal of old house siding?

Yes, comprehensive house siding projects include the complete tear-down and safe disposal of your existing exterior materials. This process also allows contractors to inspect the underlying structure and add protective wraps or rigid home insulation before installing the new siding.
