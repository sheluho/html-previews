Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our recommended cleaning methods.

How long does carpet cleaning take to dry?

Unlike traditional steam cleaning that can leave carpets soaking wet for days, modern extraction methods utilize a fraction of the water. Typically, your carpets will be completely dry and ready for normal use within 1 to 2 hours, minimizing disruption to your household.

Is upholstery cleaning safe for delicate fabrics?

Yes. Professional technicians assess the specific material of your furniture before beginning any work. The upholstery cleaning process is carefully calibrated to effectively lift dirt and oils without causing color bleeding, shrinkage, or damage to delicate weaves.

Can you guarantee complete stain removal?

While no professional can guarantee the removal of every single mark—as some substances permanently dye the fibers—expert stain removal techniques have an incredibly high success rate. Specialized solutions are used to break down tough spots that standard domestic products cannot touch.

Do you offer steam cleaning as an option?

Yes, while low-moisture extraction is often preferred for rapid drying, traditional high-heat steam cleaning is also available and highly effective for certain intensive sanitization needs, particularly on highly soiled industrial carpets or specific hard surfaces.

How often should I schedule professional rug cleaning?

For standard household traffic, it is generally recommended to have professional rug cleaning performed every 12 to 18 months. However, if you have indoor pets, suffer from allergies, or have high-traffic entryways, scheduling a cleaning every 6 to 9 months is advisable to maintain optimal air quality and fiber integrity.
