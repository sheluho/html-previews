Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule HVAC maintenance?

It is generally recommended to have your heating and cooling systems inspected twice a year. Schedule an AC check-up in the spring and a furnace or heat pump inspection in the fall to ensure optimal performance year-round.

What are the signs that I need a new air conditioner?

If your current air conditioner is over 10-15 years old, requires frequent repairs, struggles to cool your home evenly, or causes unexplained spikes in your energy bills, it may be time to consider a professional AC installation for a more efficient unit.

Do you provide emergency heating repair services?

Through our promotional network, you can connect with HVAC professionals who offer urgent repair services for sudden furnace or heat pump failures, ensuring your home doesn't stay freezing during the harsh winter months.

How can I improve my indoor air quality?

Regularly changing your air filters is the best first step. Beyond that, routine ductwork inspections, utilizing whole-home humidifiers or dehumidifiers, and investing in advanced air purifiers integrated directly into your HVAC system can vastly improve indoor air quality.

Is a heat pump better than a traditional furnace?

It depends on your climate and existing infrastructure. A heat pump offers both heating and cooling from one energy-efficient unit, making it ideal for moderate climates. However, in regions with extreme freezing temperatures, a traditional gas furnace may provide more robust, rapid heating power.
