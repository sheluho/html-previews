Common Inquiries

What is the difference between a wedding planner and a day of coordinator?
A full-service wedding planner is involved from the very beginning, assisting with budget, design, and booking every vendor. A day of coordinator steps in typically a month before the wedding to review your plans, create a timeline, and manage the execution on the actual day so you don't have to work on your wedding day.

How far in advance should we book our wedding planning services?
For full-service wedding planning, we recommend reaching out 12 to 18 months prior to your desired date to secure the best venues and vendors. If you are seeking a wedding coordinator for day-of management, booking 6 to 9 months in advance is ideal to ensure availability.

Do you handle destination wedding planning?
Yes, we have extensive experience in destination wedding planning. We manage the complex logistics, from coordinating with international vendors to assisting with travel timelines, ensuring your faraway celebration is as seamless as a local one.

Can you work within our specific budget?
Absolutely. A key part of our full-service offering involves establishing and respecting a realistic budget. We utilize our industry connections and expertise to maximize your investment and prioritize the elements that matter most to you.

Will you be present for the entire wedding day?
Yes. Whether you hire us for full planning or as a day of coordinator, our team is the first to arrive and the last to leave. We oversee setup, manage the event flow, and ensure breakdown is handled properly, providing continuous support.
