Frequently Asked Questions

How much space is required for a custom walk-in closet?

A functional walk-in closet typically requires a minimum of 4 by 4 feet to allow for basic shelving and standing room. However, for a luxurious experience featuring an island, extensive hanging areas, and specialized shoe displays, a space of 7 by 10 feet or larger is recommended. The design can be adapted to maximize whatever footprint you have available.

Can you upgrade an existing reach-in closet?

Absolutely. Upgrading a reach-in closet is one of the most effective ways to increase your home's storage capacity. By removing single wire racks and installing a customized closet organizer with tiered hanging rods, shelving, and drawers, we can often double the usable space without changing the physical dimensions of the walls.

What is the difference between a custom closet and a freestanding wardrobe?

Custom closets are built-in architectural features tailored exactly to the dimensions of a specific room, utilizing the walls and floor for support. A wardrobe is typically a standalone piece of furniture that can be moved. Both can be completely customized internally with specialized organizers, but built-in custom closets generally offer a more seamless, integrated aesthetic for modern homes.

What materials are used in your closet organizer systems?

The systems discussed feature high-grade, thermally fused laminate materials that resist scratching and warping, far superior to basic wire shelving. For premium installations, options include solid wood veneers, custom glass doors, and heavy-duty metal hardware designed to withstand daily use while maintaining a refined, elegant appearance for years.

How does the design consultation process work?

The process begins with an assessment of your current space and an inventory of your wardrobe to understand your specific storage needs. Designers then create a 3D model of your proposed custom closets, allowing you to visualize the layout, select finishes, and adjust the configuration of the closet organizer components before any manufacturing begins.
