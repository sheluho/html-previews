Frequently Asked Questions

What makes energy efficient windows better than standard ones?

Energy efficient windows utilize advanced technology like low-emissivity (Low-E) coatings, argon gas fills between the panes, and highly insulated vinyl frames. These features work together to significantly reduce heat transfer, keeping your home warmer in the winter and cooler in the summer while lowering your overall energy consumption.

How long does the window installation process usually take?

The duration of a professional window installation depends on the number of windows being replaced and the condition of the existing frames. Typically, a standard residential installation of replacement windows can be completed in one to two days. Your contractor will provide a specific timeline during your initial consultation.

Are vinyl windows better than traditional wood windows?

Vinyl windows offer several distinct advantages over traditional wood frames. They are virtually maintenance-free, meaning they will never need to be scraped, painted, or stained. Furthermore, modern vinyl is highly resistant to moisture, meaning it won't rot or warp over time, providing excellent insulation and long-lasting durability.

What is the difference between single and double hung windows?

In a single hung window, only the bottom sash moves vertically while the top sash remains fixed in place. In double hung windows, both the top and bottom sashes are operable and can move up and down. This allows for better ventilation options and makes cleaning the outside of the glass much easier from inside your home.

Will new replacement windows really lower my utility bills?

Yes. Old, drafty single-pane windows are a primary source of energy loss in many homes. By upgrading to well-insulated, energy efficient replacement windows, you significantly reduce the amount of energy required by your HVAC system to maintain a comfortable indoor temperature, which directly translates to lower monthly utility bills.
