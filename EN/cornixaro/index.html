Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about tire maintenance and care.

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?
It is generally recommended to have your tires rotated every 5,000 to 7,000 miles. Regular tire rotation ensures even wear across all four tires, which extends their lifespan and improves your vehicle's handling and safety.
What is included in a professional tire installation?
A comprehensive tire installation involves removing the old tires, mounting the new ones onto your wheels, inflating them to the correct pressure, and precisely balancing the wheel and tire assembly to prevent vibrations while driving.
How does your seasonal tire storage work?
When you switch between summer and winter tires, we facilitate secure, climate-controlled tire storage for your off-season set. This protects the rubber from extreme temperatures and frees up valuable space in your own garage.
When do I know it's time for a tire replacement?
You should consider a tire replacement when the tread depth reaches 2/32 of an inch, if you notice uneven wear patterns, visible cracks, bulges on the sidewall, or if the tires are more than six years old, regardless of tread wear.
Do you recommend a wheel alignment with new tires?
Yes, it is highly recommended to perform a wheel alignment when installing new tires. If your vehicle is out of alignment, it can cause your new tires to wear unevenly and prematurely, negating the benefits of the new investment.
