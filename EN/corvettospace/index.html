Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about switching to residential solar.

How long does a residential solar installation take?

Most residential solar installations take 1 to 3 days to complete the physical work on your roof. However, the entire process—including custom design, acquiring necessary permits, and securing utility approvals—can take several weeks before the actual installation begins.

Will solar panels completely eliminate my electric bill?

While generating your own solar power significantly reduces your monthly utility costs, you may still receive a small bill. This is typically a basic connection fee to remain tied to the utility grid, which is necessary unless you install a fully off-grid system with extensive battery storage.

Are photovoltaic systems difficult to maintain?

No, residential solar panels require very little maintenance. Because they have no moving parts, they are highly durable. Occasional cleaning to remove accumulated dust, leaves, or debris is usually sufficient to maintain optimal energy production throughout the year.

Do you help with solar permits and paperwork?

Yes, absolutely. Navigating local regulations can be complex, so our team handles all necessary building permits, utility interconnection agreements, and any municipal paperwork required to get your solar installation approved and activated legally.

How do I know if my roof is suitable for residential solar?

Before any commitments are made, we conduct a thorough site assessment. We evaluate your roof's age, structural condition, angle, and shading from nearby trees or buildings to ensure it is structurally sound and ideally positioned for generating solar power.
