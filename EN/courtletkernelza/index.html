Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about preparing for your residential move.

How far in advance should I book a moving company? +

For local movers, we recommend booking at least two to four weeks in advance. If you require long distance movers, it is best to secure your date four to eight weeks prior to your move, especially during the busy summer months to ensure availability.

Are packing services included in the standard quote? +

Standard moving quotes typically cover loading, transport, and unloading. Full or partial packing services are usually offered as an add-on. Be sure to request packing assistance during your initial inquiry so the moving company can provide an accurate, all-inclusive estimate.

How are long distance moves priced differently than local moves? +

Local movers typically charge an hourly rate based on the number of crew members and trucks required. In contrast, long distance movers calculate their rates based on the total weight (or volume) of your belongings and the total distance traveled between your old and new home.

What items will moving companies refuse to transport? +

For safety and legal reasons, a moving company will generally not transport hazardous materials, flammable liquids, explosives, perishable foods, or live plants. It is also recommended that you personally transport high-value items like jewelry, important documents, and cash.

Do I need to empty my dresser drawers before moving? +

It depends on the furniture and the moving company's policy. Generally, light clothing can remain in sturdy dressers. However, heavy items, fragile objects, or loose papers should be removed and packed separately. If you opt for professional packing services, the crew will handle this preparation for you.
