Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about auto glass and windshield replacement.

How long does a typical windshield replacement take?
Most professional windshield replacement services can be completed in about one to two hours. However, it is generally recommended to wait an additional hour for the specialized urethane adhesive to cure properly before driving the vehicle safely.
Can my auto glass be repaired instead of replaced?
Often, yes. If the damage is a minor chip smaller than a quarter, or a crack less than a few inches long that isn't directly in the driver's line of sight, a quick chip repair is usually possible and highly effective.
Is chip repair effective for long-term safety?
Yes, professional chip repair is a structural fix that restores the integrity of the auto glass. It effectively prevents the crack from spreading further, maintaining the safety of your windshield for the long term.
Do you handle side car window replacement?
Absolutely. The providers we connect you with offer comprehensive car window replacement for side and rear glass. Unlike windshields, side windows are typically made of tempered glass which shatters completely and must be fully replaced.
When is it safe to drive after a windshield repair?
For a minor windshield repair (like fixing a chip), you can usually drive immediately after the service is completed. For a full windshield replacement, you must adhere to the safe drive-away time provided by the technician to allow the adhesives to set properly.
