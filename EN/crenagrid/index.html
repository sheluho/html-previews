Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule HVAC maintenance?

It is generally recommended to have your heating and cooling systems inspected and serviced at least twice a year. Schedule an AC tune-up in the spring before the heavy summer heat, and a furnace or heat pump inspection in the fall before freezing temperatures arrive.

What are the signs I need a new air conditioner?

Key indicators include an AC unit that is over 10-15 years old, requires frequent or costly repairs, produces strange noises or odors, or fails to cool your home evenly. A steady, unexplained increase in your energy bills can also signal that your current unit is losing efficiency and an AC installation might be necessary.

Is a heat pump better than a traditional furnace?

The best choice depends largely on your local climate and specific home setup. Heat pumps are incredibly energy-efficient because they move heat rather than generate it, and they provide both heating and cooling from one unit. However, in extremely cold climates, a traditional gas furnace may provide more robust, rapid heating.

Do the providers featured here handle commercial heating and cooling?

Yes, many of the industry leaders we promote, such as E.A.C. Heating & Air, possess the licensing, equipment, and expertise to handle large-scale commercial HVAC installations and maintenance, in addition to standard residential services.

How long does a typical AC installation take?

A straightforward replacement of an existing air conditioner usually takes about one full day (8-10 hours) for a professional team. If the project requires installing new ductwork, modifying existing electrical connections, or upgrading a complex HVAC system, the timeline may extend to several days.
