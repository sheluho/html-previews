Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I need a chip repair or a full windshield replacement?

Generally, if a rock chip is smaller than a quarter and not directly in the driver's critical line of vision, a chip repair is possible. If the damage is larger, spreading, or deep, a full windshield replacement is required to maintain vehicle safety.

How long does a windshield replacement take?

A standard windshield replacement typically takes about one to two hours to complete. However, technicians advise waiting an additional hour before driving to allow the specialized adhesives to cure properly for maximum safety.

Will my auto insurance cover auto glass services?

Many comprehensive insurance policies cover windshield repair and replacement, sometimes with zero deductible for repairs. We recommend contacting your insurance provider to verify your specific coverage details before scheduling service.

Can you perform a car window replacement for side and rear glass?

Yes, the professionals we promote handle all types of auto glass. Unlike windshields, side and rear windows are usually made of tempered glass that shatters completely upon impact, requiring a full car window replacement and careful cleanup of the interior.

Do I have to bring my car to a shop for repairs?

Not necessarily. Many auto glass providers offer mobile service, meaning skilled technicians can come to your home, office, or other convenient location to perform the repair or replacement on-site.
