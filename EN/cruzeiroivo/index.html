Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about bathroom remodels and shower installations.

What is involved in a walk-in shower installation?

A walk-in shower installation typically involves removing the existing tub or shower unit, preparing the plumbing, installing a waterproof pan or base, executing precise tile installation for the walls and floor, and fitting a custom glass enclosure and fixtures.

How long does a typical bathroom remodel take?

The timeline varies based on the scope. A straightforward bathtub replacement might take just a few days, while a full-service bathroom renovation involving structural changes, new plumbing, and extensive tile work can take several weeks to complete properly.

Can I do a bathtub replacement without changing the whole room layout?

Yes. Many bathtub replacements and tub-to-shower conversions are designed to fit into the exact footprint of your old tub. This minimizes disruption to the rest of the bathroom and is a highly efficient way to update the space.

Do you promote services that handle the plumbing and tile installation?

Absolutely. We highlight full-service bathroom remodeling companies. This means the service providers generally manage all aspects of the project from demolition and rough plumbing to the final detailed tile installation and fixture mounting.

What materials are best for a bathroom renovation?

For wet areas like walk-in showers, non-porous materials such as porcelain tile, quartz, or high-quality acrylics are ideal due to their durability and mold resistance. The contractors we feature can guide you through selecting the best materials for your specific design and functional needs.
