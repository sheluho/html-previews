Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about auto glass repair and replacement processes.

How long does a windshield replacement typically take?

A standard windshield replacement usually takes about one to two hours. However, technicians often advise waiting an additional hour before driving to ensure the urethane adhesive has properly cured and the glass is firmly set.

Can you fix a crack, or do I need a full replacement?

Many small cracks and chips smaller than a quarter can be successfully repaired using specialized resin. If the crack is longer than a few inches, directly in the driver's line of sight, or at the edge of the windshield, a full windshield replacement is typically required for safety.

Is chip repair noticeable after it's completed?

While chip repair significantly improves the visual clarity of the damaged area, a faint outline or tiny blemish may still be slightly visible. The primary goal of a chip repair is to restore the structural integrity of the auto glass and prevent the damage from spreading.

Do you handle side and rear car window replacement?

Yes, our platform connects you with professionals who handle complete car window replacement for side doors, rear windows, and quarter glass. Because this glass is tempered and shatters completely, these services involve safely cleaning out the debris and installing a fresh panel.

How soon can I wash my car after new auto glass is installed?

It is highly recommended to wait at least 24 to 48 hours before taking your vehicle through a high-pressure car wash. This waiting period ensures that the specialized seals and adhesives used during the windshield replacement process have completely cured.
