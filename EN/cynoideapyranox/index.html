Frequently Asked Questions

How exactly does the seasonal tire storage work?

It's simple: you bring your vehicle in for your seasonal tire installation. The technicians remove your current set, clean them, inspect them for wear, and store them in a secure, climate-controlled facility until the next season. You leave with your new set installed and an empty trunk.

Why is regular tire rotation so important?

Tires wear differently depending on their position on the vehicle (front tires typically wear faster due to steering and engine weight). A routine tire rotation moves them to different positions, ensuring the tread wears evenly. This significantly extends the life of your tires and improves overall safety.

When is it time for a complete tire replacement?

You should consider tire replacement when your tread depth falls below 2/32 of an inch, if you notice uneven wear patterns that alignment can't fix, or if the rubber shows signs of aging like cracking or bulging. A professional inspection can give you a definitive answer.

Do I need a wheel alignment when getting new tires?

Yes, it is highly recommended. Getting a wheel alignment alongside your tire installation ensures that your new tires sit perfectly straight relative to the road. Without it, misaligned suspension can cause your brand new tires to wear out prematurely and unevenly.

Are my stored tires insured and kept safe?

Absolutely. The promoted facilities keep your tires in a dedicated, climate-controlled warehouse to prevent rubber deterioration from extreme heat or cold. They are fully inventoried, insured, and tracked so they are ready the moment you need your next seasonal swap.
