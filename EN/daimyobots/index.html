Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about our planning process.

What exactly does a full-service wedding planner do?

A full-service wedding planner manages every aspect of your event from inception to conclusion. This includes budget development, venue scouting, vendor curation and contract management, overall event design, and complete logistical coordination on the wedding day itself.

Do we need a day of coordinator if our venue provides a manager?

Yes. A venue manager is responsible for the venue's facilities, staff, and food (if in-house). A dedicated day of coordinator works exclusively for you, managing your specific timeline, coordinating external vendors (photographers, florists, bands), setting up personal decor, and handling any personal emergencies that arise.

Can you help us plan a destination wedding?

Absolutely. We specialize in destination wedding logistics. We assist with researching locations, securing room blocks, planning welcome parties and farewell brunches, and acting as the liaison between you and international or out-of-state vendors.

When should we hire a wedding coordinator?

For full-service planning, we recommend reaching out 12 to 18 months in advance. If you are looking for a day of coordinator or partial planning assistance, securing our services 6 to 8 months prior to your date ensures availability and a smooth transition of details.

How do you handle vendor selection and contracts?

We provide a curated list of trusted professionals whose style and pricing align with your vision and budget. We review all contracts to ensure terms are fair and clearly outline the deliverables, protecting your interests before you sign.
