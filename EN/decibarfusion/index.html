Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about maintaining your HVAC systems.

How often should I schedule maintenance for my HVAC system?

It is highly recommended to have your heating and cooling systems inspected at least twice a year. Schedule an AC checkup in the spring and a furnace inspection in the fall to ensure peak performance year-round.

What are the signs that I need a new air conditioner?

If your current unit is over 10-15 years old, requires frequent repairs, produces strange noises, or fails to cool your home evenly, it might be time for a new AC installation. Upgrading often results in significant energy savings.

Are heat pumps a good choice for my home?

Yes, a heat pump is an excellent, energy-efficient solution for many homes. Because they transfer heat rather than generate it, they provide both heating and cooling functions seamlessly, often lowering monthly utility bills.

How can I improve my indoor air quality?

Regularly changing your HVAC filters is the most crucial step. Additionally, consider scheduling professional duct cleaning, installing whole-home humidifiers, or adding UV purifiers to your existing heating and air conditioning setup.

What should I do if my furnace suddenly stops working?

First, check your thermostat settings and ensure the circuit breaker hasn't tripped. Also, verify that your air filter isn't completely clogged. If these basic checks don't resolve the issue, submit a service request immediately for professional diagnostic and repair.
