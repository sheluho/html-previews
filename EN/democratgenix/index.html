Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in a whole home remodeling project?

A whole home remodeling project typically involves a comprehensive update of your property's interior and sometimes exterior. This can include redesigning floor plans, updating electrical and plumbing systems, installing new flooring, completely renovating kitchens and bathrooms, and enhancing overall energy efficiency.

What does "turnkey renovation" mean?

Turnkey renovation means that the general contractor handles every single aspect of the project for you. From the initial design and permitting phases to the final construction and cleanup, the entire process is managed by one dedicated team, allowing you to "turn the key" and walk into a completed, ready-to-live-in home.

Do I need to move out during a home addition or major remodel?

This largely depends on the scope of the project. For extensive whole home remodeling that affects primary living spaces, kitchens, and bathrooms simultaneously, temporarily relocating is often recommended for your safety and comfort. For a targeted home addition, you can often remain in the house while the work is isolated to one area.

How do you ensure the new home addition matches the original house?

Expert builders take meticulous care in sourcing materials that match or complement your existing structure. From the exterior siding and roofing materials to interior trim and flooring transitions, the goal of a professional general contractor is to make the home addition look like it was always part of the original architectural design.
