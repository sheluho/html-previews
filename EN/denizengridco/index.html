Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly does a full-service wedding planner do?

A full-service wedding planner manages the entire scope of your event. This includes budget creation, venue scouting, vendor matching, contract negotiation, comprehensive event design, and complete on-site management. They take your vision and handle all the complex logistics required to bring it to life.

Do I need a day of coordinator if my venue provides a manager?

Yes, highly recommended. A venue manager is responsible for the building, food, and staff provided by the venue. A day of coordinator works directly for you. They manage your personal timeline, coordinate external vendors (photographers, florists, band), handle personal items, and ensure your specific vision is executed seamlessly.

How far in advance should we start wedding planning?

For a full-service experience, we recommend beginning the process 12 to 18 months in advance. This allows ample time to secure high-demand venues and premium vendors. However, our partners are highly adaptable and can expedite the planning process for shorter timelines if necessary.

Do you facilitate destination wedding planning?

Absolutely. Planning a destination wedding requires a specialized skill set to manage remote logistics, language barriers, and international vendor relations. We connect you with teams experienced in executing flawless celebrations anywhere in the world.

Will hiring a wedding coordinator save us money?

Often, yes. An experienced wedding coordinator helps you avoid costly mistakes, recommends reliable vendors that fit your budget, and prevents overspending in areas that don't impact your overall guest experience. Their industry relationships and logistical foresight are invaluable assets to your budget management.
