Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about residential solar panel installation.

How much can solar power save me on my energy bills?

Savings vary depending on your current energy usage, local utility rates, and the size of the residential solar system installed. Many homeowners experience significant reductions in their monthly electricity bills, often offsetting the cost of the solar installation over time.

Are residential solar systems durable against severe weather?

Yes. Modern solar panels are rigorously tested to withstand harsh weather conditions, including heavy rain, high winds, and hail. Professional solar installation ensures they are securely mounted to your roof, protecting both the photovoltaic array and your home.

What is the typical lifespan of photovoltaic cells?

High-quality solar panels generally have a productive lifespan of 25 to 30 years or more. Even after this period, they continue to produce solar power, though at a slightly reduced efficiency compared to when they were brand new.

Will solar installation damage my roof?

No, quite the opposite. When executed by certified professionals, solar installation actually protects the portion of the roof it covers from weather and UV wear. Installers also conduct comprehensive roof assessments beforehand to ensure structural integrity.

Do I need a battery backup with my solar panels?

While not strictly mandatory, a battery backup is highly recommended. It allows you to store excess solar power generated during the day to be used at night or during grid outages, maximizing your energy independence and the utility of your residential solar system.
