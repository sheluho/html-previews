Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about residential solar panel installation.

How long do residential solar panels typically last?

Modern photovoltaic panels are highly durable and typically designed to produce reliable solar power for 25 to 30 years. Even after this period, they continue to generate electricity, though at a slightly reduced efficiency.

Is my roof suitable for a solar installation?

Most residential roofs can accommodate solar panels. The ideal roof is south-facing with minimal shading from trees or other buildings, but east or west-facing roofs can also be highly effective depending on the custom photovoltaic design.

How much can I save by switching to solar power?

Savings vary based on your local electricity rates, energy consumption, and the size of your solar installation. However, many homeowners see a significant reduction in their monthly utility bills, allowing the system to pay for itself over time.

What happens to my solar power during a grid outage?

Standard grid-tied solar systems will shut down during an outage for safety reasons. However, if your residential solar setup includes battery storage integration, you can maintain essential power to your home even when the grid goes down.

Do solar panels increase the value of my home?

Yes, numerous studies indicate that homes equipped with owned (not leased) residential solar systems sell for a premium compared to non-solar homes. Buyers appreciate the immediate benefit of lower utility costs.
