Common Questions

What is the difference between vinyl and fiber cement siding?

Vinyl siding is highly cost-effective, low-maintenance, and comes in a wide variety of colors. Fiber cement siding is thicker, heavier, and can mimic the exact texture of wood or masonry. Fiber cement is exceptionally durable against fire and severe weather, making it a premium choice for long-term house siding investments.

How do I know if I need new attic insulation?

Common signs include unusually high heating and cooling bills, drafts in the upper rooms, uneven temperatures throughout the house, or ice dams forming on your roof in winter. If your current home insulation sits below the floor joists in the attic, it is highly likely you need an upgrade to meet modern efficiency standards.

What makes spray foam better than traditional fiberglass?

Unlike traditional fiberglass batts which only slow down heat transfer, spray foam acts as both a thermal barrier and an impermeable air seal. It expands upon application to fill tiny gaps and cracks where drafts originate. This dual-action performance makes spray foam a highly efficient home insulation method.

Does replacing my siding improve insulation?

Yes. While the primary role of house siding is weather protection, installing new siding provides the perfect opportunity to add rigid foam board beneath the new panels. This exterior thermal wrap significantly boosts your overall home insulation, reducing thermal bridging through the wall studs.

How long does a typical installation project take?

The timeline depends entirely on the scope of the project. A standard attic insulation upgrade can often be completed in a single day. A full exterior replacement involving tear-off and installation of new fiber cement siding or vinyl siding may take one to two weeks depending on the size of the home and weather conditions.
