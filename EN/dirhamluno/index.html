Common Questions

How long does a windshield replacement take?

Most windshield replacement appointments take between one to two hours. This timeframe includes the removal of the old glass, installation of the new auto glass, and the necessary time needed for the urethane adhesive to cure sufficiently so you can drive your vehicle safely.

Can you repair a crack, or do I need a replacement?

We can typically perform a successful chip repair if the damage is smaller than the size of a quarter and is not located in the driver's direct line of sight. Larger cracks, deep divots, or damage that reaches the edge of the glass usually compromise structural integrity and require a full windshield replacement.

Do you handle insurance claims for auto glass?

Yes, the service providers we connect you with work with most major auto insurance networks. They can often assist you in verifying your coverage and filing your claim to make the windshield repair or replacement process as smooth and stress-free as possible.

What is ADAS calibration, and do I need it?

If your modern vehicle is equipped with Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (such as forward-facing cameras mounted on or near the windshield), professional calibration is necessary after a replacement. This ensures that crucial safety features like lane departure warnings and automatic braking function correctly with the new glass.

Is it safe to wash my car right after getting new auto glass?

We strongly recommend waiting at least 24 to 48 hours before taking your vehicle through a high-pressure commercial car wash. This vital waiting period allows the specialized urethane seals to cure and set completely after your car window replacement, preventing leaks and seal displacement.
