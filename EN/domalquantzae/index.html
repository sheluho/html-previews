Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries regarding our detailing and coating processes.

What is a ceramic coating and how does it work?

A ceramic coating is a liquid polymer applied to the exterior of a vehicle. It chemically bonds with the factory clear coat, creating a layer of protection that is semi-permanent. This provides superior paint protection against UV damage, chemical stains, and minor scratches compared to traditional waxes.

How long does a full auto detailing service take?

The duration of an auto detailing service depends heavily on the condition of the vehicle and the package selected. A standard exterior and interior detailing might take 3 to 5 hours, whereas extensive paint correction followed by a ceramic coating application can take a full day or more.

Does interior detailing remove deep stains and odors?

Yes, our comprehensive interior detailing process involves specialized extraction machines and steam cleaners designed to pull deep-seated dirt and stains from upholstery and carpets. We also utilize ozone treatments and specific enzymes to neutralize and eliminate lingering odors, rather than just masking them.

Is professional paint protection really worth the investment?

Absolutely. Professional paint protection not only keeps your car looking newer for much longer, but it also preserves the vehicle's resale value. By preventing clear coat failure, oxidation, and etching from bird droppings or tree sap, a ceramic coating pays for itself by reducing the need for constant reapplications of wax and future paint repairs.

How often should I schedule regular car detailing?

For a daily driven vehicle, we recommend a thorough car detailing every 4 to 6 months to maintain the interior fabrics and exterior finish. If you have a ceramic coating applied, simple maintenance washes every few weeks are usually sufficient to keep the vehicle looking pristine.
