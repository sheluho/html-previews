Common Inquiries

What does a turnkey renovation include?

A turnkey renovation means the general contractor manages the entire project from start to finish. This includes design consultations, obtaining necessary permits, demolition, structural work, plumbing, electrical, and the final installation of finishes. You simply "turn the key" and walk into a completed space.

How long does whole home remodeling take?

Timelines vary heavily based on the scope of work, property size, and material availability. A standard whole home renovation might take anywhere from 3 to 6 months, whereas projects involving extensive structural changes or a home addition can take 6 to 9 months or more.

Do I need to move out during a home addition?

If the home addition is isolated (like adding a room to the back of the house), you may be able to stay. However, if the project involves major roof removals, foundational work affecting the main living areas, or utility shutoffs, it is often safer and more efficient for homeowners to relocate temporarily.

What is the role of a general contractor?

A general contractor is the project manager for your renovation. They hire and coordinate specialized subcontractors (plumbers, electricians, carpenters), secure building permits, ensure all work meets local building codes, and keep the project on schedule and within the agreed budget.

Will remodeling increase my property value?

Yes, strategic updates such as kitchen modernizations, bathroom overhauls, and adding square footage via a home addition generally yield a strong return on investment. Updating aging infrastructure also makes the home more appealing to future buyers by preventing structural issues.
