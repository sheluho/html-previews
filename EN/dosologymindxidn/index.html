Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly does a whole home remodeling project entail?

A whole home renovation goes beyond single-room updates. It typically involves altering the floor plan, upgrading plumbing and electrical systems, and creating a cohesive design style across the entire house. As a general contractor, we manage all these moving parts to ensure a unified result.

Do you handle the architectural plans and city permits for a home addition?

Yes. Our turnkey renovation approach means we coordinate the design phase, engineer necessary structural changes, and submit all documentation required to secure local building permits before construction begins.

How long does a comprehensive home renovation take?

Timelines vary significantly based on the scope of work, square footage, and material availability. A standard whole home remodel can take anywhere from 3 to 6 months of active construction, while a major home addition may require longer. We provide detailed schedules during the planning phase.

Can I live in my house during the construction process?

For extensive whole home remodeling, we generally recommend homeowners secure alternative living arrangements. Major renovations involve shutting off utilities, removing walls, and significant dust. Moving out ensures safety and allows the construction team to work much more efficiently.

What sets a turnkey renovation apart from hiring individual tradesmen?

When you hire individual trades, you become the project manager—coordinating schedules, resolving conflicts, and taking on liability. A turnkey renovation with a licensed general contractor provides a single point of contact and accountability, ensuring quality control and adherence to a cohesive timeline.
