Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical residential solar installation take?
While the physical installation of solar panels on your roof often takes only one to three days, the entire process—including site assessment, custom design, permitting, and final utility inspections—can take several weeks to a few months depending on your local regulations.
Are solar panels effective during cloudy or rainy weather?
Yes. Modern photovoltaic systems are highly efficient and will continue to generate electricity even on cloudy days, though at a reduced capacity compared to direct sunlight. The UV light that penetrates clouds is still captured by the solar cells.
What kind of maintenance does a photovoltaic system require?
Residential solar panels generally require very little maintenance. It is recommended to have them lightly cleaned a few times a year if you live in a dusty area, and to schedule a routine professional inspection to ensure the inverters and wiring remain in optimal condition.
Will installing solar power completely eliminate my electric bill?
This depends on your household's energy consumption and the size of your solar array. Many homeowners drastically reduce their bills, and some achieve net-zero usage. However, if you remain connected to the grid, you will typically still have a small monthly connection fee from your utility provider.
How do I determine if my roof is suitable for solar panels?
Ideal roofs have a clear, unshaded area facing south, southwest, or southeast, with materials in good condition. Professional installers will assess your roof's age, angle, shading from trees, and structural integrity before recommending a residential solar installation.
