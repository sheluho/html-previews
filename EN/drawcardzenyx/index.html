Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about securing a professional wedding videographer.

What is the difference between a cinematic wedding video and a documentary edit?

A cinematic wedding video is typically a shorter, highly stylized highlight reel (usually 3-8 minutes) set to music, focusing on the emotional peaks of the day. A documentary edit is a longer, linear format that includes the full ceremony and all the reception speeches exactly as they happened, edited between multiple camera angles.

How far in advance should we book a wedding videographer?

High-quality wedding videography teams book up very quickly, especially during peak wedding seasons (Spring and Fall). It is highly recommended to secure your videographer 9 to 12 months in advance to ensure availability for your date.

Do you provide raw footage of the event?

Policies on raw footage vary by the specific provider you choose to work with. While some studios offer raw footage for an additional fee, many focus entirely on delivering polished, finalized cinematic wedding films and documentary edits as their finished product.

Will the videographer get in the way of the photographer?

Professional event videography teams are trained to be unobtrusive. They work collaboratively with your photographers, communicating throughout the day to ensure both teams get the beautiful shots they need without interfering with the natural flow of your wedding or stepping into each other's frames.

Can we choose the music for our wedding film?

Due to copyright laws, videographers must license the music used in your cinematic wedding video. Most studios utilize high-quality, licensed music libraries and will work with you to understand your musical tastes to select a soundtrack that perfectly matches the mood and energy of your unique film.
