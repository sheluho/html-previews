Common Questions

What are the main benefits of spray foam insulation?

Spray foam insulation provides an exceptional airtight seal that traditional fiberglass often cannot match. It expands to fill gaps and cracks, offering a higher R-value per inch, which significantly reduces energy loss, prevents moisture buildup, and improves overall indoor air quality by blocking outdoor allergens.

How long does new house siding typically last?

The lifespan depends heavily on the material chosen. High-quality vinyl siding can easily last 20 to 40 years with minimal maintenance. Fiber cement siding is even more durable, often lasting up to 50 years, as it strongly resists rot, fire, and termite damage.

Is upgrading attic insulation really worth the investment?

Yes. Because heat rises, a poorly insulated attic is one of the primary sources of energy loss in a home. Upgrading your attic insulation is generally considered one of the most cost-effective ways to lower utility bills and maintain consistent temperatures throughout your living spaces year-round.

What is the difference between vinyl and fiber cement siding?

Vinyl siding is made from a durable plastic, making it lightweight, affordable, and practically maintenance-free. Fiber cement siding is a heavier composite of cement, sand, and cellulose fibers; it offers a thicker, more wood-like appearance and superior resistance to fire and extreme weather, though it typically requires a higher initial investment.

How do I know if my home insulation needs replacing?

Common signs that your home insulation is failing include noticeable drafts near walls or windows, unusually high heating or cooling bills, uneven temperatures between different rooms, and issues with moisture or ice dams on your roof during the winter months. A professional energy audit can confirm these issues.
