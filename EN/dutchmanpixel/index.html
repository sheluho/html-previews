Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into our home remodeling process.

What exactly does a turnkey renovation include?

A turnkey renovation means we handle everything as your general contractor. This includes initial design consultations, obtaining necessary permits, demolition, managing all trades (plumbing, electrical, framing), installing finishes, and final cleanup. You simply turn the key and walk into a completed space.

Do I need to hire an architect for a home addition?

It depends on the scope of the project. For significant structural changes or new home additions, architectural plans are usually required for permitting. We work closely with trusted architects and structural engineers to ensure the design is sound and compliant before we begin the building phase.

How long does a whole home remodeling project typically take?

Timelines vary greatly based on the size of the home and the complexity of the design. A standard interior remodel might take a few months, while a project involving structural changes or a large home addition can take six months or longer. We provide a detailed timeline during the planning phase.

Do you manage all the necessary building permits?

Yes. Navigating local building codes and securing permits is a core responsibility of a general contractor. We handle all documentation and schedule necessary municipal inspections throughout the home remodeling process to ensure your project is fully compliant.

Can I live in the house during a whole home remodel?

For extensive whole home renovations that involve significant demolition, electrical, and plumbing work, we strongly recommend making alternate living arrangements. This ensures your safety, reduces stress from living in a construction zone, and allows our crews to work more efficiently to complete your project sooner.
