Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about auto glass repair and replacement.

How long does a windshield replacement take?

A standard windshield replacement typically takes about 1 to 2 hours to complete. However, technicians recommend waiting an additional hour before driving the vehicle to ensure the urethane adhesive has properly cured and the glass is safely secured.

Can you repair a chip instead of replacing the whole windshield?

Yes, absolutely. If the chip is smaller than a quarter and not located directly in the driver's line of sight, a simple chip repair is often possible. This process involves injecting a clear resin into the damaged area, which restores strength and prevents the crack from spreading.

Do you handle car window replacement for side and rear glass?

Yes, our partners provide comprehensive car window replacement services for all types of vehicle glass, including side door windows, rear windshields, and quarter glass. They clean up the shattered glass and install a brand new window to factory specifications.

Is it safe to drive immediately after an auto glass replacement?

No, it is not recommended to drive immediately. The adhesives used to secure the new auto glass require a specific "Safe Drive Away Time" (SDAT) to cure, which is usually around one hour depending on humidity and temperature. Your technician will advise you on the exact wait time.

Does my insurance cover windshield repair or replacement?

Many comprehensive auto insurance policies cover auto glass services. In fact, some insurance companies will waive the deductible entirely for a chip repair to encourage fixing it before it requires a full windshield replacement. We recommend checking with your insurance provider for your specific coverage details.
