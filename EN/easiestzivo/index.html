Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries regarding professional carpet and fabric care.

How long does carpet cleaning take to dry?

Drying times vary based on the specific method utilized. Traditional steam cleaning can take up to 24 hours to dry completely. However, advanced low-moisture carpet cleaning methods highlighted by our partners generally allow your carpets to be dry and ready for foot traffic in just one to two hours.

Can professional stain removal eliminate old or set-in spots?

While no technician can guarantee the complete removal of every single spot, professional stain removal yields significantly better results than over-the-counter products. Experts use specialized solutions tailored to the specific chemical composition of the stain and the fabric type, which often successfully lifts even stubborn, older blemishes.

Is upholstery cleaning safe for delicate fabrics?

Yes, professional upholstery cleaning begins with a thorough inspection of your furniture's material. Technicians determine the safest and most effective cleaning agents—whether dry cleaning solvents or gentle water-based extraction—to ensure delicate fabrics are cleaned safely without shrinking or color bleeding.

How often should I schedule professional rug cleaning?

For typical households, it is recommended to have your area and oriental rugs professionally cleaned every 12 to 18 months. However, if the rug is placed in a high-traffic area or if you have indoor pets, scheduling a deep cleaning every 6 to 12 months will help prevent fiber breakdown and maintain its appearance.

What is the difference between steam cleaning and low-moisture methods?

Steam cleaning (hot water extraction) injects large volumes of heated water and detergent into the carpet, then extracts it. While effective, it leaves the carpet wet for hours. Low-moisture methods use specialized equipment and encapsulating cleaning solutions that require a fraction of the water, dramatically reducing drying times while still lifting dirt effectively.
