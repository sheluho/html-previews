Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into the remodeling and construction process.

What does a whole home remodeling project entail?

Whole home remodeling involves a comprehensive overhaul of your property's interior and sometimes exterior. It typically includes reconfiguring floor plans, upgrading electrical and plumbing systems, installing new flooring, and fully renovating key spaces like kitchens and bathrooms to create a cohesive, modernized home.

How long does a typical home renovation take?

The timeline for a home renovation varies widely based on the scope of work. A focused kitchen or bath update might take 6 to 8 weeks, while a large-scale home addition or full turnkey renovation can take anywhere from 4 to 8 months. A professional general contractor will provide a detailed schedule during the planning phase.

Why should I hire a general contractor instead of managing it myself?

Acting as your own project manager requires immense time, specialized knowledge, and established trade relationships. A licensed general contractor handles permitting, scheduling, quality control, and unexpected structural issues. This oversight mitigates risk, ensures building codes are met, and ultimately protects your investment.

Can you help with a home addition for my growing family?

Yes. The expert builders we promote specialize in custom home additions. Whether you are building outwards or adding a second story, the design and build teams ensure the new structure seamlessly matches your home's original architectural style while providing the vital extra square footage you need.

What makes the turnkey renovation process different?

A turnkey renovation means the general contractor handles absolutely everything from start to finish. You approve the design and materials upfront, and the team executes the entire build. When you return, the space is completely finished, cleaned, and ready for you to enjoy—without the daily stress of managing construction.
