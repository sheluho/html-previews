Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about our professional cleaning processes.

How long does the carpet cleaning process take to dry?
Because advanced extraction methods use a fraction of the water that traditional steam cleaning requires, your carpets will typically dry in just 1 to 2 hours. This fast drying time significantly reduces the risk of mold and mildew growth beneath your carpets.
Is the upholstery cleaning safe for all fabric types?
Yes, the technicians are trained to evaluate the fabric of your furniture before beginning. They use specialized, gentle solutions designed to lift dirt safely from synthetic blends, natural fibers, and delicate weaves without causing color bleeding or shrinkage.
Can you permanently remove old pet stains and odors?
Standard cleaning often just masks smells, but specialized pet urine removal treatments target the issue at a molecular level. By breaking down the urine crystals deep within the carpet pad and fibers, the source of the odor and the visible stain are effectively neutralized.
What is the difference between your process and traditional steam cleaning?
Traditional steam cleaning relies on high volumes of soapy water, which can leave behind a sticky residue that actually attracts more dirt over time. The preferred modern method uses carbonation to lift dirt to the surface, requiring much less water and leaving no dirt-attracting residue.
Do I need to move my furniture before the rug cleaning?
Technicians will gladly move light furniture (like chairs and small tables) during the appointment. For larger or highly delicate items, it is recommended to have them moved prior to arrival. If a heavy piece cannot be moved, the team will carefully clean around its base.
