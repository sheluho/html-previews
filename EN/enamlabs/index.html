Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about upgrading your home's exterior and thermal envelope.

What are the main benefits of spray foam insulation?

Spray foam provides an exceptional air seal and high R-value per inch. It expands to fill gaps and cracks, significantly reducing drafts, keeping conditioned air inside, and helping to lower overall energy consumption compared to traditional fiberglass batts.

How do I know if my house needs new attic insulation?

Signs that your attic insulation may be failing include unusually high heating or cooling bills, uneven temperatures between rooms, drafts, or noticeable ice dams on your roof during winter months. A professional inspection can measure your current R-value.

What is the difference between vinyl siding and fiber cement siding?

Vinyl siding is highly cost-effective, low maintenance, and comes in many colors. Fiber cement siding is typically thicker, mimics the exact look of wood grain, is highly fire-resistant, and offers superior durability against extreme weather, though it often requires a higher initial investment.

Can new house siding improve energy efficiency?

Yes. While standard siding protects against the elements, adding an insulated backing to vinyl siding or installing rigid foam board beneath fiber cement siding acts as a continuous thermal blanket over your home's framing, reducing thermal bridging.

How long does a typical siding or insulation project take?

Timelines vary based on the home's size and the scope of work. A standard attic insulation upgrade can often be completed in one to two days. A complete house siding replacement typically takes one to two weeks, depending on weather conditions and material availability.
