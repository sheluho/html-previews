Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to help you make informed decisions about your home exterior.

Why should I choose spray foam over traditional fiberglass insulation?

Spray foam provides a dual benefit: it acts as both an exceptional thermal insulator and an air barrier. Unlike traditional fiberglass which allows air to pass through, spray foam expands to fill gaps, preventing drafts, reducing moisture buildup, and significantly lowering energy bills.

Is it necessary to upgrade my attic insulation?

Yes, a significant amount of heat loss (and heat gain in the summer) occurs through the roof. Upgrading your attic insulation is one of the most cost-effective ways to improve your home's overall energy efficiency and comfort levels year-round.

What are the benefits of vinyl siding?

Vinyl siding is highly popular because it is incredibly low-maintenance, cost-effective, and versatile. It doesn't require painting, resists pests and rot, and is available in a wide variety of colors and textures to suit any architectural style.

How does fiber cement siding differ from vinyl?

Fiber cement siding is heavier and thicker than vinyl, made from a mixture of wood pulp and cement. It offers superior durability, is non-combustible (fire-resistant), and can convincingly mimic the texture of natural wood grain. While it generally requires a higher initial investment, its longevity is unmatched.

Can you install home insulation and house siding at the same time?

Absolutely. In fact, replacing your house siding is the perfect time to upgrade your exterior wall insulation. Adding rigid foam board or wrapped house wrap beneath new siding maximizes your thermal barrier without disrupting the interior of your home.
