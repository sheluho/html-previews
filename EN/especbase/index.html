Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

It is generally recommended to have your tires rotated every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, or every time you get your oil changed. Regular tire rotation ensures even tread wear, which extends the life of your tires and maintains optimal handling.

What is included in your promoted tire installation service?

A standard professional tire installation typically includes the removal of your old tires, mounting the new tires onto your wheels, precise computerized balancing to prevent vibrations, and installing new valve stems if necessary.

How does the seasonal tire storage work?

When you switch between summer and winter tires, you can opt for seasonal tire storage. Your off-season tires are inspected, cataloged, and stored in a secure, climate-controlled facility away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures to preserve the rubber compound.

Do you provide wheel alignment checks?

Yes, proper wheel alignment is crucial when getting a tire replacement or rotation. Misaligned wheels can cause rapid, uneven tire wear and affect your steering. Alignment checks ensure your wheels are set to the manufacturer's precise specifications.

How do I know when it is time for a tire replacement?

You should consider a tire replacement if your tread depth is below 2/32 of an inch, if you notice visible damage like bulges, deep cracks, or cuts in the sidewall, or if your tires are older than six years, regardless of tread depth.
