Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our renovation process.

What is included in a turnkey renovation?

A turnkey renovation means we handle absolutely everything. From initial architectural planning and securing city permits to demolition, construction, and final interior finishing. You simply turn the key and walk into your completed home.

Do I need to hire an architect separately for a home addition?

No, as a comprehensive general contractor, we can manage the architectural and engineering phases through our network of trusted professionals, ensuring the design aligns perfectly with your construction budget from the start.

Can we live in the house during a whole home remodeling project?

This depends entirely on the scope of the work. For extensive whole home renovations involving major plumbing, electrical, or structural changes, it is often safer and more efficient for you to relocate temporarily. We will assess this during your initial consultation.

How long does a typical home addition take?

Timelines vary greatly based on complexity, size, and local permitting speeds. A standard room addition might take 3-4 months of active construction, while a multi-story expansion could take 6-8 months. We provide a detailed schedule before any demolition begins.

Do you pull all the necessary city permits?

Yes. As your licensed general contractor, it is our responsibility to ensure all work is permitted and inspected according to local municipal codes. We navigate the bureaucracy so your project remains fully compliant and legal.
