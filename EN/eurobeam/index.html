Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between plated dinner and buffet catering?
A plated dinner involves waitstaff serving individually portioned meals directly to seated guests, offering a formal and elegant dining experience. Buffet catering features long tables where multiple food options are presented, allowing guests to serve themselves or be served by attendants, providing greater variety and a more relaxed atmosphere.
Do you accommodate dietary restrictions and food allergies?
Yes. The culinary teams we promote are well-versed in creating delicious menus that accommodate vegan, vegetarian, gluten-free, and nut-free dietary requirements. Please mention any specific allergies or restrictions during the menu planning process so they can be safely integrated into your event catering plan.
How far in advance should I book my wedding catering?
For wedding catering, it is highly recommended to secure your date at least 9 to 12 months in advance, especially during peak wedding seasons (spring and fall). Early booking ensures availability and provides ample time for tasting sessions and menu refinement.
Are tastings available before finalizing the menu?
Absolutely. Menu tastings are a crucial part of the event catering planning process. Once you have a preliminary proposal, you can schedule a tasting session to sample selected dishes and make necessary adjustments to ensure the flavor profile perfectly matches your expectations.
Do you provide staffing and tableware for corporate catering?
Yes, comprehensive corporate catering packages often include professional waitstaff, bartenders, and coordinators to manage the event flow. Additionally, rentals for elegant tableware, linens, and serving equipment can be fully arranged to create a seamless and professional presentation.
