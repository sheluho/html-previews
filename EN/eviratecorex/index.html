Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about our wedding planning process.

What is the difference between a wedding planner and a day of coordinator?

A full-service wedding planner is involved from the very beginning, helping with budget creation, venue selection, vendor hiring, and overall design. A day of coordinator (often stepping in a few weeks before the wedding) focuses purely on executing the plans you have already made, ensuring the timeline runs smoothly and managing on-site logistics.

Do you handle destination wedding coordination?

Yes, we specialize in destination wedding coordination. We assist with navigating international or out-of-state vendor contracts, managing travel suggestions for guests, and organizing multi-day event itineraries to ensure a seamless experience away from home.

When should we hire a full-service wedding planner?

We recommend securing your wedding planner immediately after getting engaged, ideally 12 to 18 months before your desired date. This allows us to secure top-tier venues and vendors that book up quickly, and gives us ample time to perfect your wedding planning timeline.

Can you help if we only need a wedding coordinator for the day itself?

Absolutely. Our day of coordinator packages are designed specifically for couples who have done the heavy lifting of planning but want professional management on the actual wedding day to handle vendors, timelines, and any unforeseen issues.

How do you personalize the wedding planning process?

We start with an in-depth consultation to understand your personal style, priorities, and relationship story. Every aspect of our wedding planning is then tailored to reflect your unique identity, ensuring the final celebration is distinctly yours rather than a cookie-cutter event.
