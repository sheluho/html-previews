Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a wedding planner and a day of coordinator?
A full-service wedding planner assists with every step of the journey, from concept creation, budget management, and vendor selection to the actual execution. A day of coordinator typically steps in a few weeks before the wedding to manage logistics, finalize the timeline, and handle the execution strictly on the day of the event, allowing you to relax.
Do your partners handle destination wedding planning?
Yes, the full-service wedding planning company we partner with specializes in destination weddings. They can handle international or out-of-state vendor sourcing, travel logistics, hotel blocks for guests, and legal requirements for marriages abroad.
When should we hire a full-service wedding planning company?
It is highly recommended to secure a wedding planner as soon as you get engaged, ideally 12 to 18 months before your desired wedding date. This ensures they can help you secure the best venues and vendors before they book out.
Can a wedding coordinator help with budget management?
Absolutely. A comprehensive wedding planning package includes detailed budget tracking. Professionals know where you can save money, how to allocate funds effectively, and how to avoid hidden costs that often surprise couples planning independently.
What does day of coordination actually include?
Day of coordination typically encompasses a venue walk-through, timeline creation, vendor communication in the weeks leading up to the wedding, and on-site management for the rehearsal and the entire wedding day. They act as the primary point of contact so you don't have to answer questions while getting ready.
