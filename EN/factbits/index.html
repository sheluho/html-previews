Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to help you make informed decisions.

What are the main benefits of energy efficient windows? ▼

Energy efficient windows feature advanced technologies like low-E glass and gas fills between panes. They dramatically reduce heat transfer, which means your home stays warmer in the winter and cooler in the summer. This reduces the strain on your HVAC system, directly lowering your monthly energy bills and minimizing your carbon footprint.

Why should I choose vinyl windows over wood or aluminum? ▼

Vinyl windows are extremely durable, highly weather-resistant, and virtually maintenance-free. Unlike wood, they won't rot, peel, or require repainting. Unlike aluminum, they offer superior thermal insulation. They provide an excellent balance of longevity, affordability, and energy performance for replacement windows.

How long does a typical window installation take? ▼

Professional window installation is usually completed quickly and efficiently. For a standard sized home, a full set of replacement windows can often be installed in a single day or two. The exact timeline depends on the number of windows, the specific style (such as double hung windows or bay windows), and the condition of your existing frames.

What is the difference between single and double hung windows? ▼

In a single hung window, only the bottom sash moves up and down, while the top sash remains fixed in place. In double hung windows, both the top and bottom sashes are operable. This allows for superior ventilation—letting warm air escape from the top while drawing cool air in through the bottom—and makes cleaning both sides of the glass from inside your home incredibly easy.

Are custom replacement windows necessary for my home? ▼

Yes, custom replacement windows are highly recommended. Because houses settle over time, existing window openings are rarely perfectly square. Custom-measured and manufactured windows ensure a tight, precise fit, which is critical for maximizing energy efficiency, preventing water infiltration, and ensuring smooth operation for years to come.
