Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a standard windshield replacement take?

A standard windshield replacement typically takes between 60 to 90 minutes to complete. However, the urethane adhesive used to seal the glass requires an additional safe drive-away time, usually around one hour, to properly cure before the vehicle can be safely operated.

Can you perform a chip repair instead of replacing the whole glass?

Yes, in many cases a chip repair is entirely possible if the damage is smaller than a quarter and is not located directly in the driver's primary line of vision. Repairing a chip early prevents it from expanding into a large crack that would require a full replacement.

Do you handle car window replacement for all vehicle makes and models?

Absolutely. The service providers we highlight are equipped to handle auto glass replacement for nearly all domestic and foreign vehicles. Whether you drive a compact sedan, a heavy-duty truck, or a luxury SUV, they source the exact glass specifications required for a perfect fit.

Is it safe to drive my car immediately after auto glass installation?

No, it is not recommended to drive immediately. Technicians will advise you on the specific "Safe Drive-Away Time" (SDAT) required for the industrial adhesives to set. Driving before this time elapses can compromise the structural integrity of the windshield replacement and endanger your safety.

Will my auto insurance cover windshield repair or replacement?

Many comprehensive auto insurance policies cover auto glass services, often waiving the deductible completely for a simple chip repair. For full replacements, your standard deductible may apply depending on your specific state laws and policy terms. Most service centers will assist you directly with the insurance claims process.
