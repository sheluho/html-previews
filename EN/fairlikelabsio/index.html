Frequently Asked Questions

What does a full-service wedding planner actually do?
A full-service wedding planner manages every aspect of your event from the initial concept to the final send-off. We handle budget creation, vendor sourcing, contract negotiations, design curation, and detailed timeline management, ensuring a cohesive and stress-free experience.
Do I need a day of coordinator if my venue provides a manager?
Yes. A venue manager is primarily responsible for the venue itself—ensuring the lights, bathrooms, and structural elements are functioning. A day of coordinator works for *you*. We manage your personal timeline, direct vendors, cue the bridal party, and handle any personal details or emergencies.
Can you help with a destination wedding?
Absolutely. We specialize in destination wedding planning. We have experience coordinating with international vendors, managing travel blocks for guests, and navigating local legal requirements for marriage ceremonies abroad.
When should we start the wedding planning process?
Ideally, we recommend reaching out 12 to 18 months before your desired wedding date. This allows ample time to secure your preferred venue and top-tier vendors. However, we are experienced in planning beautiful weddings on shorter timelines as well.
How does a wedding coordinator differ from a planner?
A planner is involved from the very beginning, helping you design the event, hire vendors, and build the budget. A wedding coordinator typically steps in 4-6 weeks before the wedding to take over the plans you have already made, ensuring they are executed flawlessly on the day.
