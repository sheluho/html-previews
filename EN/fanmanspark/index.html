Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about upgrading your home's exterior.

What are the main benefits of spray foam insulation?

Spray foam provides an exceptional air seal that traditional fiberglass often cannot match. It expands to fill gaps and cracks, drastically reducing drafts, lowering energy bills, and even improving indoor air quality by keeping outdoor allergens out.

How long does new vinyl siding typically last?

Modern vinyl siding is engineered for longevity and can easily last 20 to 40 years or more with minimal maintenance. It resists fading, won't rot like wood, and only requires occasional washing to maintain its fresh appearance.

Is fiber cement siding a better choice than wood?

For many homeowners, yes. Fiber cement siding offers the beautiful, classic look of natural wood grain but is highly resistant to fire, termites, and rot. It is incredibly durable and holds paint much longer than traditional wood siding.

How do I know if my home needs new attic insulation?

Common signs include wildly fluctuating indoor temperatures, unexpectedly high heating and cooling bills, drafts in the upper rooms, and the rapid melting of snow on your roof (which can lead to damaging ice dams).

Does upgrading house siding improve energy efficiency?

Yes, especially when paired with an insulated underlayment or house wrap. New siding helps eliminate thermal bridging through the wall studs, adding an extra layer of protection that keeps conditioned air inside where it belongs.
