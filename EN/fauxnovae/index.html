Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about switching your home to solar power.

How long does a standard residential solar installation take?

The physical installation of solar panels typically takes one to three days, depending on the size and complexity of your roof. However, the entire process—including custom design, permitting, and final utility inspections—usually spans several weeks to ensure all local compliance standards are perfectly met.

Are my roof and property suitable for solar panels?

Most homes can accommodate solar power, but efficiency depends heavily on roof condition, orientation, and shading from surrounding trees or buildings. We conduct a thorough, no-obligation site evaluation to determine exactly how a photovoltaic system will perform on your specific property.

What maintenance is required for a photovoltaic system?

Residential solar panels are remarkably low maintenance because they have no moving parts. Generally, occasional cleaning to remove dust or debris and an annual visual inspection are sufficient to keep your solar power generation operating at peak capacity for decades.

Will residential solar panels eliminate my electric bill entirely?

While a properly sized residential solar installation can drastically reduce or offset your electricity usage, most homeowners remain connected to the utility grid. You may still receive a minimal bill for fixed connection fees, though many utility companies offer net metering credits for the excess solar power you generate and return to the grid.

Do you handle the permitting required for solar installation?

Yes. The installation partners featured through FauxNovae manage the entire process comprehensively. This includes drafting structural engineering plans, submitting documentation to local municipalities, securing all necessary permits, and coordinating final inspections for your new solar panels.
