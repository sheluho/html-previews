Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions about tire installation, storage, and maintenance.

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

We generally recommend a tire rotation every 5,000 to 7,000 miles, or every six months. Regular rotation ensures even tread wear, extending the life of your tires and maintaining consistent traction.

Do you offer seasonal tire storage?

Yes, our climate-controlled tire storage facility is designed specifically to protect your off-season tires from temperature fluctuations, UV exposure, and dry rot. We handle the heavy lifting so you don't have to.

When is it time for a tire replacement?

Tires should be replaced when the tread depth reaches 2/32 of an inch, if you notice significant cracking or bulging on the sidewall, or if the tires are more than six years old, regardless of tread wear.

What is included in your wheel alignment service?

A standard wheel alignment involves adjusting the angles of the wheels—camber, caster, and toe—to meet the manufacturer's specifications. This ensures your vehicle tracks straight and prevents premature, uneven tire wear.

How long does a standard tire installation take?

A standard tire installation for a set of four, including mounting and computer balancing, typically takes between 45 minutes to an hour. Adding a wheel alignment may require an additional 30 to 45 minutes.
