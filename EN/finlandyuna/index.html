Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about heating and cooling systems.

How often should I schedule preventative HVAC maintenance?

It is generally recommended to have your heating and cooling system serviced at least once a year. Ideally, you should schedule a check-up for your air conditioner in the spring and your furnace or heat pump in the fall before peak usage seasons begin.

What are the signs that I need a new AC installation?

Key indicators include an air conditioner that is over 10-15 years old, frequent and costly repairs, rising energy bills, inconsistent cooling across different rooms, and unusual noises or odors coming from the unit. Upgrading to a modern system often improves efficiency significantly.

Do you provide information on both furnaces and heat pumps?

Yes, our platform covers comprehensive heating solutions. Whether you rely on a traditional gas furnace for harsh winters or prefer the energy efficiency of a heat pump that provides both heating and cooling, we highlight services suited for all types of equipment.

How can I improve my home's indoor air quality?

Improving air quality starts with regular HVAC maintenance, including changing air filters every 1-3 months. Additionally, ensuring your ductwork is clean and considering add-ons like whole-home humidifiers or UV air purifiers can significantly enhance the air you breathe.

Why is my heating and cooling system making strange noises?

Unusual sounds such as banging, whistling, or grinding usually indicate a mechanical issue, such as a loose belt, failing motor bearing, or airflow obstruction. It is important to turn off the system and request a professional inspection to prevent further damage.
