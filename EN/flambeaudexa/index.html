Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a wedding planner and a day of coordinator?

A full-service wedding planner walks with you from day one, helping design the theme, negotiate vendor contracts, manage the budget, and arrange spatial elements. A day of coordinator (or wedding coordinator) typically steps in 4 to 6 weeks before the wedding to assume control of finalized logistics, run rehearsals, and manage the live timeline on-site.

How far in advance should we book our wedding planning services?

We highly recommend securing your wedding planner 9 to 12 months prior to your target wedding date. This ensures maximum vendor availability, ample venue options, and a structured, low-stress planning timeline.

Do you specialize in destination wedding events?

Yes, absolutely! We coordinate destination wedding events globally, handling multi-day itinerary design, overseas travel coordination for your guests, and seamless collaborations with vetted international vendors.

Can you work with a venue that already has an on-site coordinator?

Yes. A venue coordinator works directly for the venue to oversee in-house catering, staff, and physical building logistics. Your personal wedding coordinator works exclusively for you to manage external vendors, run your ceremony rehearsal, design your custom timeline, and handle intimate personal details.
