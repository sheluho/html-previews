Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about tire replacement, storage, and maintenance.

How does the seasonal tire storage process work?

When it's time for a seasonal changeover, the service provider removes your current tires and replaces them with your off-season set. Your removed tires are cleaned, cataloged, and kept in a secure, climate-controlled warehouse to prevent rubber degradation until you need them again.

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

Industry standards recommend a tire rotation every 5,000 to 8,000 miles. Regular rotation ensures even tread wear, extends the overall lifespan of your tires, and maintains optimal traction and handling.

What is included in a standard tire installation?

A standard tire installation includes removing the old tires, mounting the new tires onto the wheels, computerized wheel balancing to prevent vibrations, installing new valve stems (if applicable), and correctly torquing the lug nuts to factory specifications.

Why is wheel alignment important after tire replacement?

A proper wheel alignment ensures your vehicle's suspension is angled correctly so the tires make optimal contact with the road. Skipping an alignment after a tire replacement can lead to rapid, uneven wear, potentially ruining your new tires prematurely.
