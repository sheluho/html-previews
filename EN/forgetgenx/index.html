Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into our wedding planning process.

What is the difference between a wedding planner and a day of coordinator?

A full-service wedding planner works with you from the very beginning, handling budget creation, venue scouting, vendor hiring, and event design. A day of coordinator typically steps in 4 to 6 weeks before the wedding to finalize timelines, communicate with your already-hired vendors, and execute the logistics on the wedding day itself.

Do you offer packages for a destination wedding?

Yes, our destination wedding services are comprehensive. We manage travel logistics, coordinate with international vendors, and ensure that local cultural nuances are respected and seamlessly integrated into your celebration, allowing you to enjoy a flawless event anywhere in the world.

When should we hire our wedding coordinator?

If you are looking for a full-service wedding planning company, we recommend securing our services 12 to 18 months prior to your desired date. If you only require a day of coordinator, reaching out 6 to 8 months in advance ensures availability for your date.

Will you be the only point of contact for our vendors?

Absolutely. Once our coordination timeline begins, your wedding coordinator becomes the primary liaison for all hired professionals. We field their questions, confirm arrival times, and distribute the master timeline so you aren't overwhelmed with logistics leading up to the big day.

Can you help us stay within our established budget?

Yes, budget management is a core component of our full-service wedding planning offering. We provide realistic cost projections, match you with vendors that align with your financial parameters, and track deposits and final balances to ensure there are no surprises.
