Frequently Asked Questions

How long does residential solar installation typically take? ▼

Typically, the physical installation of solar panels takes 1 to 3 days, depending on the size of the system and the complexity of your roof architecture. Permitting and inspections may add extra time to the overall project timeline.

Will solar panels completely eliminate my electric bill? ▼

While solar power significantly reduces your energy costs, most homes remain connected to the local utility grid. This means you may still incur a small, minimal connection fee, even if your solar panels generate enough electricity to cover your usage.

Are modern photovoltaic systems durable? ▼

Yes, modern solar panels are exceptionally robust. They are meticulously engineered and tested to withstand severe weather events, including heavy snow, high winds, and hail. Most reputable installations come with warranties lasting 25 years.

Do I need battery storage for my residential solar? ▼

Batteries are entirely optional but highly recommended if you want to store excess solar power. Having a backup battery allows you to utilize stored clean energy during nighttime hours or in the event of local grid outages.

How do I maintain my solar panels? ▼

Solar panels generally require very minimal maintenance. Because they have no moving parts, an occasional annual cleaning to remove accumulated dust, leaves, and debris is usually sufficient to maintain optimal energy production.
