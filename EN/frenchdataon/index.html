Frequently Asked Questions

Information regarding residential moving processes and preparing for your transition.

How far in advance should I book a moving company?
For optimal scheduling, it is recommended to book your moving company at least 4 to 6 weeks prior to your intended move date. During peak seasons, such as summer months, securing local movers or long distance movers even earlier is highly advisable to ensure availability.
What is included in professional packing services?
Comprehensive packing services typically include the provision of all necessary materials (boxes, tape, bubble wrap, packing paper), the meticulous wrapping of fragile items, careful boxing of household goods, and clear labeling of boxes by room. This ensures a highly organized unpacking process at your new destination.
Is there a difference in planning for local movers versus long distance movers?
Yes. Local movers generally complete the job within a single day and charge based on an hourly rate and crew size. Long distance movers require complex logistical planning, adherence to interstate transport regulations, and typically charge based on the total weight of the inventory and the mileage traveled.
Do I need to empty my dresser drawers before moving?
For most local residential moving company guidelines, lightweight clothing can remain in sturdy dressers. However, for long distance moves, or if the furniture is particularly heavy or fragile, it is generally safer to pack all contents separately to prevent damage to the furniture frame during transit.
Are there items that a moving company cannot transport?
Yes, for safety and legal reasons, movers cannot transport hazardous materials, combustible items (like propane tanks or certain cleaning chemicals), perishables, and living plants over long distances. It is advised to transport highly valuable personal items, such as jewelry and vital documents, yourself.
