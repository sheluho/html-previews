Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a windshield replacement take?

A standard windshield replacement usually takes about 1 to 2 hours. However, the exact time can vary depending on your vehicle's make and model. You also need to factor in the safe drive-away time required for the urethane adhesive to properly cure.

Can you perform chip repair on any size crack?

Generally, chip repair is effective for damage smaller than a quarter in diameter. If a crack is longer than a few inches or is located directly in the driver's line of sight, a full windshield replacement is usually recommended for safety reasons.

Do I need to replace my auto glass or just repair it?

The decision between repair and replacement depends on the size, depth, and location of the damage. Small chips and minor starbursts can typically be repaired, while deep cracks or damage near the edges of the glass compromise structural integrity and require full replacement.

Is it safe to drive immediately after a car window replacement?

No, it is not safe to drive immediately. Technicians use specialized adhesives that require time to cure. The safe drive-away time is typically at least one hour after the installation is complete, ensuring the glass stays firmly in place in the event of a collision.

Does insurance cover windshield repair?

Many comprehensive auto insurance policies cover windshield repair completely, often waiving the deductible to encourage you to fix small chips before they spread. Replacement may require paying your deductible, depending on your specific policy terms.
