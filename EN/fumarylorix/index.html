Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions regarding HVAC maintenance and installation.

How often should I schedule HVAC maintenance?

It is highly recommended to have your heating and cooling systems inspected at least twice a year. Schedule an AC tune-up in the spring before the summer heat arrives, and a furnace inspection in the fall to prepare for winter weather. Regular maintenance ensures efficiency and prevents costly breakdowns.

What are the signs I need a new air conditioner?

If your AC unit is over 10-15 years old, requires frequent repairs, struggles to maintain a consistent temperature, or if you notice a sudden, unexplained spike in your energy bills, it might be time to consider a full AC installation. Newer models offer significantly better energy efficiency.

Is a heat pump better than a traditional furnace?

A heat pump is an excellent, energy-efficient alternative that provides both heating and cooling from a single unit. While they are highly efficient in moderate climates, homes in areas with extreme, prolonged freezing temperatures may still benefit from pairing a heat pump with a supplemental furnace (a dual-fuel system).

How long does a typical AC installation take?

Most standard residential AC installations can be completed within a single day, typically taking between 4 to 8 hours. However, if the installation requires significant modifications to your existing ductwork or electrical systems, the process may take longer.

What is included in a standard heating and cooling service?

Standard service calls typically include a thorough inspection of your HVAC unit, checking refrigerant levels, cleaning internal coils, inspecting ductwork for leaks, testing the thermostat, and replacing dirty air filters. The technician will ensure the system is operating safely and efficiently.
