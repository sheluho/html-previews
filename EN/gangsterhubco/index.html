Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in a standard tire installation? ▼

A standard tire installation includes the removal of your old tires, mounting the new tires onto your rims, computerized balancing to prevent vibrations, and installing new valve stems. We ensure everything is torqued to your vehicle manufacturer's specifications.

How often do I need a tire rotation? ▼

We generally recommend a tire rotation every 5,000 to 7,000 miles, or at the same time as your oil change. Regular rotation ensures even tread wear, extending the life of your tires and maintaining optimal traction and safety.

How does your seasonal tire storage service work? ▼

When you come in for a seasonal swap, we take your off-season set, wash them, document their tread depth, and place them in a secure, climate-controlled warehouse. When the season changes again, simply book an appointment, and your tires will be ready and waiting at the shop.

Is a wheel alignment necessary after tire replacement? ▼

While not always strictly mandatory, a wheel alignment is highly recommended whenever you undergo a tire replacement. If your vehicle's suspension is out of alignment, it can rapidly cause uneven wear on your brand-new tires, reducing their lifespan significantly.

What are the signs that I need an immediate tire replacement? ▼

Key indicators include tread depth falling below 2/32 of an inch, visible cracks or cuts in the sidewall, bulges or blisters on the tire surface, or a noticeable increase in vibration while driving. If you notice any of these, schedule an inspection immediately.
