Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries regarding auto glass and windshield repair.

How long does a typical windshield replacement take?

Most standard windshield replacement services take about 60 to 90 minutes to complete. However, the advanced adhesives used require additional cure time before the vehicle is safe to drive, which usually adds another hour to the process.

Can you fix a rock chip, or do I need a full replacement?

If the rock chip is smaller than a quarter and is not located directly in the driver's line of sight, a simple chip repair is usually highly effective. If the damage consists of long cracks or multiple impact points, a full windshield replacement is required for safety.

Do you handle side and rear car window replacement?

Yes, the professionals we promote handle all types of auto glass. Car window replacement for side doors, quarter glass, and rear windshields includes thorough cleaning of the shattered glass and precise installation of the new pane.

Will my insurance cover the windshield repair?

Many comprehensive auto insurance policies cover auto glass and windshield repair with little to no out-of-pocket cost to you. The service providers typically assist in filing the claim directly with your insurance network.

Is ADAS recalibration necessary after auto glass replacement?

Yes. If your vehicle is equipped with Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (like lane departure warnings or automated braking) mounted to the windshield, recalibration is essential following a windshield replacement to ensure the cameras and sensors function accurately.
