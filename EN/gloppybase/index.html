Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about custom blinds, shades, and window treatments.

What is the difference between custom blinds and shades?
Blinds are typically made of hard materials like wood, faux wood, or aluminum and feature individual slats that can be tilted to control light. Shades are constructed from continuous panels of fabric (like roller shades or cellular shades) and are raised or lowered to adjust light and privacy.
Do plantation shutters increase home value?
Yes, plantation shutters are widely considered a permanent architectural fixture rather than just a window covering. Because they are custom-fitted to the window frames and highly durable, they often increase the resale value and curb appeal of a home.
Are roller shades suitable for bedrooms?
Absolutely. Roller shades are an excellent choice for bedrooms, especially when equipped with blackout fabrics. They provide a clean, uncluttered look while effectively blocking out morning sunlight and streetlights for a better night's sleep.
How do I maintain and clean my new window treatments?
Maintenance depends on the material. Wood blinds and plantation shutters should be dusted regularly with a soft cloth; avoid excessive moisture. Faux wood and aluminum blinds can be wiped with a lightly damp cloth. Fabric shades and custom drapes typically require gentle vacuuming with a brush attachment or professional dry cleaning.
Can I combine custom drapes with other window coverings?
Yes, layering window treatments is a highly popular design choice. Pairing custom drapes over roller shades or wood blinds adds depth, texture, and improved insulation to a room. This approach allows you to filter light during the day with the blinds or shades while using the drapes for enhanced privacy and warmth at night.
