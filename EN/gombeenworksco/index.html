Common Inquiries

Is residential solar a good investment for an urban home?

Yes, installing solar panels significantly reduces your monthly utility bills and increases your property value. Most homeowners see a return on investment within a few years thanks to compounding energy savings and available local or federal tax incentives designed for renewable energy adoption.

How long does a typical solar installation take?

The physical installation of photovoltaic panels on your roof usually takes 1 to 3 days. However, the entire process—including initial site assessment, custom engineering design, acquiring necessary city permits, and final utility interconnection—typically spans a few months from start to finish.

Do solar panels still generate power on cloudy days?

Yes, your residential solar system will still generate electricity on cloudy or overcast days. While production operates at a reduced capacity compared to direct, unshaded sunlight, modern photovoltaic technology is highly efficient at capturing diffused light to keep your home powered.

What maintenance does a solar power system require?

Solar panels have no moving parts and require very little maintenance. Occasional cleaning to remove heavy dust, leaves, or debris, paired with an annual professional inspection, is usually sufficient to keep your system running optimally for decades.

What happens to my solar energy during a grid power outage?

Standard grid-tied solar systems automatically shut down during a municipal power outage for the safety of utility workers repairing the lines. If you desire continuous backup power during outages, we strongly recommend integrating a battery storage system into your initial installation plan.
