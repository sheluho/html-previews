Frequently Asked Questions

How frequently should I schedule lawn mowing?

During the peak growing seasons in spring and summer, weekly lawn mowing is generally recommended to keep grass healthy and prevent weeds. In slower growing periods, a bi-weekly schedule may suffice. Consistent lawn care ensures your grass establishes strong, deep roots.

What does a standard landscape installation process look like?

A landscape installation begins with an initial consultation and garden design phase. Once the design is approved, the team prepares the site, installs any hardscaping elements, prepares the soil, and plants your selected vegetation. Finally, mulch and irrigation are added to support the new plants.

Do you handle complex tree service and removal?

Yes, our featured partners are fully equipped for professional tree service. This includes routine pruning for tree health, safe removal of dead or hazardous trees, and stump grinding. Safety and property protection are the highest priorities during these operations.

Can garden design be tailored for low-maintenance properties?

Absolutely. If you prefer minimal upkeep, the garden design can focus on native plant species, drought-resistant shrubs, and strategic hardscaping. These choices significantly reduce the amount of ongoing lawn care and watering required while maintaining a beautiful aesthetic.

When is the best time to start a landscaping project?

While general lawn care continues year-round, early spring or early fall are the optimal times for major landscape installation or planting. The mild temperatures help new roots establish themselves before the stress of extreme summer heat or winter freezes.
