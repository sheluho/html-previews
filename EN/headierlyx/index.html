Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I get a tire rotation?

It is generally recommended to have your tire rotation performed every 5,000 to 7,500 miles. Regular rotation ensures even tread wear, extending the life of your tires and maintaining consistent handling.

Do you offer seasonal tire storage?

Yes, we provide secure, climate-monitored tire storage. This service frees up space in your garage and ensures your off-season tires are kept in optimal conditions to prevent rubber degradation.

What is included in a standard tire installation?

Our tire installation includes removing the old tires, mounting the new ones, computerized wheel balancing, installing new valve stems (if applicable), and safely torquing the lug nuts to the manufacturer's specifications.

How do I know if I need a wheel alignment?

Signs that you need a wheel alignment include your vehicle pulling to one side while driving straight, an off-center steering wheel, or uneven and rapid tire wear. We recommend checking alignment whenever you get a tire replacement.

When is it time for a tire replacement?

You should consider a tire replacement when the tread depth falls below 2/32 of an inch, if you notice visible damage like bulges or deep cracks, or if the tires are more than six years old regardless of tread depth.
