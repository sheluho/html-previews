Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about residential solar power and our promotional services.

Are solar panels worth it for a residential city roof?

Absolutely. Even in dense urban environments, residential solar panels can generate substantial clean energy. Custom installations like elevated canopies ensure your photovoltaic system captures maximum sunlight without sacrificing functional roof space, leading to significant long-term savings.

How long does a typical solar installation take?

While the physical installation of the solar panels typically takes just a few days, the entire process—including consultation, custom design, permitting, and grid connection—can take several weeks to a few months. The installers handle the entire bureaucratic process to ensure a smooth timeline.

Do I need a battery backup system?

A battery backup is not strictly required, but it is highly recommended. It allows you to store excess solar power generated during the day to use at night or during grid outages, providing energy independence and resilience for your home.

What happens if my roof needs repairs before installation?

Installers perform a thorough structural assessment prior to fitting your residential solar system. If your roof requires maintenance or structural reinforcement to support the photovoltaic panels safely, these requirements will be identified and addressed during the planning phase.

What is Hinaugen's role in this process?

Hinaugen acts as an independent promotional operator. We help educate homeowners about the benefits of residential solar and connect you with leading providers like Brooklyn SolarWorks, who handle the actual sales, technical design, permitting, and solar installation.
