Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about working with a whole home remodeling expert.

What exactly does a turnkey renovation include?

A turnkey renovation means we handle the entire project from start to finish. This includes architectural design, acquiring permits, demolition, framing, plumbing, electrical work, and all final interior finishes. You simply turn the key and walk into a completed home.

Do you handle large-scale home additions?

Yes. Whether you are looking to build a second story, expand your kitchen footprint outward, or add an attached in-law suite, we have the engineering and construction expertise to integrate the new space flawlessly with your existing structure.

How long does whole home remodeling typically take?

Timelines vary greatly based on the scope of work. A moderate interior remodel might take 3 to 4 months, while a major project involving structural changes and home additions can take 6 to 9 months or more. We provide a detailed schedule during the planning phase.

Why should I hire a general contractor instead of managing trades myself?

Acting as your own general contractor requires immense time, industry knowledge, and connections to reliable subcontractors. We bring established relationships, volume purchasing power, and the experience needed to foresee and solve complex structural issues before they cause costly delays.

Do I need to move out during the renovation?

For a true whole home remodeling project, we highly recommend making alternative living arrangements. Major renovations involve shutting off utilities, extensive dust, and significant noise. Moving out ensures your safety and allows our crews to work more efficiently to complete your project faster.
