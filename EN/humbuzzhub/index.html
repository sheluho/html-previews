Frequently Asked Questions

What is a ceramic coating and how does it work? ▼
A ceramic coating is a liquid polymer applied to your vehicle's exterior. It chemically bonds with the factory paint, creating a semi-permanent layer of protection. This paint protection shields against UV damage, chemical stains, and makes cleaning incredibly easy due to its hydrophobic properties.
How often should I get professional auto detailing? ▼
For optimal maintenance, it is recommended to schedule a thorough auto detailing session every four to six months. Regular car detailing keeps your clear coat healthy and ensures your interior detailing remains fresh, preventing long-term wear and tear on your upholstery and dashboard.
Does ceramic coating replace the need for wax? ▼
Yes, absolutely. Once a high-quality ceramic coating is applied, you no longer need to wax your vehicle. The coating provides a much more durable and long-lasting shine than traditional carnauba wax or synthetic sealants, protecting your car for years rather than just a few months.
What is included in interior detailing? ▼
Comprehensive interior detailing goes far beyond a simple vacuum. It includes deep extraction of carpets and cloth seats, conditioning of leather surfaces, cleaning of all interior glass and mirrors, and meticulous dusting of vents and small crevices. The goal is to restore the cabin to a like-new condition.
Can paint protection fix existing scratches? ▼
Paint protection and ceramic coatings themselves do not fix scratches. However, the professional auto detailing process usually involves paint correction beforehand. This essential step removes swirl marks and light scratches so that the surface is completely flawless before the protective layer is permanently applied.
