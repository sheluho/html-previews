Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical windshield replacement take?
A standard windshield replacement usually takes about one to two hours to complete. However, the exact time can vary depending on the make and model of your vehicle. The technicians we connect you with work efficiently to minimize your downtime without sacrificing the quality of the installation.
Can my auto glass be repaired instead of fully replaced?
Often, yes. If the damage is a minor chip smaller than a quarter, or a crack shorter than a few inches, a windshield repair is usually a viable option. Chip repair restores the structural integrity of the glass and prevents the damage from spreading, saving you the expense of a full replacement.
Do you promote car window replacement for side and rear glass?
Absolutely. We provide information and connection options for comprehensive car window replacement. Whether your side door glass, vent glass, or rear window has been damaged or shattered, the professionals we partner with can source and install the correct, high-quality replacement glass for your specific vehicle.
How soon can I safely drive my car after the glass is replaced?
Following a windshield replacement, a specific "safe drive-away time" is required to allow the specialized adhesives to cure properly. This usually ranges from one to several hours depending on humidity and temperature. The technician will provide precise instructions to ensure your safety before you get back on the road.
Will a chip repair completely erase the mark on my windshield?
While chip repair significantly improves the appearance of the damaged area, it rarely makes it 100% invisible. The primary goal of a windshield repair is to restore structural strength and prevent the crack from spreading further. Most repairs leave only a very faint blemish that does not interfere with your line of sight.
