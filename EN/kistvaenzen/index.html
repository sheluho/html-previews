Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into the remodeling process.

What does a "turnkey renovation" actually mean?

A turnkey renovation implies that the general contractor handles every single aspect of the project from initial design and permitting to the final coat of paint and debris removal. You do not need to hire separate architects, plumbers, or electricians. The firm manages the entire workflow, delivering a completed, ready-to-use space.

How long does a whole home remodeling project typically take?

Timelines vary significantly based on the scope of work, structural changes required, and material availability. A comprehensive whole home remodeling project can take anywhere from 4 to 12 months. Accurate timelines are established during the pre-construction planning phase.

Do I need architectural plans before contacting a general contractor?

Not if you choose a design-build firm. The partners we highlight typically offer in-house design services. They will work with you to create the architectural plans, select materials, and execute the build, ensuring the design aligns perfectly with your budget from day one.

Are permits handled by the homeowner or the contractor?

A reputable general contractor will manage all necessary local building permits, zoning approvals, and inspections required for your home addition or renovation. This ensures all work complies with local building codes and regulations.

Can I live in my house during a major home renovation?

It depends entirely on the scope of the project. For isolated kitchen or bathroom updates, homeowners often remain in the residence. However, for extensive whole home remodeling or major structural additions, it is usually safer and more efficient for the family to relocate temporarily. This will be discussed during your initial consultation.
