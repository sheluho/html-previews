Frequently Asked Questions

How long do residential solar panels typically last?

Modern photovoltaic systems are incredibly durable. Most high-quality solar panels are designed to efficiently generate solar power for 25 to 30 years, often continuing to produce energy well beyond their standard warranty period.

What exactly is a photovoltaic system?

A photovoltaic (PV) system is the technical term for a solar power setup. It utilizes specialized solar panels to capture sunlight and convert it directly into usable electricity for your home, reducing your dependence on the traditional utility grid.

How does the solar installation process work?

The process begins with a comprehensive site assessment to evaluate your roof's condition and sun exposure. From there, a custom residential solar design is created. Once permits are secured, the physical solar installation typically takes just one to three days, followed by utility inspections and final grid connection.

Do I need a battery with my solar power system?

While not strictly mandatory, adding a battery backup to your solar panels is highly recommended. It allows you to store excess solar power generated during the day for use at night or during grid outages, maximizing your energy independence.

Will residential solar increase my property value?

Yes. Numerous real estate studies show that homes equipped with owned (not leased) residential solar panel systems tend to sell faster and for a premium compared to non-solar homes, as buyers highly value built-in energy efficiency and lower utility costs.
