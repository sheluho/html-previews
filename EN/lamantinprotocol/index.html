Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about our whole home renovation process.

What exactly is considered a whole home remodel?

A whole home remodel is a comprehensive overhaul of your property, typically involving changes to the floor plan, significant structural updates, and the renovation of multiple major rooms (such as kitchens, bathrooms, and living areas) simultaneously. It aims to unify the aesthetic and functional flow of the entire house.

How long does a turnkey renovation usually take?

Timelines vary greatly depending on the scope of work, permitting processes, and whether structural changes are required. Typically, a large-scale turnkey renovation can take anywhere from 4 to 8 months from the start of demolition to final walkthrough. We provide detailed timeline estimates during the planning phase.

Do you handle the architectural design as well as construction?

Yes, as a comprehensive general contractor, we manage the entire design-build process. Our architectural design team collaborates with you initially to finalize the blueprints, and then our construction crews execute that precise vision, ensuring consistency and accountability throughout.

Can you incorporate a home addition into the remodel?

Absolutely. Expanding your home's footprint is a very common component of our projects. Whether it's extending the kitchen, adding a master suite, or building a second story, we possess the engineering and construction expertise to integrate the new addition seamlessly with your existing structure.

Do I need to move out during a whole home renovation?

For extensive structural changes and whole home projects, we highly recommend relocating for the duration of the heavy construction. It ensures your safety, protects you from dust and noise, and allows our teams to work much faster and more efficiently without having to maneuver around occupied living spaces.
