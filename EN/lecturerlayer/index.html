Frequently Asked Questions

Clarity on our process and offerings.

What is the difference between full planning and a day of coordinator?

A full-service wedding planner is involved from the very beginning, handling budget creation, venue scouting, vendor hiring, and overall design conceptualization. A day of coordinator typically steps in 4-6 weeks before the wedding to take over the plans you've already made, finalize the timeline, and execute the event on the actual day.

Do you handle destination wedding planning?

Yes, we specialize in destination wedding design and management. We assist with securing international or out-of-state venues, managing travel logistics for you and key guests, and sourcing reliable local vendors to ensure your remote celebration meets our high standards of quality.

When should we hire a wedding planner?

Ideally, you should hire a full-service planner right after you get engaged and before you select a venue or set a date. This allows us to guide your budget and vendor choices from a clean slate. For coordination services, booking 6-12 months in advance secures your date on our calendar.

Will my wedding coordinator be there for the rehearsal?

Yes. Rehearsal coordination is a standard inclusion in our coordination packages. We direct the processional, recessional, and ensure the wedding party understands their roles and timing, ensuring everyone feels confident before the actual ceremony.

How do we start the wedding planning process with you?

The process begins with an initial consultation where we discuss your vision, scale, and specific needs. From there, we propose a customized service scope. Once agreed upon, we begin the structured onboarding phase, establishing communication channels and building your initial design profile.
