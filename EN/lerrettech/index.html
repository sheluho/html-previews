Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about transitioning your home to solar power.

How long does a residential solar panel installation take?
The physical installation of solar panels on your roof typically takes just 1 to 3 days, depending on the size and complexity of the system. However, the entire process—including custom design, engineering approvals, and securing necessary local permits—can take a few weeks before the actual installation day.
Will solar panels completely eliminate my electric bill?
While a properly sized photovoltaic system can significantly reduce or even eliminate your monthly energy costs, you may still receive a small bill for grid connection fees. If you produce more solar power than you consume, you may also earn credits depending on your local net metering policies.
Do solar panels work during cloudy days or winter?
Yes, solar panels still generate electricity on cloudy or overcast days, though at a reduced output compared to direct sunlight. Modern residential solar panels are highly efficient and continue to produce usable power even during the winter months, provided they are not completely covered by heavy snow.
What happens to my solar power during a grid outage?
Standard grid-tied solar systems automatically shut off during a power outage for safety reasons. However, if you invest in a battery backup solution alongside your solar installation, your home can continue to run on stored solar energy seamlessly while the grid is down.
Is my roof suitable for a solar installation?
Most roofs are suitable, but the ideal roof has a south-facing orientation with minimal shading from trees or nearby buildings. Before any installation begins, the provider will conduct a thorough structural and shading analysis to ensure your roof can support the panels and generate optimal solar power.
