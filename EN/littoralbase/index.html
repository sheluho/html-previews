Common Questions

Insights into designing your perfect custom closets.

What is the difference between a walk-in closet and a reach-in closet?

A walk-in closet provides enough physical space for a person to step inside and often features storage on multiple walls, making it ideal for master bedrooms. A reach-in closet is typically shallower; you stand outside it and reach in to access your items. Both can be dramatically improved with a dedicated closet organizer system.

Can you design a wardrobe for a room without existing closets?

Absolutely. A custom wardrobe is an excellent solution for rooms lacking built-in storage. These freestanding or wall-mounted units can be customized with hanging rods, shelves, and drawers to replicate the functionality of traditional custom closets while complementing the room's decor.

What elements are included in a standard closet organizer?

A premium closet organizer typically includes a combination of adjustable shelving, varying heights of hanging rods for long and short garments, soft-close drawers, and specialized accessories like shoe racks, belt hooks, and jewelry trays to ensure everything has a designated place.

How does the design process work?

The process begins with a thorough consultation and measurement of your space. Designers assess your specific storage needs, inventory your wardrobe, and then create a 3D model of your proposed custom closets. Once approved, the materials are crafted and professionally installed.

Are your custom closets suitable for spaces other than bedrooms?

Yes. While we often focus on the master walk-in closet or bedroom reach-in closet, organizational systems can be adapted for pantries, mudrooms, home offices, and garages. A good closet organizer system brings efficiency to any area of the home.
