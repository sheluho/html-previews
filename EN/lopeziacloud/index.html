Frequently Asked Questions

How does residential solar installation impact home value?

Installing solar panels generally increases a home's market value. Buyers increasingly look for energy-efficient homes equipped with modern photovoltaic technology, recognizing the long-term savings on utility bills.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes, solar panels still generate electricity on cloudy days, though their output is reduced compared to days with direct, bright sunlight. Modern residential solar systems are highly efficient and can capture ambient light to continue producing power.

How long does the solar installation process take?

While the actual physical installation of solar panels on your roof typically takes just 1-3 days, the overall process—including design, permitting, and grid connection approvals—can take several weeks to a few months depending on local regulations.

Do I need a battery storage system?

While not strictly necessary, a battery storage system is highly recommended. It allows you to store the excess solar power your panels generate during the day for use at night or during power outages, maximizing your energy independence.

What kind of maintenance do solar panels require?

Residential solar panels require very little maintenance. Because there are no moving parts, they are highly durable. Occasional cleaning to remove dust or debris and an annual professional inspection are usually sufficient to keep them running at peak efficiency.
