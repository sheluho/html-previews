Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about our tire management insights.

Why is professional tire storage better than keeping them in my garage?

Garages often experience extreme temperature fluctuations and varying humidity levels, which can cause the rubber compounds in tires to degrade, dry rot, or crack over time. Professional tire storage facilities maintain a climate-controlled environment, shielding your tires from UV light and harsh conditions, thereby extending their usable lifespan.

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

Industry standards generally recommend scheduling a tire rotation every 5,000 to 8,000 miles, or roughly every time you have your oil changed. Regular rotation ensures that all four tires wear evenly, which improves overall vehicle handling and maximizes the mileage you get out of a single set before needing a full tire replacement.

What indicates that I need a wheel alignment?

Signs that your vehicle requires a wheel alignment include the car pulling to one side while driving straight, the steering wheel being off-center when driving on a straight road, or noticeable uneven or rapid wear on your tire treads. Proper alignment is crucial after any new tire installation to protect your new investment.

Does the installation process include balancing?

Yes, a comprehensive tire installation service should always include computerized spin balancing. Balancing ensures that the weight of the tire and wheel assembly is distributed evenly, which prevents vibrations at higher speeds and reduces premature wear on your suspension system.

Can I transition directly from storage to tire replacement?

Absolutely. If the technicians pull your seasonal set from tire storage and determine that the tread depth is below safe limits, they will recommend a complete tire replacement rather than re-installing unsafe rubber. This ensures you only drive on tires that provide adequate traction for the season.
