Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about tire care and our promotional services.

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

Industry standards generally recommend a tire rotation every 5,000 to 8,000 miles, or roughly every time you get an oil change. Regular rotation ensures even tread wear, which significantly extends the life of your tires and maintains optimal handling.

What is included in a standard tire installation?

A professional tire installation typically includes removing the old tires, mounting the new tires onto your existing wheels, computerized spin balancing to prevent vibrations, installing new valve stems (if applicable), and securing the wheels back onto your vehicle with the proper torque specifications.

Why should I use a dedicated tire storage service?

Proper tire storage requires a cool, dry, and dark environment to prevent the rubber compounds from degrading prematurely. Dedicated facilities offer climate-controlled spaces that protect your off-season tires from extreme temperatures, moisture, and UV light, ensuring they are ready for action when the seasons change.

How do I know if I need a wheel alignment?

Signs that you may need a wheel alignment include your vehicle pulling to one side while driving straight, uneven or rapid tire wear, a steering wheel that is crooked when driving straight, or squealing tires. It is highly recommended to check alignment during a new tire replacement.
