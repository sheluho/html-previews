Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about whole home remodeling and structural updates.

What is a turnkey renovation?

A turnkey renovation means the general contractor handles every single aspect of your project from start to finish. This includes architectural design, pulling permits, sourcing materials, construction, and final finishing. You simply "turn the key" to enter your completed, ready-to-live-in home.

How long does a home addition typically take?

The timeline for a home addition varies heavily based on the scope, scale, and local permitting processes. Generally, a significant addition can take anywhere from 3 to 6 months of active construction, preceded by several months of design and approval phases.

Do I need to hire an architect separately?

In many whole home remodeling projects, the general contractor provides design-build services, meaning architectural and engineering work is managed in-house or through their dedicated partners. This ensures continuity and often saves time and communication errors.

Can I live in my house during a whole home remodeling project?

It depends on the extent of the work. For extensive renovations involving major plumbing, electrical, and structural changes across multiple rooms, it is often safer, faster, and more practical for homeowners to relocate temporarily. Your contractor will advise on the best approach during the planning phase.

What areas do you serve?
