Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

It is generally recommended to rotate your tires every 5,000 to 8,000 miles, or every time you get an oil change. Regular tire rotation ensures even tread wear, extending the lifespan of the tires and improving overall vehicle safety.

What does your tire storage service include?

The tire storage service provides a secure, climate-controlled environment for your off-season tires. This protects the rubber compounds from extreme temperature fluctuations, UV exposure, and moisture, preventing premature aging and dry rot.

How do I know when I need a tire replacement?

You should consider a tire replacement if your tread depth falls below 2/32 of an inch, if you notice visible damage like sidewall cracks, bulges, or punctures, or if the tires are more than six years old regardless of tread wear.

Do you recommend a wheel alignment with new tires?

Yes, a wheel alignment is highly recommended when installing new tires. Proper alignment ensures that the new tires meet the road at the correct angle, preventing rapid, uneven wear and protecting your investment from day one.

Are appointments required for tire installation?

While walk-ins may be accommodated depending on the daily schedule of the service center, booking an appointment for tire installation is strongly advised. It guarantees dedicated time for your vehicle and reduces waiting periods significantly.
