Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have my heating and cooling system serviced?

It is generally recommended to service your HVAC system twice a year. You should schedule an air conditioner tune-up in the spring and a furnace inspection in the fall to ensure optimal performance and prevent unexpected breakdowns.

What are the signs that I need a new AC installation?

If your current unit is over 10-15 years old, requires frequent and costly repairs, fails to cool your home evenly, or causes a sudden spike in your energy bills, it might be time to consult with an HVAC professional about a new, energy-efficient AC installation.

What is a heat pump and how does it work?

A heat pump is a versatile HVAC system that provides both heating and cooling. During the summer, it acts like an air conditioner by pulling heat out of your home. In the winter, it reverses the process, extracting ambient heat from the outside air and moving it indoors.

Can regular maintenance really lower my energy bills?

Absolutely. When your heating and cooling equipment is dirty or poorly calibrated, it has to work much harder to maintain your desired temperature. Routine maintenance cleans critical components, ensuring the system runs efficiently and uses less electricity or gas.

Do you handle emergency HVAC repairs?

Yes, through our promotional network, you can connect with technicians who offer prompt emergency repair services. Extreme temperatures can be dangerous, and our partners strive to restore your home's comfort as quickly as possible.
