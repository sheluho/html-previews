Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about auto glass and windshield replacement.

How do I know if I need windshield repair or a full replacement?
Generally, if a chip or crack is smaller than a quarter and not located directly in the driver's line of sight, a simple chip repair is sufficient. If the crack is larger, near the edge of the glass, or deep, a complete windshield replacement is required to maintain your vehicle's safety standards.
How long does a windshield replacement take?
A standard windshield replacement typically takes about 60 to 90 minutes to complete. However, you must also allow the specialized adhesive to cure for an additional hour before the vehicle is safe to drive away.
Do you handle side and rear car window replacement?
Yes, the professionals we promote handle complete car window replacement for all types of vehicle glass, including side door windows, quarter glass, and rear windshields. They ensure all shattered glass is safely removed from the interior as well.
Will my insurance cover the cost of auto glass repair?
In many cases, comprehensive auto insurance policies cover auto glass repair and replacement, sometimes without requiring a deductible for chip repairs. We recommend checking your specific policy, and the technicians can often assist with the insurance claims process.
Can I wash my car immediately after a windshield replacement?
You should wait at least 24 to 48 hours before using a high-pressure car wash. The urethane adhesive used to seal the new auto glass needs adequate time to fully cure and set. However, a gentle hand wash is usually fine after a few hours.
