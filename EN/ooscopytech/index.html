Frequently Asked Questions

Is spray foam better than fiberglass for attic insulation?
Yes, spray foam generally provides superior air sealing compared to traditional fiberglass. It expands to fill microscopic gaps and cracks, creating an airtight barrier that significantly reduces heat loss and improves overall home insulation performance.
What are the main benefits of fiber cement siding?
Fiber cement siding offers exceptional durability. It resists rot, fire, and termite damage far better than traditional wood. Additionally, it provides a premium, natural wood-grain appearance without the heavy ongoing maintenance, making it a robust choice for house siding.
How long does vinyl siding typically last?
High-quality vinyl siding can last anywhere from 20 to 40 years depending on the climate and level of maintenance. It is an extremely cost-effective house siding option that requires minimal upkeep—typically just an occasional wash to maintain its vibrant look.
Can I upgrade house siding and attic insulation at the same time?
Absolutely. Upgrading your exterior siding while simultaneously addressing your attic insulation is a comprehensive approach to securing your home's building envelope. This dual upgrade maximizes energy efficiency and completely transforms your home's structural integrity.
How does home insulation impact my energy bills?
Proper home insulation keeps conditioned air inside and prevents outside air from infiltrating. By reducing the workload on your HVAC system, modern insulation techniques—like targeted spray foam—can drastically lower your monthly heating and cooling costs.
