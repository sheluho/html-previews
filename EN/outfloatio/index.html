Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book a moving company?

For optimal availability, especially during peak summer months, we recommend booking your moving company at least four to six weeks in advance. If you are planning a long-distance relocation, scheduling eight weeks ahead provides ample time for detailed logistical planning.

Do local movers charge by the hour or by weight?

Generally, local movers charge an hourly rate based on the number of crew members and trucks required for your specific job. In contrast, long distance movers typically calculate costs based on the total weight of your belongings and the distance traveled.

What is exactly included in professional packing services?

Professional packing services provide all necessary materials—such as sturdy boxes, bubble wrap, packing paper, and tape. The crew will systematically wrap, pack, and clearly label every item in your home. You can opt for full-house packing or partial services for specific fragile items like kitchenware or artwork.

Are my belongings insured during transit with long distance movers?

Reputable moving companies offer basic liability coverage as required by law, which compensates at a standard rate per pound. For higher-value items, it is highly recommended to discuss full-value protection plans with the provider to ensure comprehensive coverage throughout the journey.

How should I prepare my home before the movers arrive?

Before the crew arrives, ensure all pathways are clear of obstacles. Disconnect major appliances, empty your refrigerator, and set aside personal essentials, important documents, and valuables in a separate area that will travel with you rather than on the moving truck.
