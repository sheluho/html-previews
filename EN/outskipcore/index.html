Common Questions

Vehicle Care FAQ

What exactly is a ceramic coating and why do I need it?

Ceramic coating is a liquid polymer applied to your vehicle's exterior. It chemically bonds with the factory paint, creating a protective layer that is much harder and more durable than traditional wax. It provides excellent paint protection against UV rays, chemical stains, and minor scratches, while making the car much easier to clean due to its hydrophobic properties.

How often should I schedule auto detailing?

For optimal maintenance, we recommend a thorough auto detailing service every 4 to 6 months. However, this depends on how often you drive, where you park, and whether your vehicle already has a protective coating. Regular car detailing prevents dirt buildup and protects your clear coat from degrading.

What is included in deep interior detailing?

Our interior detailing goes far beyond a simple vacuum. It includes deep cleaning and extraction of carpets and upholstery, steam cleaning of vents and crevices, conditioning of leather surfaces, and specialized treatment for all plastics and vinyl to restore color and provide UV protection without a greasy finish.

Do I need paint correction before applying a coating?

Yes, highly recommended. Paint correction removes swirl marks, oxidation, and minor scratches. Because ceramic coating forms a semi-permanent bond over the paint, any imperfections left on the surface will be locked in and magnified. Correcting the paint first ensures the best possible gloss and finish.

How long does the detailing and coating process take?

A standard interior or exterior detailing usually takes a few hours. However, a full paint correction followed by a professional ceramic coating application is a meticulous process that generally requires keeping your vehicle for 1 to 2 days to allow for proper curing time in a controlled environment.
