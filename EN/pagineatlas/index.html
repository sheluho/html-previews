Common Questions

What is the difference between local movers and long distance movers? ▼

Local typically refers to moves within the same state or a specific mileage radius (often under 50-100 miles). Long distance involves crossing state lines or significant mileage. Pricing structures often differ, with local moves usually billed hourly and long distance billed by weight and distance.

Are packing services worth the additional cost? ▼

For many homeowners, professional packing services save valuable time and significantly reduce the risk of damage during transit. The expertise and specialized materials used make them a highly recommended investment for a stress-free move.

How far in advance should I book a moving company? ▼

It is generally advisable to secure your movers at least four to six weeks prior to your intended move date. During peak summer months or end-of-month periods, booking even earlier is recommended to ensure availability.

Do residential movers handle large, specialty items? ▼

Yes, experienced moving teams are trained to handle heavy and delicate items such as pianos, large safes, and antique furniture. It is important to notify the movers in advance about any specialty items so they can prepare the proper equipment.

What happens if my new home isn't ready on moving day? ▼

Many moving companies offer secure, short-term storage solutions. Your belongings can be safely held in a facility until your new residence is ready for delivery. Be sure to ask about storage options when requesting your quote.
