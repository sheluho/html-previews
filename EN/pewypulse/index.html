Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

Generally, a tire rotation is recommended every 5,000 to 8,000 miles, or roughly every time you get an oil change. Regular rotation ensures even tread wear, extending the life of your tires and maintaining consistent handling.

What are the benefits of professional tire storage?

Professional tire storage keeps your off-season tires in a cool, dry, and dark environment. This prevents the rubber compounds from degrading due to UV light, extreme temperature fluctuations, or moisture, ensuring they are perfectly safe and ready for the next season.

How do I know if I need a wheel alignment?

Signs that you need a wheel alignment include your vehicle pulling to one side while driving straight, your steering wheel being off-center, or noticing rapid, uneven wear on your tire treads. It's also standard practice to check alignment during a new tire installation.

Is tire replacement always done in pairs or sets of four?

For optimal safety and vehicle handling, it is highly recommended to replace tires in sets of four, especially for All-Wheel Drive (AWD) vehicles. If replacing only two, the new tires should always be installed on the rear axle to maintain stability in wet conditions.

How long does a standard tire installation take?

A standard tire installation for a set of four tires, which includes mounting, balancing, and replacing valve stems, typically takes about 45 minutes to an hour. Adding a wheel alignment will usually add another 30 to 45 minutes to the service time.
