Frequently Asked Questions

How does residential solar panel installation work? ▼

Installation begins with a site assessment to evaluate your roof and energy needs. Once a custom photovoltaic system is designed, expert technicians secure the panels to your roof, connect the electrical wiring, and integrate the system with your home's power panel and the local grid.

Will solar power eliminate my electric bill entirely? ▼

While residential solar panels can significantly reduce your electricity costs—often by 70% to 100%—you may still receive a small bill for grid connection fees, depending on your local utility company and net metering policies.

What happens during a power outage? ▼

Standard grid-tied solar systems automatically shut off during an outage for safety reasons. However, if you include a battery backup system with your installation, your home will remain powered using the clean energy stored in your batteries.

How long do photovoltaic panels typically last? ▼

High-quality residential solar panels are incredibly durable and typically last 25 to 30 years or more. They require minimal maintenance, usually just occasional cleaning to ensure maximum sunlight absorption.

Are there incentives for switching to solar? ▼

Yes, homeowners can often take advantage of federal tax credits, state-level rebates, and local utility incentives that substantially lower the upfront cost of a residential solar installation. Our partners can help you navigate these options.
