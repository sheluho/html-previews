Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about residential solar panel installation.

How long does a residential solar installation typically take?

Once the permits are approved and the design is finalized, the actual physical solar installation on your roof generally takes only 1 to 3 days, depending on the size and complexity of the photovoltaic system.

Are solar panels effective in cloudy or colder climates?

Yes, modern solar panels still generate significant solar power on cloudy days. Photovoltaic technology relies on light, not heat, so cold, bright days can actually result in highly efficient energy production for residential solar setups.

Will residential solar panels increase my property value?

Numerous studies have shown that homes equipped with owned solar power systems often sell for more than non-solar homes. Buyers appreciate the immediate benefit of reduced utility costs provided by a robust photovoltaic installation.

Do I need a solar battery storage system?

While not strictly mandatory, adding a solar battery ensures that the excess energy your solar panels produce during the day can be used at night or during grid outages, maximizing the independence of your residential solar setup.

How do I maintain my photovoltaic system?

Residential solar panels require very little maintenance. Generally, keeping them clear of heavy debris and occasionally rinsing off dust or pollen is enough to keep your solar power generation running efficiently for decades.
