Frequently Asked Questions

Expert insights into maintaining your heating and cooling systems.

What signs indicate I need a new air conditioner?

If your AC is over 10-15 years old, requires frequent repairs, struggles to cool your home evenly, or causes unexplained spikes in your energy bills, it may be time for a professional AC installation. Modern units offer significantly better efficiency and reliability.

How often should I schedule heating and cooling maintenance?

It is highly recommended to have your HVAC system serviced twice a year. Schedule a tune-up for your air conditioner in the spring and an inspection for your furnace or heat pump in the fall to ensure peak performance before extreme weather hits.

What is the difference between a furnace and a heat pump?

A furnace generates heat by burning fuel (like natural gas) or using electric resistance, making it ideal for extremely cold climates. A heat pump, however, transfers heat between the indoors and outdoors. It acts as an air conditioner in the summer and reverses the process to heat your home in the winter, offering a highly efficient dual solution for moderate climates.

Do the professionals you connect with offer emergency services?

Yes, HVAC emergencies don't wait for business hours. The service providers we promote typically offer rapid response times for critical heating and cooling repair to restore your home's safety and comfort as quickly as possible.

How can I improve my home's indoor air quality?

Beyond regular filter changes, you can enhance air quality by installing whole-home humidifiers or dehumidifiers, integrating UV germicidal lights, and utilizing high-MERV media filters. An HVAC professional can assess your ductwork and recommend the best system upgrades.
