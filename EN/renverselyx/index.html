Common Questions

Everything you need to know about switching your home to solar power.

How long does residential solar panel installation take?

The physical installation on your roof typically takes only 1 to 3 days. However, the entire process—including custom photovoltaic design, permitting, and final utility inspections—usually takes a few weeks to complete before the system is fully operational.

Will my solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes, your photovoltaic system will still generate power on cloudy or overcast days, though at a reduced capacity compared to direct sunlight. Solar panels use ambient light to produce energy, ensuring your home receives a steady supply of solar power year-round.

Do I need battery storage for my solar power system?

Battery storage is optional but highly recommended. While grid-tied residential solar panels provide great savings, adding a battery allows you to store excess energy generated during the day for use at night or during grid power outages.

How much maintenance do solar panels require?

Modern solar power systems require very little maintenance. Because there are no moving parts, the primary upkeep involves occasional cleaning to remove dust or debris, and routine electrical checks to ensure the inverter and panels are operating at peak efficiency.

Is my roof suitable for a residential solar installation?

Most roofs are excellent candidates for solar panels. Before any installation, a comprehensive structural and shading analysis is conducted to determine the best placement. South-facing roofs with minimal tree shading generally provide the highest solar power yields.
