Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers about our tire care and maintenance procedures.

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?
Industry standards generally recommend a tire rotation every 5,000 to 7,000 miles, or essentially every time you get an oil change. Regular rotation ensures that the tires wear evenly, which significantly extends their safe operational lifespan and maintains proper vehicle handling.
Do I need a wheel alignment when getting a tire replacement?
Yes, it is highly recommended. Installing fresh tires on a misaligned vehicle will cause the new rubber to wear unevenly and rapidly. A proper wheel alignment guarantees your new tires sit flat on the road, protecting your investment from day one.
How does your seasonal tire storage service work?
When you bring your vehicle in to swap out your seasonal sets (e.g., winter to summer), we clean, catalog, and store your off-season tires in a secure, climate-monitored facility. When the seasons change again, simply book an appointment and your tires will be ready for mounting.
What are the warning signs that I need immediate tire replacement?
Key indicators include tread depth falling below 2/32 of an inch, visible cracks or cuts in the sidewall, bulges or blisters on the tire surface, or excessive vibration in the steering wheel at highway speeds. If you notice any of these, a professional inspection is required immediately.
Can I just replace one tire instead of all four?
It depends on your vehicle's drivetrain and the wear level of the remaining tires. For All-Wheel Drive (AWD) vehicles, replacing all four is usually mandatory to prevent severe transmission damage. For 2WD vehicles, replacing them in pairs (front or rear) is the safest practice to ensure balanced traction.
