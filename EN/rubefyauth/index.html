Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have my HVAC system serviced?

It is generally recommended to have your heating and cooling system inspected and serviced at least twice a year. Ideally, you should schedule AC maintenance in the spring before the summer heat arrives, and furnace or heat pump maintenance in the fall before freezing temperatures set in.

What are the signs that I need a new air conditioner?

Key indicators that it might be time for a new AC installation include an unexplainable rise in energy bills, frequent and costly repairs, uneven cooling throughout your home, unusual noises or odors coming from the unit, and if your system is over 10-15 years old.

Is a heat pump better than a traditional furnace?

A heat pump is highly energy-efficient because it transfers heat rather than generating it. It can provide both heating and cooling from a single unit. However, in regions with extreme, prolonged freezing temperatures, a traditional gas furnace might be required to maintain optimal indoor warmth.

How can I improve my indoor air quality?

Improving indoor air quality starts with regularly changing your HVAC filters every 1-3 months. Additionally, you can consider installing whole-home humidifiers, UV light purifiers, or advanced air filtration systems integrated directly into your existing ductwork.
