Common Questions

Learn more about upgrading your home's windows.

What are the main benefits of energy efficient windows?

Energy efficient windows are designed to prevent your heated or cooled air from escaping, which reduces the workload on your HVAC system. They typically feature multiple panes of glass, inert gas fills like argon, and Low-E coatings that reflect infrared light. This keeps your home warmer in the winter and cooler in the summer.

Why should I consider vinyl windows over wood?

Premium vinyl windows offer exceptional durability with virtually no maintenance required. Unlike wood, vinyl will not rot, peel, or require repainting over time. They also provide excellent thermal insulation and are generally more cost-effective while still offering a clean, modern aesthetic for replacement windows.

What makes double hung windows so popular?

Double hung windows feature two operable sashes that slide vertically. This design allows for excellent ventilation control—you can open the top, the bottom, or both. Additionally, modern double hung windows often feature a tilt-in mechanism, allowing you to easily clean the exterior glass from the safety and comfort of inside your home.

How long does a typical window installation take?

The timeline for window installation depends on the number of windows and the complexity of the project. However, professional installation teams are highly efficient. In many cases, replacing the windows in an average-sized home can be completed in just one or two days, ensuring minimal disruption to your daily routine.

Do I need to replace all my windows at once?

Not necessarily. While replacing all windows at once ensures consistent aesthetics and maximum immediate energy savings, many homeowners choose to replace windows in phases to manage costs. You might prioritize the draftiest rooms or the front-facing windows first to immediately boost curb appeal and comfort.
