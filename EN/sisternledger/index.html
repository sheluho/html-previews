Knowledge Base

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in a whole home remodel? +

A whole home remodel typically involves structural changes, updating electrical and plumbing systems, installing new flooring, and overhauling key areas like kitchens and bathrooms. The general contractor oversees all aspects to ensure the entire house flows cohesively.

How long does a turnkey renovation take? +

The timeline for a turnkey renovation depends heavily on the scope of the project. A standard kitchen or bath may take 4-8 weeks, while a full home renovation or a complex home addition could require 3-6 months. All timelines are established during the initial planning phase.

Do I need to hire a separate architect and general contractor? +

Through our promoted turnkey service providers, you gain access to a streamlined process where design and construction are coordinated together. The general contractor typically collaborates with trusted architects and engineers to deliver a unified project without you having to manage multiple separate contracts.

Can you handle complex home additions? +

Yes. Adding square footage—whether building outward or adding a second story—involves complex foundation work, framing, and roofing integration. The contractors we promote have extensive experience ensuring the new home addition structurally and aesthetically matches your existing property.

What should I expect during the initial remodeling consultation? +

During the first consultation, the general contractor will evaluate your space, discuss your vision and budget, and outline potential structural considerations. This meeting sets the foundation for a detailed proposal and timeline for your home remodeling project.
