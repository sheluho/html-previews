Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into residential solar panel installation.

How long does residential solar installation take? +

Once designs are approved and local permits are secured, the physical installation of solar panels on a standard residential roof typically takes between 1 to 3 days. Our partners work efficiently to minimize disruption to your home life while ensuring the highest safety standards.

Are solar panels suitable for my specific roof type? +

Most residential roofs—including asphalt shingle, metal, and tile—are excellent candidates for a photovoltaic system. During your initial consultation, solar experts will evaluate your roof's age, angle, and sun exposure to design a system that maximizes energy production without compromising structural integrity.

Will solar power entirely eliminate my electricity bill? +

While transitioning to solar power drastically reduces your monthly utility costs, whether it completely eliminates your bill depends on your household energy consumption and the size of the photovoltaic system installed. Many homeowners achieve near-zero bills, factoring in basic grid connection fees.

What happens to my solar power during a grid outage? +

Standard grid-tied solar panels automatically shut down during a power outage to protect utility workers fixing the lines. However, if you opt to include a solar power battery storage system with your residential solar installation, your home will seamlessly switch to stored power, keeping your lights and essential appliances running.

Do photovoltaic panels require a lot of maintenance? +

No, modern solar panels require very little maintenance. Because there are no moving parts, they are incredibly durable. Occasional rinsing to remove dust or debris and standard periodic inspections by your solar installation company are generally all that is needed to keep them operating at peak efficiency for decades.
