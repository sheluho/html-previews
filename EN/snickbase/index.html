Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a windshield replacement take?

Typically, a standard windshield replacement takes about one hour to complete physically. Following the installation, there is usually a mandatory safe drive-away time of at least another hour to allow the advanced urethane adhesive to cure properly before the vehicle can be safely driven.

Can my windshield be repaired, or does it need replacement?

Small damage, such as chips smaller than a quarter or minor cracks under a few inches long, can often undergo successful windshield repair. However, if the damage is larger, located directly in the driver's line of sight, or reaches the edge of the glass, a full replacement is required for structural safety.

Do you handle side and rear car window replacement?

Yes, the auto glass professionals featured through our platform are equipped to handle all types of vehicle glass. This includes comprehensive car window replacement for door glass, rear windshields, quarter glass, and vent windows on almost any make and model.

Will a chip repair make the damage completely disappear?

The primary goal of a chip repair is to restore the structural integrity of the auto glass and prevent the crack from spreading further across the windshield. While the process significantly improves the optical clarity of the damaged area, a faint blemish or scar may still be slightly visible where the impact occurred.

Is it safe to drive with a cracked windshield?

It is highly discouraged to drive with a significantly cracked windshield. The windshield provides up to 30% of a vehicle's structural strength in a rollover accident and supports passenger airbag deployment. A compromised windshield can impair your vision and fail to protect you adequately in a collision.
