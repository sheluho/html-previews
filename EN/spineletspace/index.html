Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries regarding auto glass services.

How do I know if I need a windshield replacement or just a repair?

Generally, if a chip is smaller than a quarter and not directly in the driver's line of sight, a chip repair is often sufficient. However, if the crack is larger, deep, or spreading toward the edges, a full windshield replacement is necessary to maintain the structural integrity of your vehicle.

How long does a typical auto glass replacement take?

A standard windshield replacement usually takes about 60 to 90 minutes. However, it is crucial to allow the urethane adhesive to cure properly before driving. Technicians typically advise waiting an additional 1 to 2 hours after installation before operating the vehicle safely.

Is car window replacement different from windshield replacement?

Yes. Windshields are made of laminated glass designed to stay intact upon impact, which is why they can sometimes be repaired. Side and rear windows are typically made of tempered glass, which shatters into small, blunt pieces for safety. Tempered glass cannot be repaired; a complete car window replacement is required if broken.

Can I drive immediately after a chip repair?

Yes, windshield repair is a fast process that involves injecting a specialized resin into the damaged area and curing it with UV light. Once the repair is complete, the vehicle is immediately ready to be driven without any mandatory waiting period.
