Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about upgrading to residential solar power.

How long do solar panels typically last?
Most high-quality solar panels are designed to be highly durable and can generate reliable solar power for 25 to 30 years. Even after this period, they often continue to produce electricity, though at a slightly reduced efficiency.
Is my roof suitable for a solar installation?
Professional installers evaluate your roof's age, structural integrity, angle, and sun exposure. They use these factors to design the optimal residential solar layout. If your roof needs repairs, it's generally recommended to complete them before the panels are installed.
What exactly is a photovoltaic system?
A photovoltaic (PV) system is the technical term for a solar energy setup. It consists of panels that convert sunlight directly into electricity, an inverter that changes that electricity into a format your home can use, and the mounting hardware that secures the system.
Will residential solar eliminate my electric bill entirely?
While solar power significantly reduces your monthly utility costs, it rarely eliminates the bill entirely unless you have a fully off-grid system with extensive battery storage. Most homeowners remain connected to the grid to draw power at night or during heavy usage periods, usually resulting in a minimal monthly connection fee.
How long does the physical solar installation process take?
The actual physical installation of solar panels on your roof usually takes only 1 to 3 days. However, the entire process—including site assessment, custom design, permitting, and final utility inspections—can take several weeks to a few months depending on local regulations.
