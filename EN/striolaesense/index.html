Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into the whole home remodeling journey.

What defines a "whole home remodel"?

A whole home remodel typically involves renovating 50% or more of your home's square footage simultaneously. This comprehensive approach ensures a cohesive design language throughout the house, often involving structural changes, updating core systems, and completely reimagining the flow and function of the living space.

Why should I hire a general contractor instead of managing trades myself?

Managing a large-scale home renovation requires immense time, industry knowledge, and a network of reliable tradespeople. A professional general contractor handles all logistics, permitting, scheduling, and quality control. This mitigates risks, keeps the project on schedule, and provides you with a single point of accountability.

What is the benefit of a turnkey renovation?

A turnkey renovation means the design and build teams work seamlessly under one umbrella. You are guided from the initial architectural sketches to the final coat of paint without having to mediate between different companies. It streamlines communication, reduces unexpected costs, and delivers a fully finished home ready for you to enjoy.

Can a home addition be integrated into a whole home remodel?

Absolutely. In fact, doing a home addition concurrently with a whole home remodel is often the most efficient approach. It allows the architectural design team to blend the new structure flawlessly with the existing footprint, ensuring the rooflines, exterior finishes, and interior flow feel like they were originally built that way.

How do I start planning my project?

The best first step is to establish your goals and general budget. Consider how your current home is failing to meet your needs. Then, use the inquiry form on this site to connect with professionals who can evaluate your space, discuss architectural possibilities, and outline a realistic roadmap for your transformation.
