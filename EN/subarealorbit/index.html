Frequently Asked Questions

Essential knowledge for your heating and cooling systems.

How often should I schedule HVAC maintenance?
It is highly recommended to schedule HVAC maintenance twice a year. You should have your air conditioner checked in the spring before the summer heat arrives, and your furnace or heating system inspected in the fall before winter begins. This ensures optimal efficiency and prevents unexpected breakdowns.
What are the signs I need a new AC installation?
Key indicators that you may need a new air conditioner include a system that is over 10-15 years old, requires frequent and costly repairs, fails to cool your home evenly, or causes a sudden, unexplained spike in your monthly energy bills. If your unit uses R-22 Freon, replacement is also advised due to phase-outs.
Do you promote heat pump installations?
Yes. Heat pumps are highly efficient systems that provide both heating and cooling from a single unit. They are an excellent, energy-saving choice for moderate climates and are increasingly popular for homeowners looking to reduce their carbon footprint and lower utility costs.
Why is my furnace making strange noises?
Unusual sounds like rattling, banging, or squealing are clear signs that your heating system requires professional attention. Rattling can indicate loose panels or failing motors, while banging might suggest delayed ignition. Prompt heating and cooling repair is necessary to prevent further damage or safety hazards.
How long does an AC installation typically take?
A standard AC installation or replacement typically takes one full day (about 4 to 8 hours) to complete. However, if extensive ductwork modifications are required or if you are switching to a completely different type of system, the process may take longer. Professional installers aim to minimize disruption to your home.
