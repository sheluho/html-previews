Common Solar Questions

Are my roof and home suitable for solar panels?
Most homes can support residential solar installations. Our partners conduct thorough structural and shading analyses to determine the best photovoltaic system for your specific roof type, whether pitched or flat.
How long does a typical solar installation take?
The physical installation of the solar panels usually takes just 1 to 3 days. However, the entire process—including site assessment, custom design, permitting, and final utility interconnections—can take a few months depending on local municipal timelines.
Do solar panels work on cloudy days or during winter?
Yes. While direct sunlight maximizes production, modern photovoltaic technology is highly efficient and will continue to generate solar power even on overcast days or during the colder winter months.
What kind of maintenance does a solar power system require?
Residential solar systems require very little maintenance. The panels are designed to withstand harsh weather, and normal rainfall typically washes away dust. Periodic visual inspections and checking your monitoring app are usually all that is needed.
Can I store the energy my solar panels produce?
Absolutely. We highly recommend pairing your solar installation with a home battery backup system. This allows you to store excess energy generated during peak daylight hours to use at night or as backup power during utility grid outages.
