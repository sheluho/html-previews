Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our auto glass services.

How long does a windshield replacement take?

A standard windshield replacement typically takes about 60 to 90 minutes to complete. However, we recommend waiting an additional hour before driving the vehicle to allow the urethane adhesive to properly cure and ensure your safety.

Can you perform a chip repair, or do I need a full replacement?

In many cases, if the damage is a small chip (smaller than a quarter) and is not in the driver's direct line of sight, a windshield repair is possible. If the crack is larger, spreading, or near the edge of the glass, a full windshield replacement is usually required for safety reasons.

Is it safe to drive immediately after a car window replacement?

For side and rear car window replacements, you can usually drive immediately after the service is complete, as they do not rely on the same curing adhesives as a windshield. However, we advise against rolling down newly installed side windows for at least 24 hours.

Do you handle mobile auto glass repair?

The original provider we promote specializes in convenient service options, which frequently includes mobile dispatch to your home or office in the San Francisco area, allowing you to get your auto glass fixed without disrupting your day.

What happens to the old auto glass?

The technicians will carefully remove and dispose of your damaged auto glass. All broken glass from your car window replacement or windshield replacement is cleaned out of your vehicle and properly recycled or disposed of according to local environmental guidelines.
