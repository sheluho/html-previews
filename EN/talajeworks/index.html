Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule a tire rotation?

It is generally recommended to have a tire rotation performed every 5,000 to 8,000 miles. Regular rotation promotes even tread wear, extending the lifespan of your tires and improving overall vehicle handling.

What is included in your tire installation service?

A professional tire installation includes removing the old tires, mounting the new tires onto your wheels, balancing the wheel assemblies to prevent vibration, and securing them to your vehicle with proper torque specifications.

Do you offer seasonal tire storage?

Yes, we facilitate seasonal tire storage. Storing your off-season tires in a controlled environment protects the rubber compounds from extreme temperature fluctuations and UV damage, keeping them in prime condition for their designated season.

How do I know if I need a wheel alignment?

Signs that you may need a wheel alignment include your vehicle pulling to one side while driving straight, an off-center steering wheel, or rapid, uneven tread wear on your tires. If you notice any of these issues, it's time for an inspection.

When is it time for a complete tire replacement?

Tire replacement is necessary when the tread depth reaches 2/32 of an inch, which is the legal safety limit. You should also consider replacement if you notice visible damage such as deep cuts, bulges, or severe cracking in the sidewall.
