Heating & Cooling Inquiries

Common questions regarding your HVAC system's health and maintenance.

How often should I schedule HVAC maintenance?

We recommend professional maintenance at least twice a year. Your air conditioner should be serviced in the spring before the summer heat arrives, and your furnace should be inspected in the fall before temperatures drop. Regular check-ups prolong system lifespan and improve energy efficiency.

What are the signs that I need a new air conditioner?

Key indicators include an AC unit older than 10-15 years, frequent breakdown and repair cycles, rising energy bills despite normal usage, and inconsistent cooling throughout your home. If you notice unusual noises or poor airflow, it may be time to consider an AC installation upgrade.

How does a heat pump differ from a traditional furnace?

A traditional furnace burns fuel (like gas or oil) or uses electrical resistance to generate heat. A heat pump, on the other hand, moves existing heat from the outside air into your home during winter, and reverses the process to act as an air conditioner during summer. Heat pumps are highly energy-efficient in moderate climates.

Why are some rooms in my house hotter or colder than others?

Uneven heating and cooling can stem from multiple issues, including poorly balanced air ducts, inadequate insulation, a failing blower motor, or an HVAC unit that is incorrectly sized for your home's square footage. A professional assessment can pinpoint the exact cause.

Can a new HVAC system really lower my energy bills?

Yes. Modern heating and cooling systems are engineered with significantly higher SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) ratings than units produced just a decade ago. Upgrading to a highly efficient air conditioner or furnace means the system requires less energy to maintain your desired temperature, often resulting in noticeable monthly savings.
