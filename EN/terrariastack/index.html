Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common auto glass concerns.

How long does a full windshield replacement take?

A standard windshield replacement typically takes about 1 to 2 hours. However, the urethane adhesive requires additional time to cure before the vehicle is safe to drive, usually an extra hour.

Can you perform a chip repair on any size crack?

No. Chip repair is highly effective, but it is generally limited to damage smaller than a quarter and cracks shorter than three inches. If the damage exceeds these limits, a full replacement is required for safety.

Do I need to replace damaged auto glass immediately?

Yes. Even minor damage can quickly spread due to temperature fluctuations or driving over bumps. Furthermore, the windshield provides significant structural support to the vehicle's roof, making prompt repair or replacement vital.

Is car window replacement covered by my insurance?

Most comprehensive auto insurance policies cover auto glass and windshield replacement. Many policies even waive the deductible for a chip repair. We recommend checking with your specific provider to confirm your coverage details.

How soon can I wash my car after auto glass service?

You should wait at least 24 to 48 hours before using a high-pressure car wash after a windshield replacement. The high pressure can damage the new seal before it is fully cured. Normal rain is completely fine.
