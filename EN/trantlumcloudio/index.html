Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about upgrading your home's exterior and thermal barriers.

What are the main benefits of upgrading attic insulation?

Upgrading your attic insulation helps prevent heated air from escaping during the winter and blocks radiant heat from entering during the summer. This significantly reduces the workload on your HVAC system, leading to lower energy bills, more consistent indoor temperatures, and a reduced carbon footprint.

How do I know if it's time for new house siding?

Signs that you may need new house siding include visible cracks, warping, rot, or severe fading. Additionally, if you notice peeling paint or wallpaper inside your home, it could indicate that moisture is penetrating your exterior siding. High heating and cooling bills can also be a symptom of failing exterior cladding.

What makes spray foam different from traditional fiberglass insulation?

Unlike traditional fiberglass batts that simply sit in wall cavities, spray foam expands upon application to fill gaps, cracks, and hard-to-reach areas. This creates a superior air seal that not only provides excellent thermal resistance (R-value) but also prevents drafts and moisture infiltration much more effectively.

Is fiber cement siding better than vinyl siding?

Both offer distinct advantages. Vinyl siding is highly cost-effective, lightweight, and requires almost no maintenance. Fiber cement siding is thicker, incredibly durable, fire-resistant, and can closely mimic the texture of natural wood. The best choice depends on your budget, aesthetic preferences, and local climate.

Will upgrading my home insulation and siding improve property value?

Yes. Exterior renovations like new house siding are consistently ranked among the top home improvement projects for return on investment. Furthermore, modern home insulation is highly attractive to prospective buyers who are increasingly conscious of energy costs and environmental impact.
