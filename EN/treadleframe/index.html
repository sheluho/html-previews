Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about residential solar power.

How long does a typical residential solar installation take?

The physical installation of solar panels on your roof typically takes between one to three days, depending on the system's size and your roof's complexity. However, the entire process, including permitting and inspections, can take several weeks.

Do solar panels still generate power on cloudy days?

Yes. While direct sunlight produces the highest energy output, modern photovoltaic systems are highly efficient and will continue to generate electricity even when the weather is overcast or rainy.

What maintenance does a residential solar power system require?

Residential solar panels are remarkably low maintenance. They generally require only occasional cleaning to remove dust or debris, and periodic visual inspections to ensure the mounting hardware and wiring remain secure.

Is home battery energy storage necessary?

While not strictly mandatory, adding battery storage is highly recommended. It allows you to store excess solar power generated during the day to use at night or during power grid outages, maximizing your energy independence.

Will residential solar panels affect my roof's integrity?

When installed by top-tier professionals, solar installation actually protects the portion of the roof it covers from weather and UV damage. Installers perform thorough structural assessments prior to mounting to ensure your roof can safely support the system.
