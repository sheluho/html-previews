Frequently Asked Questions

Insights into the renovation process.

What exactly is a turnkey renovation?

A turnkey renovation means the general contractor handles every single aspect of the project. From the initial architectural design and local permitting to the physical construction and final cleaning, you simply "turn the key" and walk into a fully finished, ready-to-live-in space without having to manage multiple separate vendors yourself.

How long does whole home remodeling typically take?

Timelines vary greatly based on the scope of work. A comprehensive interior home remodeling project can take anywhere from 4 to 8 months. However, projects that involve major structural changes, foundation work, or a large-scale home addition may extend beyond a year due to permitting and construction complexities.

Do you handle the architectural design for a home addition?

Yes, the design-build process highlighted on our platform integrates architectural planning directly with the construction phase. This unified approach ensures that the design for your home addition is not only aesthetically beautiful but also structurally sound, code-compliant, and strictly aligned with your established budget.

What makes a general contractor different from specialized trades?

A general contractor oversees the entire home renovation ecosystem. Instead of you acting as the manager, the general contractor hires, schedules, and manages all specialized trades (such as plumbers, electricians, and carpenters), secures necessary permits, and maintains the overarching project schedule, acting as your single point of responsibility.

Should we move out during a whole home remodel?

For extensive whole home remodeling—especially when involving the primary kitchen and multiple bathrooms simultaneously—moving out is highly recommended. Vacating the property accelerates the construction timeline, provides workers with unimpeded access, and importantly, keeps your family safely away from construction dust, debris, and noise.
