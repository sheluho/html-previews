Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about auto glass repair and replacement.

How long does a windshield replacement take?

A standard windshield replacement generally takes about 60 to 90 minutes to complete. However, the vehicle must sit for an additional period (usually an hour) to allow the urethane adhesive to cure properly before it is safe to drive.

Can my damaged windshield be repaired, or does it need replacement?

If the chip or crack is smaller than a quarter and is not located directly in the driver's line of sight, a simple chip repair is often possible. Larger cracks, deep spiderwebbing, or damage near the edges typically require a full windshield replacement to maintain structural integrity.

Do you handle car window replacement for side and rear glass?

Yes. Unlike windshields, side and rear windows are usually made of tempered glass that shatters completely upon impact. We provide full car window replacement services, including cleaning out the broken glass from your door panels and interior.

Is mobile service available for auto glass repair?

Yes, the service professionals we promote offer convenient mobile auto glass repair and replacement. Technicians can come directly to your home or workplace, saving you the hassle of driving a vehicle with compromised visibility.

Will my auto insurance cover the windshield replacement?

Many comprehensive auto insurance policies cover windshield repair and replacement, often waiving the deductible for simple chip repairs. The service provider will help you verify your coverage and can typically assist with filing the necessary paperwork.
