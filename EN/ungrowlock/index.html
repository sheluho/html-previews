Frequently Asked Questions

Get straightforward answers about your upcoming kitchen transformation.

What is included in a turnkey kitchen remodeling project?

A turnkey kitchen remodeling project handles every aspect of the renovation from start to finish. This includes the initial design and 3D rendering, demolition of the old space, plumbing and electrical updates, installation of custom kitchen features, cabinets, countertops, flooring, and the final aesthetic touches. You simply turn the key and start enjoying your new space.

How long does it take to install a custom kitchen?

The timeline for a custom kitchen varies depending on the complexity of the design and the materials selected. On average, after the design phase is completed and materials like custom cabinets are ordered, the active construction phase typically takes between 4 to 8 weeks. Your dedicated project manager will provide a detailed schedule before work begins.

Can I choose my own materials for cabinets and countertops?

Absolutely. Material selection is a core part of building your custom kitchen. You will have a wide variety of options for cabinets—including different woods, finishes, and hardware—as well as an extensive selection of countertops, such as granite, quartz, marble, and butcher block. The design team works closely with you to match materials to your lifestyle and budget.

Why should I hire a full-service kitchen renovation company?

Hiring a full-service kitchen renovation company eliminates the stress of acting as your own general contractor. Instead of coordinating plumbers, electricians, and carpenters separately, a full-service firm manages the entire crew. This ensures cohesive communication, stricter adherence to timelines, and a much higher standard of quality control throughout the kitchen remodeling process.

Do you provide 3D renderings before construction begins?

Yes. Visualizing the final result is crucial when investing in a custom kitchen. The design partners we promote utilize advanced 3D rendering software to show you exactly how your new cabinets, countertops, and overall layout will look. This allows you to make adjustments and approve the design with confidence before any demolition starts.
