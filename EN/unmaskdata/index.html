Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about planning your next residential move.

How far in advance should I book a moving company?

It is generally recommended to book your movers at least 4 to 6 weeks in advance, especially during peak moving seasons like the summer months. For long distance movers, providing even more notice ensures availability and allows for better logistical planning.

Do you offer packing services for fragile or high-value items?

Yes, comprehensive packing services are available through our recommended partners. Professional teams use specialized materials, custom crates, and proven techniques to secure fragile items, electronics, and artwork, significantly minimizing the risk of damage during transit.

What is the exact difference between local movers and long distance movers?

Local movers typically charge by the hour and operate within a specific metropolitan area or a defined state radius (usually under 100 miles). Long distance movers usually charge based on the total weight of your belongings and the exact distance traveled, and they require specialized federal licensing for interstate transport.

Are my household belongings insured during the move?

Standard basic valuation coverage (usually a set amount per pound) is typically included by any reputable moving company by law. However, for full replacement value of expensive items, it is highly recommended to discuss comprehensive insurance or full-value protection options with the provider before the move begins.

How can I best prepare my home before the movers arrive?

Clear all major walkways, disconnect major appliances (like washers and refrigerators), and ensure boxes are clearly labeled by room if you are packing yourself. If you are utilizing full packing services, simply organize your personal essentials, secure valuable documents, and let the professionals handle the rest of the house.
