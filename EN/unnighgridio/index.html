Premium Care Services

Comprehensive solutions for ultimate paint protection and interior perfection.

Advanced Ceramic Coating

Shield your vehicle's clear coat from harsh UV rays, chemical stains, and environmental contaminants. Ceramic coating creates a hydrophobic layer that repels water and dirt, making maintenance washes incredibly easy while locking in a deep, mirror-like gloss.

Paint Correction

Over time, improper washing leaves microscopic scratches and swirl marks that dull your car's finish. Professional paint correction safely levels the clear coat, permanently removing defects and restoring the true clarity and depth of your vehicle's original color.

Complete Interior Detailing

Exterior Auto Detailing

Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about our auto detailing processes and protective treatments.

How long does a ceramic coating last?

With proper maintenance, a professional ceramic coating can last anywhere from 2 to 5 years, depending on the specific grade applied and your driving habits. It provides long-lasting paint protection that significantly outperforms traditional waxes and sealants.

What is included in an interior detailing service?

Our comprehensive interior detailing includes deep vacuuming, steam cleaning of carpets and upholstery, leather cleaning and conditioning, and thorough sanitization of all hard surfaces, dashboard, and crevices to restore a factory-fresh feel.

Is paint correction necessary before ceramic coating?

Yes, it is highly recommended. Ceramic coating locks in the current state of your paint. Paint correction removes scratches, swirl marks, and oxidation beforehand, ensuring that the coating seals in a flawless, high-gloss finish.

How often should I get my car detailed?

For optimal maintenance, we suggest a full auto detailing service every 4 to 6 months. However, if your vehicle is ceramic coated, regular maintenance washes are usually sufficient, with a minor detail needed only once a year to keep the interior pristine.

Can ceramic coating prevent rock chips?

No. While ceramic coating provides excellent chemical resistance, UV protection, and hydrophobicity, it is not thick enough to absorb the physical impact of a flying rock. For rock chip prevention, a Paint Protection Film (PPF) is the recommended solution.
