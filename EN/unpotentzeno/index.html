Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about working with a wedding planner.

What is included in full-service wedding planning?

Our full-service wedding planning covers everything from the moment you get engaged until the final guest leaves the reception. This includes budgeting, venue selection, vendor hiring, contract negotiation, overall event design, and complete management of the timeline.

Do I really need a day of coordinator?

Absolutely. A day of coordinator allows you, your family, and your friends to actually enjoy the celebration without worrying about logistics, late vendors, or setting up décor. We take over the execution so you can be fully present.

Can you help plan a destination wedding?

Yes! Planning a destination wedding comes with unique challenges, such as navigating different time zones and sourcing reliable local vendors. We manage all travel logistics and on-site coordination to ensure your getaway wedding is picture-perfect.

When should I hire a wedding planner?

Ideally, you should hire a wedding planner 12 to 18 months in advance of your desired date. However, if you have a shorter timeline or only need a wedding coordinator for the final weeks, we offer flexible packages to step in whenever you need us.

What is the difference between a venue manager and a wedding coordinator?

A venue manager works exclusively for the venue to ensure their facilities are operating correctly. Your wedding coordinator works exclusively for you. We manage your personal timeline, direct your specific vendors (like photographers and florists), and ensure your personal vision is executed.
