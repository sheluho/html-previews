Frequently Asked Questions

What is the process for designing custom closets?
The typical process begins with a consultation to assess your space and discuss your organizational needs. Designers then create a 3D model of your proposed walk-in closet or wardrobe. Once approved, the system is custom-manufactured and professionally installed.
How long does a typical walk-in closet installation take?
Most standard walk-in closet installations can be completed in one to two days. However, the exact timeline depends on the size of the room and the complexity of the custom cabinetry and accessories chosen.
Can you design a closet organizer for an unusually shaped room?
Absolutely. The primary benefit of custom closets is their adaptability. Systems can be designed to work around sloped ceilings, awkward corners, windows, and non-standard dimensions that off-the-shelf products cannot accommodate.
What materials are used for the wardrobes and shelving?
Premium custom closets utilize high-quality, durable materials including furniture-grade melamine, thermally fused laminates, and solid wood veneers. These materials are chosen for their longevity, resistance to warping, and easy maintenance.
Do I need to empty my current closet before the consultation?
It is actually helpful to leave your belongings in the space for the initial consultation. This allows the designer to see exactly what you need to store—whether it's long dresses, extensive shoe collections, or bulky sweaters—to create the perfect reach-in closet or walk-in solution.
