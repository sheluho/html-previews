Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between traditional wax and a ceramic coating?

Traditional wax sits on top of the paint and provides temporary protection, usually lasting a few months before it washes away or degrades from sun exposure. Ceramic coating is a liquid polymer that chemically bonds to the factory paint, creating a semi-permanent layer of protection that lasts for years, offering superior resistance to UV rays, chemicals, and minor scratches while providing incredible gloss.

How often should I schedule a full auto detailing service?

For daily driven vehicles, we recommend a comprehensive auto detailing session two to three times a year to maintain the condition of both the interior and exterior. However, routine maintenance washes should be performed every two weeks to prevent dirt buildup from etching into the paint protection layer.

Does interior detailing include the removal of deep stains and odors?

Yes, professional interior detailing involves deep cleaning techniques such as hot water extraction for carpets and specialized chemical treatments for upholstery. This process effectively lifts deep-seated stains and neutralizes persistent odors rather than simply masking them, returning the cabin to a hygienic and fresh state.

Is paint correction necessary before applying a ceramic coating?

Absolutely. Because ceramic coating forms a hard, transparent shell over your vehicle's surface, it will lock in any existing imperfections like swirl marks, holograms, or light scratches. Paint correction via machine polishing is essential to ensure the surface is entirely flawless before the coating is applied, guaranteeing a perfect, mirror-like finish.

Can I take my ceramic-coated car through an automatic car wash?

It is highly discouraged. Automatic car washes that use abrasive brushes can introduce micro-scratches and gradually degrade the ceramic coating. To preserve the integrity and longevity of the paint protection, hand washing using the two-bucket method with pH-neutral soap is the recommended approach for maintenance.
