Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is a ceramic coating?

A ceramic coating is a liquid polymer applied by hand to the exterior of a vehicle. It chemically bonds with the vehicle's factory paint, creating a layer of protection that won't wash away or break down quickly like traditional wax. It provides outstanding paint protection against UV damage, chemical stains, and makes the car much easier to clean due to its hydrophobic properties.

How often should I schedule full auto detailing?

For optimal maintenance, we recommend a comprehensive auto detailing session 1 to 2 times a year. This ensures that environmental fallout, minor scratches, and interior wear are addressed promptly. Regular maintenance washes in between these major details will keep your vehicle looking its absolute best year-round.

Does interior detailing remove tough stains and odors?

Yes. Deep interior detailing involves specialized extraction equipment and professional-grade cleaning agents designed to lift stubborn stains from fabrics and carpets. Additionally, thorough steam cleaning and ozone treatments can effectively neutralize and eliminate deep-seated odors, rather than just masking them.

What is the difference between traditional wax and ceramic coating?

Traditional carnauba wax sits on top of the paint and provides temporary gloss and protection, usually lasting a few months before it degrades. Ceramic coating forms a semi-permanent chemical bond with the clear coat, offering vastly superior durability, extreme heat resistance, and a much higher level of gloss that can last for several years with proper maintenance.

Is paint protection recommended for brand new cars?

Absolutely. The best time to apply a ceramic coating or advanced paint protection is when the vehicle is brand new. Even fresh from the dealership, cars often have minor paint imperfections. Applying protection early locks in the flawless finish, prevents premature fading, and makes the vehicle much easier to maintain from day one.
