Frequently Asked Questions

Expert answers to common auto detailing inquiries.

How long does a ceramic coating last? ▼

The longevity of a ceramic coating depends on the specific grade applied and how well the vehicle is maintained. Professional-grade coatings can last anywhere from two to five years, and sometimes longer. Regular maintenance washes and avoiding abrasive chemicals will help preserve the coating's hydrophobic properties and gloss over time.

What is the difference between wax and ceramic coating? ▼

Traditional carnauba waxes sit on top of the paint and typically degrade within a few weeks to a few months, depending on environmental exposure. A ceramic coating, on the other hand, chemically bonds with the factory clear coat, creating a semi-permanent layer of protection that is significantly harder, more resistant to chemicals, and lasts for years instead of weeks.

Does Paint Protection Film (PPF) alter the look of my car? ▼

High-quality paint protection film is virtually invisible once applied correctly. It does not alter the color or clarity of your factory paint. In fact, modern PPF is highly glossy and seamlessly integrates with your vehicle's exterior, ensuring protection from rock chips while maintaining a flawless aesthetic appearance.

How often should I get full car detailing? ▼

For most daily driven vehicles, a comprehensive auto detailing session is recommended two to three times a year. This helps reset the vehicle's condition, removing embedded contaminants and applying fresh protection. Between these major details, regular maintenance washes are suggested to keep the vehicle looking its best.

What does interior detailing include? ▼

Premium interior detailing goes far beyond a standard vacuum. It includes deep extraction of carpets and upholstery, steam cleaning of hard-to-reach crevices, leather cleaning and conditioning, and dressing of all plastics and vinyls with UV protectants. The goal is to restore the cabin to a like-new condition and eliminate any lingering odors or stubborn stains.
