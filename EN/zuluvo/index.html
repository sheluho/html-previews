Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries regarding menu planning and event execution.

What is included in full-service wedding catering?
Full-service wedding catering typically encompasses much more than just the food. It generally includes personalized menu creation, professional waitstaff and bartenders, elegant table settings (such as china, flatware, and linens), setup, and complete cleanup after the event. The goal is to provide a seamless culinary experience so you can focus entirely on your celebration.
How do we decide between a plated dinner and buffet catering?
The choice depends on the atmosphere you wish to create. A plated dinner offers a highly formal, synchronized experience where guests are served simultaneously, ideal for elegant galas or formal weddings. Buffet catering encourages mingling, offers guests a wider variety of menu choices, and tends to create a more relaxed, communal dining environment. Both styles are executed with the highest standards of presentation.
How far in advance should we book corporate catering?
For large-scale corporate catering events, such as annual summits or holiday parties, it is recommended to secure your date at least three to six months in advance. For smaller executive luncheons or routine meetings, reaching out two to four weeks prior is usually sufficient. Early planning ensures the best selection of seasonal ingredients and staff availability.
Can dietary restrictions be accommodated for event catering?
Absolutely. Top-tier culinary teams are highly experienced in designing inclusive menus. Whether your guests require gluten-free, vegan, vegetarian, or allergy-specific dishes, the chefs will craft gourmet alternatives that ensure every attendee enjoys a delicious and safe dining experience without compromising on flavor or presentation.
Are tasting sessions available before finalizing the menu?
Yes, tasting sessions are highly recommended, particularly for comprehensive wedding catering packages. A tasting allows you to sample proposed dishes, experience the chef's presentation style, and make necessary adjustments to flavor profiles. It is a vital step in ensuring the final menu perfectly aligns with your vision for the event.
