Frequently Asked Questions

How long does residential solar installation take?

The actual physical installation of solar panels usually takes between 1 to 3 days depending on the size of your system and roof complexity. However, the entire process—including consultation, system design, and obtaining necessary permits—can take several weeks.

Will solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. While photovoltaic panels are most efficient in direct sunlight, they still generate solar power on cloudy or overcast days by capturing diffuse sunlight. System production is estimated based on the average weather patterns in your specific region.

What is a solar battery storage system?

A battery storage system captures and stores the excess solar power generated by your panels during peak sunlight hours. You can then use this stored energy at night or during grid power outages, maximizing your energy independence.

Do I need to replace my roof before getting solar panels?

It depends on the current condition of your roof. Solar panels are built to last 25 years or more. If your roof is nearing the end of its lifespan, it is highly recommended to replace it prior to the solar installation to avoid the cost of removing and reinstalling the panels later.

How much can I save with residential solar?

Savings vary based on your average energy consumption, local utility rates, and the size of the residential solar system installed. Many homeowners see a significant reduction in their monthly electric bills and achieve a return on their investment within a few years.
